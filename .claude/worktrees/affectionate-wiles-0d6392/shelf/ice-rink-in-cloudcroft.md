# Ice Rink in Cloudcroft

## Overview

The James Sewell Ice Rink is one of Cloudcroft’s most distinctive winter attractions. It is unusual enough to matter. For years it was known as the southernmost natural ice rink in the Southwest, and it remains one of the clearest reasons families visit Cloudcroft in winter.

If you are planning a cold-weather trip, this is one of the first things worth checking.

## Why this matters

Cloudcroft’s winter identity depends on a few key experiences: snow, Ski Cloudcroft, cabins, and the ice rink. The rink gives non-skiers and mixed groups something easy and memorable to do.

It is also one of the best “even if you do not skate” stops in town because it helps define the winter feel of the village.

## What to know

Current official and public-facing sources support:
- the rink is commonly called the **James Sewell Ice Rink**
- it is associated with Zenith Park
- current public-facing social pages still promote skate rentals, sled sales, snacks, and open sessions
- the Village of Cloudcroft still highlights the rink as part of the winter visitor experience

A major caution: Cloudcroft Reader reported in November 2025 that the 2025–2026 season would be the rink’s final season as a natural outdoor rink. That does not mean there is no rink activity now, but it does mean visitors should verify what format, schedule, or operating model is current before relying on older descriptions.

## What it’s actually like

When it is operating, the rink is one of the easiest family outings in town:
- skate a little
- watch a little
- warm up
- enjoy the winter scene

It works especially well for families, couples, and groups where not everyone wants to ski. It is more approachable than a full ski outing and easier to fit into a short trip.

## Best for

- winter visitors
- families
- non-skiers
- couples wanting a simple seasonal activity
- mixed-age groups

## Potential drawbacks or cautions

- Seasonality is the biggest issue.
- Current operations should be checked directly because of the 2025 reporting about the end of the natural-rink era.
- Weather can affect ice quality and schedule.
- If you are visiting outside winter, this is not part of the trip.
