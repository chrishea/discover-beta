## 1. Executive Summary

Mountain Monthly and DiscoverCloudcroft.com are two adjacent properties with complementary missions: Mountain Monthly is the community journalism voice of the Sacramento Mountains; DiscoverCloudcroft.com is the trip-planning resource for Cloudcroft visitors. They launch and scale together. The strategic insight that drives this entire plan, and the one v2 added and v1 missed, is that **we are not launching into a void**: we start with 5,000+ Mountain Monthly Facebook followers, 15,000+ Cloudcroft Reader Facebook followers, and 3,200 Cloudcroft Reader email subscribers — 20,000+ combined social reach and a high-trust email asset that most new publications spend 12–18 months building (v2). The job of marketing is to convert that existing trust into Discover's owned audience, Mountain Monthly's paid subscriber base, and durable advertising revenue, without exhausting reader goodwill. Success is measured against $18,000–32,000 combined Y1 marketing-driven revenue (both), a ranked Discover site in Google, Mountain Monthly admitted to Google News, and a clean-running monthly operating cadence across four channels by Day 90.

## 3. Target Audiences & Personas

Three primary audiences across the two properties:

**Visitor / trip-planner (Discover primary).** Searching before booking. Coming from El Paso (90 miles south), Alamogordo (16 miles downhill), Las Cruces, Albuquerque, Midland/Odessa, Lubbock, Roswell. Intent varies by season: summer = escape the heat (v2), fall = aspen foliage, winter = southernmost ski area in the US (v2), spring = wildflowers and hiking. Keyword strategy and page ownership detailed in `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md` §4.

**Resident / community member (Mountain Monthly primary).** Year-round residents of Cloudcroft and the Sacramento Mountains, plus second-home owners, former residents, and regional community members who still track Cloudcroft from a distance — v2 notes the Reader's 15,000 followers is roughly 15× the year-round resident population, which means the existing audience already skews toward this tourism-adjacent second-home / diaspora demographic. That is the crossover that makes cross-promotion work.

**Tourism-adjacent business audience.** Lodge at Cloudcroft, Ski Cloudcroft, Chamber of Commerce, restaurants, cabin operators, Burro Avenue merchants. Not end readers; they are partners and advertisers. The pitch to them is now "established media partner with 20K+ reach" rather than "new tourism site seeking validation" (v2) — this is the framing change that unlocks partnership economics throughout §9.

## 4. Brand Positioning & Messaging

Two properties, two lanes, one reinforcing loop.

**Mountain Monthly.** Community journalism voice for the Sacramento Mountains. Primary reporting, named sources, hyperlocal specificity. Editorial voice is personal and accountable — "I'm Chris" works in advertising copy because it is the voice on the masthead (v1/v2). Proof points: breaking news first, council coverage with vote counts, named officials, primary documents, corrections policy visible (v1 EEAT framework).

**DiscoverCloudcroft.com.** Warm, knowledgeable guide to visiting Cloudcroft. Ground-level specificity that AI cannot fabricate: named trails, named streets, named coffee shops, which parking lot fills first (carried from SEO plan §8). First-person where appropriate, author bylines with local bio (SEO plan §5.4). Stock imagery is banned on editorial pages.

**The one-line promise for each:**
- Mountain Monthly: *Community journalism at 9,000 feet.*
- DiscoverCloudcroft.com: *Everything you need to plan a Cloudcroft trip — written locally, updated constantly.*

**Voice discipline across channels.** v2's launch-email guidance applies everywhere: lead with content value, not the brand. Never "our new website" — always "Cloudcroft's complete visitor guide." The site is introduced as the resource, not the product.

**Cross-promotion discipline (v2, load-bearing):** these audiences belong to the Cloudcroft Reader and Mountain Monthly, not to DiscoverCloudcroft.com. Every cross-promotional post should feel organic and editorially motivated — a genuine recommendation, not a forced advertisement. Cross-promotion cadence ceilings, by channel, are enforced in §5 and §10.

The channels reinforce each other (v1): awareness (social + partnerships) → engagement (email) → monetization (advertising + subs) → advocacy (readers become ambassadors). Every channel below is sized to that loop, not to vanity metrics.

### 5.1 Facebook

Three pages, three operating modes. **Do not merge them.**

- **Mountain Monthly (5,000 followers) — primary community news channel (both).** 1–2 posts/day (both). 4-1-1 content mix (both). Breaking-news protocol: post first, post fast — a well-timed breaking-news post can reach 10,000+ through shares from the 5,000 base (v2). Native video 3×/week; Facebook suppresses link posts, a 30-second "Today in Cloudcroft" phone video outperforms a link post 4–6× (both). Facebook Live for council meetings (check NM open meeting law for recording rights) — Live videos receive 6× more engagement than pre-recorded (both). Editor replies to every comment within 4 hours during business days (v1 specifies business days; v2 says 4 hours generally). **DC cross-promotion: 1–2x per week** when content genuinely warrants — natural, not forced (v2).
- **Cloudcroft Reader (15,000 followers) — strategic cross-promotion partner for DC (v2).** **Maximum 2 cross-promotion posts per week**; more than that risks feeling like advertising rather than editorial curation (v2). Cross-posts must be framed as community resource, never as "our new site." After launch, settle into 1 cross-promotion per week (v2).
- **DiscoverCloudcroft (new, launching at 0).** Target 1,000 followers in first 60 days via launch cross-promotion (v2), 3,000 by end of Y1. Visitor-facing at all times: destination photography, event promotion, visitor tips, real-time conditions. Create a Facebook Event for every major Cloudcroft event (Apple Festival, Christmas in the Clouds, Mayfair) — events surface independently in search and generate organic reach (both).

**Facebook Groups strategy (v1, preserved).** Join and participate in existing Cloudcroft-area Facebook groups — as a journalist who adds value, not as a spammer. Share relevant Mountain Monthly stories where they are genuinely useful. Groups drive significant referral traffic to local news sites.

### 5.2 Instagram

**Discover is the flagship (both).** @DiscoverCloudcroft. Username consistency across all platforms matters for search (v2). 5 feed posts/week (3 static, 1 Reel, 1 carousel), daily Stories (both). Minimum 2 Reels/week — Reels receive 3–5× organic reach of static posts (both). Photography floor: every feed post excellent; a blurry image suppresses the whole account's performance for days (v2). Budget for professional photography — "the single most important production investment for DiscoverCloudcroft.com" (v2).

**Hashtag mix (v2):** broad (#NewMexico #NMTrue #VisitNM), mid (#SacramentoMountains #LincolnNationalForest), specific (#DiscoverCloudcroft #CloudcroftNM). 15–20 per post, rotated.

**@newmexicotrue collaboration (both).** 700K+ followers. Tag in every relevant post. A single reshare can generate thousands of new followers. Build the relationship proactively — engage their content, then pitch a formal collaboration.

**Link-in-bio rotation (v1).** Change weekly to the most relevant seasonal page. Use Linktree or equivalent to maintain multiple destinations simultaneously.

**Seasonal campaigns (both):** #CloudcroftFall / #CloudcroftWinter / #CloudcroftSpring / #CloudcroftSummer — one hashtag campaign per season.

**Mountain Monthly Instagram is secondary (both).** 3–4 posts/week, behind-the-scenes journalism, community portraits, historical photos from the Sacramento Mountains Museum archive.

### 5.3 YouTube & short-form video

**One shared channel, two playlists (both): "Mountain Monthly | Discover Cloudcroft NM."** Build one strong channel rather than two weak ones (v1).

- Playlist 1 — Community News (MM): council highlights, community event coverage, news features. Editorial voice.
- Playlist 2 — Discover Cloudcroft: trail guides, seasonal destination videos, local business features, driving tours.

**Cadence: 1 video/week minimum (v1). Don't overcommit.**

**Launch video (v2).** 3–4 minutes, all four seasons, original or licensed ambient music. Title: *"Cloudcroft, New Mexico: America's Best-Kept Mountain Secret."* Promote simultaneously across Reader FB, MM FB, and DC Instagram. Target 500 views in Week 1.

**YouTube Shorts (both).** Repurpose every DC Instagram Reel as a Short — same vertical video, zero additional production, different distribution. Shorts get significant organic distribution and convert to channel subs.

**YouTube SEO (both):** title format `[Keyword]: [Compelling Detail]` — e.g., *"Hiking Cloudcroft NM: Osha Trail Complete Guide (Difficulty, Views, and Current Conditions)."* First 150 characters of description are what appears in search — put the most important info there.

**End cards and chapters (v1, preserved).** Every video ends with a card driving viewers to the MM newsletter or DiscoverCloudcroft.com. Use timestamp chapters on longer content — they improve watch time and create search-indexable chapter titles.

### 5.4 Pinterest

**Discover only; Mountain Monthly is not a Pinterest play.** Pinterest users are active trip planners; a well-optimized pin can drive DC traffic for 2–3 years (both). No audience to seed — pure organic, content-driven growth.

- **Boards (v2):** Cloudcroft NM Things to Do · Cloudcroft Hiking Trails · Cloudcroft Cabins & Lodging · Cloudcroft Restaurants · Fall in Cloudcroft · Winter / Ski Cloudcroft · New Mexico Road Trips · White Sands + Cloudcroft Itinerary.
- **Cadence:** 5–10 pins/day via Tailwind (~$20/mo) (both).
- **Design:** 2:3 ratio (1000 × 1500 px), text overlay on every pin with content title. Canva template for consistency (both).
- **Rich Pins (Article type) enabled** so pins auto-pull title, description, author from DC metadata (both).

### 5.5 Platform priority principle (v1, preserved)

Do fewer platforms exceptionally well rather than all platforms poorly. Launch-phase sequencing: MM Facebook + Discover Instagram are the two must-haves from Day 1. YouTube, Pinterest, and the Discover Facebook page build in the 90 days after launch. Mountain Monthly Instagram is a slow grow.

## 8. Paid Media Plan

Starting assets change the paid-advertising math (v2). With 20,000+ combined FB followers and 3,200 email subscribers, we have the seed audiences required for Meta's Lookalike Audience tool — one of the most powerful targeting capabilities in digital advertising.

### 8.1 Lookalike audiences (v2, foundational)

Upload the 3,200-subscriber Reader email list to Meta Ads Manager as a Custom Audience. Create a 1% Lookalike — Meta will find approximately 100,000–200,000 people in target geography who match the demographic and behavioral profile of our most engaged readers. Do the same with the MM Facebook page audience. These Lookalike audiences are our most cost-effective paid targeting option.

### 8.2 Four Meta campaigns (v2) + one MM limited-use Google Ads line (v1)

**Campaign 1 — MM email subscriber acquisition.** Lead Generation (Meta native lead form — reduces friction). Audience priority: (1) MM FB followers not yet subscribed; (2) Lookalike from Reader email list; (3) geographic targeting of Lincoln/Otero County residents with local-news interest. Ad creative: short "I'm Chris" editor video (authenticity outperforms polish for local news ads). **Budget: $5–10/day. CPL target under $2.50.** Projects to 60–130 new subscribers/month.

**Campaign 2 — Discover newsletter acquisition.** Audience: Reader FB + MM FB followers as Custom Audiences (exclude already-subscribed), then expand to 1% Lookalike from Reader email list. Creative: stunning seasonal photo + "Get the free monthly Cloudcroft visitor guide." Lead form captures email directly. **Budget: $5–8/day. CPL target under $2.00.** Projects to 75–120 new subs/month.

**Campaign 3 — Seasonal tourism (4 campaigns/year, 6 weeks each, activating 8 weeks before peak).** Landing pages are dedicated DC pages (fall ad lands on fall guide, ski ad lands on ski guide — mismatch kills conversion). **Budget: $500/campaign, $1,500–2,000/year.**

- Summer (run Apr–May): El Paso + Las Cruces within 150-mile radius. Message: *"Escape the heat — Cloudcroft is 25° cooler and 90 minutes away."*
- Fall (run Aug–Sep): broader NM + West Texas. Message: *"The aspens are turning gold. Here's where to see them."*
- Ski/Winter (run Nov–Dec): El Paso, Midland, Odessa, Lubbock families. Message: *"The southernmost ski area in the US is 90 minutes from El Paso."*
- Spring (run Feb–Mar): outdoor + photography enthusiasts. Message: *"Wildflowers, fresh trails, and 75° afternoons."*

**Campaign 4 — Retargeting (both sites).** Anyone visiting DC who doesn't subscribe gets retargeted 14 days with newsletter-focused creative. Anyone visiting MM who doesn't subscribe gets retargeted with "free weekly briefing" ads. **Budget: $3–5/day. One of the highest-ROI ad spend categories in digital marketing (v2).**

**Google Ads — DC search campaigns.** Priority keywords: *"Cloudcroft NM cabins/lodging/hotels," "things to do Cloudcroft," "Cloudcroft skiing," "weekend getaway El Paso mountains," "New Mexico mountain town."* **Match types: Exact + Phrase only. Never Broad (v1 — preserved).** Ad copy formula: H1 = keyword ("Cloudcroft NM Visitor Guide"), H2 = differentiator ("Locally Written — No Tourist Traps"), H3 = CTA ("Plan Your Mountain Escape"). Dedicated landing pages (not homepage) with single CTA — removing navigation from ad landing pages increases conversion 30–50% (both). **Budget: $300–500/mo, $3,600–6,000/year. CPC target < $1.50; cost-per-email-signup target < $8.00.** At Month 3–4 once search is optimized, add **Performance Max** at $10/day (v1 — preserved).

**Google Ads — MM limited use.** Annual reader-support / donation campaigns targeting *"support local journalism New Mexico"* during NM Local News Fund matching periods (v1). **Apply annually for Google News Initiative grants** — available specifically for local news publishers and can fund campaigns at no cost (both).

## 10. Email & First-Party Data

Email is the most valuable long-term marketing asset — unlike social followers, subscribers are a direct, owned relationship (v2).

### 10.1 The three-newsletter ecosystem (v2, expanded from v1's two-newsletter model)

| Newsletter | Audience | Cadence | Voice & purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Mountain Briefing (MM)** | Year-round residents, second-home owners, regional community | Weekly, Wednesday 7 AM | Editor's personal voice. Local news, events, accountability. Drives subscriptions and reader revenue. |
| **Cloudcroft This Season (DC)** | Visitors, trip planners, tourism-minded followers | Monthly, 1st Tuesday 8 AM | Warm, knowledgeable guide voice. Seasonal content, events, lodging. Drives tourism ad revenue. |
| **Cloudcroft Reader (existing, 3,200 subs)** | Mixed community + tourism | Existing cadence (maintain) | Current editorial voice. Protect this list's trust — use for cross-promo carefully, not frequently. |

All three live in the same Mailchimp account under separate audience lists (v2) — allows cross-promotion visibility without list merging and lets us eventually measure overlap between audiences.

**Platform:** Mailchimp. Free up to 500 combined subs, then $13–20/mo (v1). Free tier covers first 3–4 months of growth.

### 10.2 The Reader launch email — template (v2, preserve exactly)

Send from the Cloudcroft Reader brand, not from DC. Feel like an editorial recommendation from an editor the reader already trusts.

- **Subject line:** lead with content value, not brand. *"Your complete guide to Cloudcroft this [season]"* or *"The 2026 Cloudcroft visitor guide is here — and it's free."*
- **Opening:** *"One of the things we hear most from Cloudcroft Reader subscribers is that your friends and family constantly ask where to stay, what to do, and how to get to Cloudcroft. We built something for that."* Frames launch as community service, not self-promotion.
- **Body:** 2–3 highlights from DC — trail guide, lodging directory, events calendar — with real content previews. The email itself useful.
- **CTA:** *"Subscribe to the free monthly DiscoverCloudcroft.com visitor guide."*
- **Second CTA:** *"Share with someone planning a Cloudcroft trip."* Word-of-mouth from trusted readers is the highest-quality list-building source.
- **Frequency ceiling:** **max 4 DC cross-promo sends per year** from the Reader list — once per season. More than that risks trust erosion and higher unsubscribes.

Expected yield: 300–700 new DC newsletter subscribers from a single well-crafted send to the 3,200-subscriber Reader list at a 20–22% conversion rate of openers (v2).

### 10.3 Welcome sequence (v1, preserved — dropped in v2)

Every new subscriber to either list receives a 3-email automated welcome sequence:

- **Email 1 (immediate):** Welcome + our most popular guide or article.
- **Email 2 (Day 3):** The story of Mountain Monthly / DiscoverCloudcroft — why we exist.
- **Email 3 (Day 7):** Introduction to the sister publication + cross-promotion.

### 10.4 Newsletter content formulas

**Mountain Briefing (v2):** Lead (150 words, one story, editorial craft) → three quick headlines (bold + one-sentence context + link, scannable) → This Week in Cloudcroft (3–5 events with date/time/location — consistently most-clicked section) → sponsor message (one advertiser, framed as editor recommendation) → quarterly reader-support message (tied to something specific, e.g., *"We just published our 100th article"*) → permanent footer: *"Hosting visitors? Send them to DiscoverCloudcroft.com — the complete Cloudcroft visitor guide."*

**DC monthly (v2):** Hero seasonal story (200–300 words + photo + link) → This Month (3–5 events with practical detail: parking, cost, kid-friendly) → Featured Stay (one lodging property, 100 words, premium ad placement) → What's New (business openings, trail updates, road conditions — links to MM for news context) → Practical Bit (one tactical visitor tip: altitude awareness, best time to drive Highway 82, where to get groceries on a Sunday) → permanent footer: *"Stay connected year-round with Mountain Monthly."*

### 10.5 List health & hygiene (v1, preserved)

- **Open-rate floor:** below 35% on either list = immediate attention (v1). Local news newsletters average 40–55%, meaningfully above industry (v1).
- **Click-rate target:** 8–12%. Below 5% means content-expectation mismatch or buried CTAs (v1).
- **Re-engagement campaign every 6 months** to inactive subscribers (opened nothing in 90 days). Single "Are you still interested?" email. Remove non-responders. A smaller, engaged list outperforms a large disengaged one on every measurable axis, including advertiser value (v1).
- **Unsubscribe analysis quarterly.** Mailchimp collects reasons. Patterns reveal content problems before they become growth problems (v1).

### 10.6 List building — for each property

**For Mountain Monthly (v2):**
- Facebook Lead Ads seeded from MM 5,000-follower audience (exclude already-subscribed). $5–10/day → 30–60 new subs/mo at < $2.50/sub.
- Reader newsletter cross-promo once per quarter: estimated 150–300 new MM subs per mention.
- In-person signup sheet at every public event (council, Chamber, Mayfair, Harvest Festival). Face-to-face converts at 40–60%.
- MM Facebook "forward to a neighbor" post monthly.

**For DiscoverCloudcroft.com (v2):**
- The Reader launch email (detailed §10.2): 300–700 new subs from one send.
- Both Reader + MM FB pages promote DC newsletter with "Get the free seasonal visitor guide" framing. Combined 20,000 followers — even 1% conversion = 200/post.
- Homepage scroll-triggered popup at 50% scroll or 30 seconds: *"Get the free monthly Cloudcroft visitor guide."* (v2 — requires a small addition to `js/common.js`; see §13.)
- Visitor Guide PDF gated download: one-page *"Essential Cloudcroft Visitor Guide"* requiring email. Converts high-intent planners at 15–25%. **Already flagged as an asset in `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md` §2 — same asset, double duty.**
- White Sands visitor-center QR card: physical QR display directing to DC newsletter signup. Visitors who just finished White Sands are actively planning their next stop (v2).

## 11. Measurement, Tooling & Reporting Cadence

**Stack:** Mailchimp, Meta Ads Manager, Google Ads, GA4, GSC (with News filter for MM), FB/IG Insights, YouTube Studio, Pinterest Analytics (both). Ahrefs or Semrush for keyword research at $100–200/mo (carried from `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md` §11).

**Monthly review — first Monday of every month, 90 minutes (v2, expanded with v1's ritual):**

1. Pull all KPI-table metrics into a shared Google Sheet. Flag any metric that moved more than 15% in either direction — investigate before moving on.
2. Identify best-performing piece of content on each site. What did it do differently? Produce more like it.
3. Identify worst-performing scheduled content. Topic, format, timing, or execution? Document the lesson.
4. Paid review: kill any campaign above target CPL; increase budget on campaigns below target. Check Meta attribution and Google Ads conversion tracking for anomalies.
5. Email health: open/click/unsubscribe trends. If open rate dropped, subject line or content — test one variable at a time.
6. **Reader cross-promotion audit:** how many times did we cross-promote DC from the Reader page this month? Did we stay at or below 2×/week? Referral traffic yield from Reader cross-promo posts?
7. Partnership progress: conversations this month, pending, one partnership action before next meeting.
8. Set 3 marketing goals for the coming month, owner-assigned, reviewed next month.

## 13. Risks, Open Questions, and Decisions That Still Need an Owner

1. **Reader list trust erosion.** The single highest risk. Every additional DC cross-promo from the Reader list above the 4/year ceiling is a direct bet against trust. Monitor unsubscribe rate every send; abort cadence escalation if it spikes.
2. **MM Facebook follower baseline ambiguity (v2 flag, preserved).** v2 Master KPI table lists MM email subscribers as "Unknown — audit first." Day 1 task: export current MM Mailchimp list, set a real baseline, then recalibrate Y1 target if the starting point differs materially from zero.
3. **Landing page infrastructure for paid ads.** Both docs specify dedicated Google Ads landing pages (not homepage), with single CTA. The repo is static HTML with no CMS — each landing page is a new `.html` file in the repo, added via `content/sync-nav.py`. Budget engineering time into campaign launches.
4. **Homepage scroll-triggered popup.** V2 specifies. Needs a small addition to `js/common.js` — not a free lift. Assign implementation to the same sprint as the Week-1 consistency fixes from `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md`.
5. **Elevation discrepancy.** `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md` §5.6 flags "9,000 feet" on `index.html` vs "8,663 feet" in an earlier SEO memo. The v2 marketing doc's Pinterest SEO example ("Lincoln National Forest at 9,000 feet") also uses 9,000. Pick one canonically, find-and-replace sitewide — applies to marketing copy too.
6. **Reader FB follower count.** v1 says "12,500+", v2 says "15,000+". Carrying v2's 15,000+ through this plan; confirm live FB Insights figure at Day 1 and adjust targets if wrong.
7. **Google News setup.** V1 mentions Newspack/Yoast (Mountain Monthly is WordPress); make sure DiscoverCloudcroft.com is **not** submitted to Publisher Center — it is not a news product.
8. **Which email platform owns which list.** Three lists, one Mailchimp account, three audiences. Audience separation discipline is load-bearing for the cadence ceilings — if the lists merge, the 4/year Reader ceiling becomes meaningless. Set up the audience structure before the launch email goes out.
9. **Photography pipeline.** Budget is allocated; the operational gap is *who* and *when*. Need a named photographer (staff or contract), a named shot list per season, and a review cadence. Assign in Week 1.
10. **Alignment with SEO plan's keyword-map gate.** Every DC landing page for Meta / Google Ads must be logged in the `md/keyword-map.md` file recommended by `The-Big-Plan-for-SEO.md` §4.2. Marketing and SEO share one keyword map, not two.
