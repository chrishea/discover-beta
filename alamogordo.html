About

Where Desert Meets Mountain

Alamogordo sits at the base of the Sacramento Mountains, serving as the gateway between the Tularosa Basin's stunning white sand dunes and the cool pine forests of Cloudcroft above. This desert city offers a striking contrast to the mountain village, with warm temperatures, world-class museums, and proximity to one of America's newest national parks.

Whether you're looking for groceries, supplies, or a full day of exploration, Alamogordo provides everything you need while visiting Cloudcroft. From the otherworldly landscapes of White Sands to the fascinating history of space exploration, there's plenty to discover.

Alamogordo & White Sands

Things To Do

Top Attractions

From stunning natural wonders to fascinating museums, Alamogordo offers unforgettable experiences.

🏜️

White Sands National Park

Explore the world's largest gypsum dune field. Hike the trails, sled down pristine white dunes, and witness spectacular sunsets over this otherworldly landscape.

15 min drive Hiking Photography
🚀

Space History Museum

Discover the history of space exploration at the New Mexico Museum of Space History. Features rockets, spacecraft, the International Space Hall of Fame, and an IMAX Theater.

Downtown Family-Friendly IMAX Theater
🦁

Alameda Park Zoo

Visit the oldest zoo in the Southwest, home to over 250 animals from around the world. Perfect for families with children of all ages.

Downtown Kid-Friendly Affordable
🎨

Tularosa Basin Museum

Learn about the rich cultural history of the region, from prehistoric peoples to the railroad era and the development of the space industry.

Local History Art Exhibits Free Admission
🌅

Take the breathtaking Highway 82 from desert floor to mountain peaks. Watch the landscape transform as you climb 4,000+ feet in just 16 miles.

Travel Info

Getting to Alamogordo

Multiple routes bring you to this gateway city.

🚗

From Cloudcroft

Take Highway 82 west down the mountain. The scenic 16-mile drive takes about 25-30 minutes with stunning views of the Tularosa Basin.

🛣️

From El Paso

Head north on US-54 for approximately 90 miles. The drive takes about 1.5 hours through the desert landscape.

🧭

From Las Cruces

Take US-70 east for about 70 miles through the Organ Mountains-Desert Peaks. Allow approximately 1 hour and 15 minutes.

✈️

By Air

The closest major airport is El Paso International (ELP), about 90 miles south. Alamogordo has a small regional airport for private aircraft.

Lodging
