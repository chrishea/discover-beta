# The Crofting Bed & Breakfast

**A century-old historic home turned B&B, two blocks from Burro Avenue, with homemade breakfast and individually decorated rooms.**

---

## Overview

The Crofting has been welcoming guests since the 1990s, but the house itself goes back to 1909 — one of the oldest structures in Cloudcroft. It sits on Swallow Place, a quiet side street just a short walk from the village's main drag. The building is a two-story historic home surrounded by spruce trees, with the kind of character you don't find in new construction: creaky wood floors, antique furnishings, and rooms full of country decor and vintage objects.

The inn has eight rooms, each individually furnished and decorated. Several open onto private balconies with mountain views. Mornings include a full breakfast — cooked by the hosts — served at 8:30 a.m. The owners, currently listed as Gail and Scott on review platforms, draw consistent praise for their warmth and local knowledge.

This is a traditional bed and breakfast, not a resort. No kitchenettes, no late check-in, no daily housekeeping. What you get is a historic room, a real breakfast, and hosts who know the area well. It's a good fit for couples and travelers who want character over amenities. Families with young children should note that rollaway beds and cribs are not available.

## What Guests Say

The Crofting carries a 9.2 out of 10 score on Expedia based on 678 reviews, with consistent praise for the hosts, breakfast quality, and the property's historic character. The location — quiet but close to town — is frequently cited as a highlight.

Common positives: warm and knowledgeable hosts, good full breakfast, charming antique decor, mountain views from balcony rooms, quiet setting.

Common concerns: rooms are small; wood floors can be noisy between rooms; décor is heavy and may not suit minimalist tastes; breakfast is at a fixed time (8:30 a.m.), which doesn't work for early departures.

The property has been a bed and breakfast since the mid-1990s and draws a mix of couples, solo travelers, and multi-generational family groups.
