# Dusty Boots Motel & Cafe

**A quirky, meticulously themed motel on James Canyon Highway with an on-site cafe, hot tub, and Pedego e-bike rentals — a short drive from the village center.**

---

## Overview

The Dusty Boots started life as the Aspen Motel. Under current ownership it's been transformed room by room into one of the more distinctive places to stay in the Sacramento Mountains. Each of the 15 rooms has its own theme — past guests have reported a Roy Rogers room, a Route 66 room, an Antique Rose room, a Beverly Hills/Texas Oil room, a Boy Scout room, a Ski room, a Harry Potter room, and a Western room, among others. The decor is hands-on and detailed: antiques, quilts, custom touches, and in at least one room, a watering-can showerhead and a canoe suspended from the ceiling. The property sits at the edge of Lincoln National Forest, within walking distance of a city park and trail.

The Dusty Boots Cafe operates on-site and serves three meals daily during summer, with a rotating menu that's more than 90 percent made from scratch. Motel guests receive a dining discount. Saturday evenings feature a German-themed dinner service. The owners are consistently described by guests as warm, hands-on, and full of local knowledge.

The property is located on James Canyon Highway — about 0.7 miles from Cloudcroft's main street — which means it's a short drive or longer walk to Burro Avenue shops and restaurants. Some rooms face the highway; lighter sleepers should request a room away from the road.

## Cafe

The Dusty Boots Cafe is open daily during summer from 7:00 a.m. to 9:00 p.m. Breakfast runs 7:00 a.m. to noon. More than 90 percent of menu items are made from scratch. The menu includes biscuits and gravy, French toast, pancakes, BLTs, patty melts, chicken strips, taco platters, and steak and shrimp. Saturday evenings feature a German dinner from 5:00 p.m. to 8:00 p.m. — schnitzel, sauerbraten, bratwurst, German meatballs, spaetzle, and housemade cottage rolls.

The cafe is open to the public, not just motel guests. It gets busy on weekends; expect a possible wait.

*Cafe hours listed are summer hours. Confirm winter/shoulder season hours directly with the property.*

## What Guests Say

Dusty Boots draws strong reviews across platforms, driven largely by the room theming, cleanliness, and the on-site cafe. Guests frequently mention the quality of the memory foam beds, the thick towels, and the owners' genuine hospitality and local knowledge. The complimentary banana bread and the sled loans in winter are regularly called out as thoughtful touches.

Common positives: creative and immersive room themes, spotless housekeeping, friendly and helpful staff, excellent cafe food, good value for the area, pet-friendly atmosphere.

Common concerns: some rooms are on the small side; rooms facing the highway can be noisy; housekeeping is on request rather than daily; a few recent guests noted inconsistent room supplies (soap, tissues). The property has been in an ongoing renovation program since new ownership took over; room quality may vary.
