03

Best Activities in Cloudcroft

From world-class mountain trails to a slow afternoon on Burro Avenue, these are the seven activities that make Cloudcroft worth the drive.

1. Hike Osha Trail

If you want one easy answer for a first Cloudcroft hike, this is it. The Forest Service calls Osha Trail a great beginning hike for all ages and places it just north of the village off US 82. It is close, scenic, and manageable enough for many visitors to do on the same day they arrive. You get forest, elevation, and basin views without a long drive or a complicated route.

Watch: The trail sits around 8,500 feet, so altitude can catch people who came straight up from Alamogordo. Bring water and a layer even for a short hike.

2. Mexican Canyon Trestle & Railroad Trails

This is one of the most Cloudcroft-specific things you can do. The Mexican Canyon Trestle ties the village to its railroad history and gives shape to several nearby trail experiences, including the Cloud-Climbing Trestle Trail, Overlook Trail, Village Spur Trail, and parts of the Old Cloudcroft Highway alignment. The activity can be as light or as involved as you want.

Watch: Trail access around the Trestle Recreation Area is not fully normal. The Forest Service says the Old Cloudcroft Highway Trail is not currently accessible from the Trestle Recreation Area. Check status before planning a longer route.

3. Stroll Burro Avenue

Burro Avenue is not a major shopping district. That is part of why it works. It is compact, walkable, and easy to browse without pressure. The Chamber shopping directory shows a concentrated strip of shops, galleries, jewelry, gifts, outdoor gear, wine, tea, and related stops, especially around the Burro Street Exchange. One good lap can include coffee, a boutique stop, a gallery, and lunch.

Watch: Hours vary more than many visitors expect. The district is best for browsing, not serious comparison shopping. Weekend parking can feel compressed when events are in town.

4. Build a Food-and-Drink Crawl

In Cloudcroft, eating can be part of the activity rather than just a break in the day. Burro Street Bakery, Black Bear Coffee, Old Barrel Tea Company, Noisy Water Winery, Cloudcroft Brewing Company, Mad Jack's BBQ, and The Lodge's dining outlets give the village enough variety to build a slow afternoon or evening around stops rather than a single reservation.

Watch: Hours and seasonal patterns vary. Do not assume late-night options. Popular places can have waits on busy weekends.

5. Drive the Sunspot Scenic Byway

One of the strongest drives tied to a Cloudcroft visit. New Mexico Tourism describes it as a 15-mile road beginning two miles south of Cloudcroft, with stops such as Haynes Canyon Vista and broad views toward White Sands and the basin country below. The road itself is the activity. Even if Sunspot access is limited, the drive still gives you the transition from dense high-country forest to wider open viewpoints.

Watch: Sunspot access is currently unclear and likely closed. Weather can change quickly on the route. Do not assume every stop is open or staffed.

6. The Lodge & Its Golf Course

Golf at The Lodge is one of the classic Cloudcroft activities because it is tied to the village's long-running idea of mountain leisure. The Lodge's current site presents golf, dining, and the historic hotel itself as part of one experience, and the golf course opened for the 2026 season. Even for non-golfers, The Lodge is one of the strongest places in town to build a slower afternoon or evening around views, drinks, and a meal.

Watch: Golf is seasonal. Historic properties trade consistency for character. Dining reservations can matter more than in a larger market.

7. Camp, RV, or Rent a Cabin

The area's cabin stock, RV parks, and campgrounds do not just support activity. They are part of the activity. Staying in the pines, cooking outside, taking a short walk at dusk, and using town only as a resupply point is a real version of the place. Options include Pines Campground in Lincoln National Forest, 16 Springs, Camp Rio, and a large inventory of cabin and short-term-rental stays.

Watch: Fire restrictions matter. Forest and dispersed camping rules are tighter in 2026 than many visitors may expect. Winter access, check-in complexity, and cleaning fees can change the value math for rentals.
💡

Insider tip: Pair activities. Cloudcroft is best in combinations, not single-purpose sprints. A morning hike, an afternoon on Burro Avenue, and an evening meal at The Lodge or Cloudcroft Brewing makes a near-perfect day.

04

Outdoor Activities

The Sacramento Mountains offer year-round outdoor recreation tied directly to a Cloudcroft stay.

🥾

Easy Hiking

Osha Trail (T10), Overlook Trail (T5001B), and Village Spur Trail (T5001A) — close to town, beginner-friendly, good for families.

Best: May – October
🛤️

Scenic-History Hikes

Cloud-Climbing Trestle Trail (T5001) and Grand View Trail / Salado Canyon area — tied to the old railroad grade.

Best: Spring & Fall
⛰️

Longer Trail Outings

Switchback Trail (T5004) and other Sacramento Ranger District trails for hikers planning ahead with the district trail map.

Best: May – October
🚗

Scenic Drives

Sunspot Scenic Byway (NM 6563), Fresnal Canyon Road (FR 162C) with basin overlooks, and Sacramento Canyon backroads.

Best: All year (verify access)
🏕️

Camping & RV

Pines Campground (opens May 15, 24 sites), 16 Springs RV park, and Camp Rio in the Mayhill area.

Best: May – October
⛷️

Winter Recreation

Ski Cloudcroft (2025–26 season closed March 7 due to warm weather), snow walks, and fireside lodge weekends.

Best: Dec – March (conditional)
⛰️

Altitude reminder: Cloudcroft sits at 8,676 feet. If you are coming from sea level or from Alamogordo (4,000 ft), plan a slower first day. Bring water even on short walks. Cell service becomes unreliable once you get away from town.

05

In-Town Activities

Cloudcroft's village is small, but it rewards slow exploration. These are the in-town activities that give the place its feel.

🛍️

Burro Avenue & Burro Street Exchange

The core "just be in town" activity. The Chamber describes Burro Street Exchange as a mixed retail and eatery cluster that reflects an Old West feel. It concentrates the town's easiest browsing.

☕

Coffee, Tea, Bakery & Wine

A coffee stop can be a hinge between activities. Good in-town pause points:

  • Black Bear Coffee
  • Burro Street Bakery
  • Old Barrel Tea Company
  • Noisy Water Winery
  • Instant Karma
🍽️

Dining as an Evening Activity

Cloudcroft is not a late-night town, but dinner can still be a destination activity. Strongest anchors:

  • The Lodge / 1899 and the lounge
  • Cloudcroft Brewing Company
  • Mad Jack's BBQ
  • Local cafés and casual spots
📖

Quiet In-Town Time

Cloudcroft also rewards low-key activity: sitting on a cabin deck, walking short residential stretches near the village, reading or working from a porch while weather moves through, treating town as a pause rather than a program. That is not filler. It is part of why repeat visitors come back.

Intro

Cloudcroft works best when you treat it as a compact mountain base rather than a checklist town. The village itself is small. The range of things to do is not.

In one day you can hike a Forest Service trail, look across Mexican Canyon, browse Burro Avenue, stop for coffee or tea, drive the Sunspot Scenic Byway, and end with dinner or a drink. In another season, that same day might turn into snow play, skiing, or a lodge-and-fireplace kind of trip.

This guide focuses on activities that are actually tied to a Cloudcroft stay in 2026. It does not try to list every possible outing in the Sacramento Mountains. It curates the activities that make the place distinct.

⚠️

2026 note: Conditions matter more than usual this year. Lincoln National Forest entered Stage 1 fire restrictions on March 27, 2026, and some recreation areas remain under separate closure orders. Sunspot information is also conflicted across official pages; one visitor page still describes tours and a walking loop, but the NMSU calendar page says Sunspot, the visitor center, surrounding areas, and local hiking trails are closed to the public for the foreseeable future.

Overview

What Cloudcroft does well, what it does not, and how to spend a single day if that's all you have.

What Cloudcroft is good at

  • Forest hikes that start close to town
  • Scenic drives with fast payoff
  • Short, memorable overlooks and trestle views
  • Burro Avenue strolling, shopping, and food stops
  • Cabin, campground, and RV-based mountain downtime
  • Seasonal events that can turn a quiet weekend into a busy one
  • Golf, if you want a classic Cloudcroft activity with history attached

What it is not

  • A deep nightlife market
  • A large-town restaurant scene
  • A place where you can assume late hours, broad inventory, or chain-style consistency
  • A place where you should ignore weather, fire restrictions, or closures

Best bets if you only have one day

06 — Seasonal Guide

When to Visit

Every season brings something different to Cloudcroft. Here is what to expect.

🌸

Spring

March – May

Good for hiking before summer crowds. Also the season when wind and fire conditions can complicate plans. Check restrictions before planning campfires, dispersed camping, or certain recreation areas.

☀️

Summer

June – August

Peak Cloudcroft season: cooler weather, open trails, outdoor meals, events, and family travel. Best season for first-time visitors. Also the busiest time for cabins, festivals, and casual trail use.

🍁

Fall

September – November

Strong shoulder season with crisp weather and high scenic value. Good for scenic drives, golf, hiking, and quieter cabin trips. One of the best seasons for couples and repeat visitors.

❄️

Winter

December – March

Potentially excellent for ski weekends, snow play, and lodge stays. Also the most conditional season because snow and operations vary. Good for travelers who can pivot if weather changes.

07 — By Traveler Type

Best Activities by Traveler Type

Different travelers get different things from Cloudcroft. Here are curated picks.

👋

First-Time Visitors

Osha Trail, Mexican Canyon Trestle area, Burro Avenue, The Lodge for dinner or drinks, and the Sunspot Scenic Byway if conditions allow.

👪

Families

Osha Trail, trestle viewing, Ski Cloudcroft in a good snow year, cabin stays with low-key town browsing, and bakery/coffee-stop days in town.

💑

Couples

The Lodge and golf or dinner, scenic drive on NM 6563, cabin weekend with one good hike and one slow town day, Burro Avenue plus wine or tea.

🥾

Hikers

Osha Trail for convenience, trestle-area trails for short scenic history, Switchback or deeper Sacramento Ranger District trail planning for longer efforts.

🚗

Day-Trippers from Alamogordo

Osha Trail, Burro Avenue, Mexican Canyon Trestle, lunch or coffee or brewery stop, and a partial Sunspot Scenic Byway drive.

🏡

Repeat Visitors & Second-Home Owners

Cabin-based weekends, slower food-and-drink circuits, seasonal event weekends, forest-road drives and camp-oriented stays. Doing less, on purpose.

08 — Know Before You Go

Planning Notes

2026 conditions, closures, and practical advice for your trip.

🔥

Fire Restrictions

Stage 1 fire restrictions are in effect forest-wide beginning March 27, 2026 and scheduled to remain until September 30, 2026 unless rescinded. Check before planning campfires or dispersed camping.

🚫

Bluff Springs Closure

Official Forest Service alert says the Bluff Springs recreation area, parking lot, toilets, and Trail 112 bridge are closed through December 31, 2026 unless rescinded.

🔭

Sunspot Closure Conflict

One official visitor page still describes open-season access, but the NMSU Sunspot calendar page says the observatory, visitor center, surrounding areas, and local hiking trails are closed for the foreseeable future. Treat as closed until confirmed.

⛷️

Ski Season Variability

Ski Cloudcroft's official site says the 2025–26 season closed March 7, 2026 because of warm weather and lack of snow. Winter visits are conditional.

⛰️

Altitude

Altitude changes effort levels fast. Carry water even on short walks. Forest roads can matter as much as trail conditions.

📱

Cell Service & Prep

Cell service becomes unreliable once you get away from town. Check official Forest Service alerts before hiking, camping, or driving forest roads. Pair activities — Cloudcroft is best in combinations.
