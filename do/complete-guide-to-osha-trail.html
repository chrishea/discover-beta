About

A Peaceful Forest and Meadow Trail

Osha Trail is a scenic hiking trail winding through the Sacramento Mountains near Cloudcroft, New Mexico. Named after the osha plant (Ligusticum porteri), a medicinal root long prized by local healers and herbalists, the trail offers a peaceful journey through dense pine forests and open mountain meadows.

The approximately 4-mile round-trip route features moderate elevation changes and connects to other trail systems in Lincoln National Forest, making it a versatile choice for day hikers. At around 8,600 feet elevation, the trail offers cool temperatures even in summer, along with stunning wildflower displays in the meadows during the growing season.

Trail Information

Trail Details

Plan your hike on the Osha Trail with these key details.

📏

Distance

Approximately 4 miles round trip. The trail is an out-and-back route that can also connect to other trails in the Lincoln National Forest system for longer adventures.

📈

Elevation Change

Starting at around 8,600 feet with moderate elevation changes throughout. The trail gently rolls through forest and meadow terrain without extreme climbs.

🗓️

Best Seasons

Late spring through fall (May–October) offers the best conditions. Summer brings wildflower blooms in the meadows. Fall foliage is spectacular in September and October.

🚗

Trailhead Access

Located in Lincoln National Forest near Cloudcroft. Access via forest roads from the Cloudcroft area. Check with the Sacramento Ranger District for current road conditions.

Trail Highlights

What Makes Osha Trail Special

Four reasons to explore this beautiful Sacramento Mountain trail.

🌼

Wildflower Meadows

Open mountain meadows burst with wildflowers during summer months. Look for columbine, iris, and the trail's namesake osha plant along the way.

🌲

Dense Pine Forest

Walk through cathedral-like stands of ponderosa pine and Douglas fir. The towering canopy provides welcome shade and fills the air with the scent of pine.

🏔️

Mountain Views

Meadow clearings offer panoramic views of the surrounding Sacramento Mountains. On clear days, the vistas stretch for miles across the forested ridgelines.

🧘

Peaceful Solitude

Less trafficked than some popular Cloudcroft trails, Osha Trail offers a quieter experience. Enjoy the sounds of birdsong and wind through the pines in relative solitude.

Be Prepared

Visitor Tips

Make the most of your Osha Trail hike with these helpful suggestions.

💧

Carry Water

No potable water is available on the trail. Bring at least one liter per person. The high altitude increases dehydration risk even on cooler days.

🥾

Proper Footwear

Trail shoes or hiking boots with good traction are recommended. Some sections may be muddy after rain, and rocky patches require sturdy soles.

🌤️

Start Early

Begin your hike in the morning to avoid afternoon thunderstorms, especially during summer monsoon season (July–September). Lightning is a real hazard at this elevation.

🧥

Layer Up

Mountain weather is unpredictable. Temperatures can drop quickly, and rain can appear without warning. Bring a light jacket or rain layer even on sunny days.

🗺️

Bring a Map

Cell service is unreliable in the forest. Download an offline trail map before your hike. The trail connects to other paths, so knowing your route is important.

🌿

Leave No Trace

Pack out all trash, stay on marked trails, and do not pick wildflowers or plants. Help preserve this beautiful trail for future visitors to enjoy.
