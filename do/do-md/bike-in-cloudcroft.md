# Biking in Cloudcroft

## Overview

Cloudcroft is one of the better biking bases in southern New Mexico. It works for two very different kinds of riders:
- mountain bikers who want forest trails, elevation, and technical effort
- road and gravel riders who want scenic mountain loops and cooler temperatures

The riding is real. The main decision is not whether there is enough biking. It is which kind of biking fits your trip.

## Why this matters

Biking makes more sense in Cloudcroft than many first-time visitors expect. The elevation gives you cooler weather, the forest gives you trail variety, and the road network drops you into some of the most dramatic mountain-to-basin riding in the region.

If you already travel with bikes, Cloudcroft is one of the strongest reasons to extend a trip.

## What to know

### Mountain biking
The strongest current official source is the **Lincoln National Forest biking page**, which lists multiple Cloudcroft-area trails open to mountain biking, including:
- Rim Trail (T105)
- Elk Canyon Trail (T253D)
- Fir Trail (T122)
and many others.

Useful link:
- https://www.fs.usda.gov/r03/lincoln/recreation/opportunities/biking

Cloudcroft Reader’s 2023 mountain-bike trail guide also supports the area’s riding depth and specifically calls out the old railroad and loop-style route possibilities.
Useful link:
- https://www.cloudcroftreader.com/p/mountain-bike-trails

### Road and event riding
The strongest current event-style evidence is **Cloudcroft Bike Racing**, which promotes 2026 fondo and gravel events tied to Cloudcroft and High Rolls.
Useful link:
- https://cloudcroftbikeracing.com/

### Local bike support
High Altitude remains the main in-town bike shop/outdoor store resource, but its official site says it is not doing bike rentals for the season. That matters if you were hoping to arrive without a bike.
Useful link:
- https://www.highaltitudenm.com/bicycles

## What it’s actually like

Cloudcroft biking is not casual-flat riding. Even the easier routes are shaped by elevation.

Mountain biking here tends to mean:
- forest trails
- old grades
- mixed surfaces
- elevation gain
- routes that can feel longer than the mileage suggests

Road and gravel riding here tends to mean:
- scenic climbs
- basin views
- cooler weather at the top
- more serious descending and climbing than casual riders may expect

That makes Cloudcroft a good bike destination for people who already like riding, and a weaker fit for people who only want a very easy spin.

## Best for

- mountain bikers
- gravel riders
- stronger road cyclists
- summer visitors escaping heat
- riders traveling with their own bikes
- repeat visitors who want an active trip

## Potential drawbacks or cautions

- Altitude and climbing are the main issues.
- Some trails are mixed-use, not bike-only.
- Forest restrictions and closures can affect plans.
- Current in-town rental availability appears limited.
- Riders should check trail condition after storms, snow, or fire-related closures.

- shuttle or event support if you are planning a bigger ride
