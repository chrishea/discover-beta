The 2nd Annual Pickleball in the Clouds is a community-run fundraiser tournament at the Cloudcroft Village Courts — high-elevation play, three doubles brackets, and a weekend designed to feed Cloudcroft Bear Basketball.

June 6–7, 2026 · Cloudcroft Village Courts · directed by Samantha Odom · proceeds to We Love Cloudcroft
How to register

It's easy — register at PickleballDen.com

The tournament is hosted on the PickleballDen platform. Three quick steps:

  1. Go to PickleballDen.com
  2. Search for "Cloudcroft"
  3. Select 2nd Annual Pickleball in the Clouds and complete your registration

Even if you sign in via the app on tournament day, please stop by the registration tent to pick up your T-shirt — it's included with your registration fee.

Why we play

It's a true FUNdraiser

100% of profits go straight to Cloudcroft kids. This year's proceeds support Cloudcroft Bear Basketball — middle school and high school, boys and girls — through the We Love Cloudcroft foundation.

Where the money goes

Proceeds support We Love Cloudcroft, a charity organization that financially supports local youth sports. This year's tournament has a special interest in Cloudcroft Bear Basketball — middle-school and high-school programs, boys and girls.

We are still accepting sponsors for this event. If you or your business is interested in partnering with us financially, please let us know — we will send a student your way ASAP. We also have a few spaces available for food vendors.

A note to competitive players: The Cloudcroft Village Courts have some dead spots that can affect play. Please keep in mind that this is a FUNdraiser for Cloudcroft-area youth — the spirit of the event is community, fun, and supporting our Bears.

With your registration

What's included

The registration fee covers more than your bracket entry. Here's what to expect on tournament weekend at the Cloudcroft Village Courts.

Tournament T-shirt

A custom 2nd Annual Pickleball in the Clouds T-shirt is included with your registration fee. Pick it up at the registration tent on tournament day, even if you've already signed in via the PickleballDen app.

Food trucks will be parked at the courts so you and your team can refuel between matches without leaving the venue. Vendor lineup confirmed closer to the date.

Practical notes

Pack for high-elevation play

The Cloudcroft Village Courts sit at 8,676 feet. Plan for stronger UV, drier air, and faster breath fatigue than you'd see at lower-elevation tournaments. Hydrate the day before, bring sunscreen, and don't be surprised if your first match feels harder than your usual rating.

Bring a jacket for Saturday evening — temperatures drop fast after sunset, even in June.

Saturday melodrama in the park

For Saturday evening entertainment, the FREE Melodrama in the Park starts at 7:30 PM. It's a community tradition — easy walk from the courts and a good way to recover between play days.

Court conditions & the FUNdraiser tone

The Cloudcroft Village Courts have some dead spots that can affect play and frustrate competitive players. The tournament organizers ask players to keep in mind that this is a FUNdraiser for Cloudcroft-area youth — the goal is community, fun, and supporting Cloudcroft Bear Basketball.

Sponsors & food vendors

The tournament is still accepting sponsors and food vendors for the 2026 event. Sponsorship helps the proceeds reach more Cloudcroft kids; food vendors help keep players on-site between matches. If you or your business is interested, contact tournament director Samantha Odom or the We Love Cloudcroft team.

Funding & community partners

Paid in part by the Cloudcroft Lodger's Tax. Tournament proceeds benefit We Love Cloudcroft — 100% to Cloudcroft Bear Basketball (MS & HS, boys and girls).

Weather & what to confirm before driving up

June in Cloudcroft is typically mild and clear, but mountain weather can shift. Check the National Weather Service Cloudcroft forecast and NMRoads before driving up. See the visitor FAQ for fire restrictions, water conservation, and other current notices.
