Decision aid

Good for · skip if

What this shop actually does well, and when to aim somewhere else on the Burro Ave strip instead.

Good for

  • A first-stop coffee walking onto Burro from the west end
  • Settling in upstairs with a book or a friend
  • Seasonal drink fans who want more than a standard latte
  • Beans to take home — or to ship home
  • Gluten-free, dairy-free, or sugar-free drinkers
  • Anyone who prefers a shop that rotates local art

  • You want a full breakfast — go to Dave's Café or Dusty Boots
  • It's Monday or Tuesday — the shop is closed those days
  • You want a full bar — Western Bar is next door up the street
  • You want dedicated BBQ or a sit-down dinner — wrong category for this shop

Practical notes

Hours, seasons & calling ahead

Posted hours are Wednesday through Saturday 8 AM to 4 PM, Sunday 8 AM to 3 PM, closed Monday and Tuesday. The shop runs a morning-to-early-afternoon coffee rhythm — weekends are the most consistent window.

Hours can shift seasonally. Before making the drive up off-season, a quick call to (575) 682-1239 or the most recent @blackbearcoffeenm Instagram story is the surest way to confirm.

Sit-down vs. grab-and-go

This is a linger-and-socialize shop, not a morning-rush espresso bar. The upstairs seating area is the reason — a cozy space suited for a friend, a book, or just settling in. Locals treat it as a gathering room, not a drive-thru alternative.

If you're on a tight schedule, you can absolutely take the cup to go — but you'll be leaving the best part of the experience on the stairs.

The gallery & local art

Co-owners Crystal and Nathan Tompkins run a petite gallery space inside the shop with a monthly featured artist rotation. Crystal is a working photographer — Cloudcroft Reader has specifically identified her as the shop's October featured artist — and her own work is displayed alongside the guest rotations.

It's a small cultural layer most Burro Avenue retail doesn't have, and it's part of why the shop rewards sitting down.

Beans, merch & ship-home

Roasted beans are sold in-shop for home brewing, and the shop's website supports ship-home ordering — so Cloudcroft coffee doesn't have to stay in Cloudcroft. Merchandise (mugs, tumblers) is typically stocked near the counter.

If you're carrying gear from a full trip and can't fit another bag, order a pound online at mybbcoffee.com and have it arrive at home a few days after you do.

Editor's take

"Black Bear is the Burro Avenue shop that reads like a local coffee room that happens to welcome visitors, not a visitor coffee room that tolerates locals. The seasonal drinks tell you the kitchen actually cares; the upstairs seating tells you the shop expects you to stay; the petite gallery tells you it's doing more than pouring coffee. If the hours line up, it's the shop to start the Cloudcroft day at."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
