The menu

A working BBQ counter menu — brisket, ribs, and house-smoked sausages anchor the pit, with sides, sandwiches, specialties, and desserts filling the plate. Specific items rotate; "moist or lean" is the brisket call you'll be asked at the counter.

1

Smoked meats

House specialty

Premium cuts smoked low and slow.

  • Sliced brisket (moist or lean)
  • Chopped brisket
  • Baby back ribs
  • Beef ribs
  • Smoked turkey
  • BBQ chicken

If you eat here once, get the sliced moist brisket — that's the kitchen's headline.

2

Sausage

House-smoked

Three smoked links — plain, jalapeño, and jalapeño-cheddar.

  • Smoked sausage (plain)
  • Jalapeño sausage
  • Jalapeño-cheddar sausage

The jalapeño-cheddar is where the Texas accent shows up loudest — order it as a third meat on a combo plate.

3

Sandwiches

Piled on a bun with your choice of meat — lunch-speed BBQ.

  • Brisket sandwich
  • Pulled pork sandwich
  • Smoked turkey sandwich
  • Sausage sandwich

A brisket sandwich + a side of fried okra is a sneaky-good lunch order.

4

Specialties

House creations that go beyond the sliced-plate format.

  • Brisket fries
  • Loaded baked potato
  • Texas-style chili (no beans)
  • Dinner plates — meat + 2 sides

Brisket fries as a shared starter, dinner plate as the main — that's the order that fills a table.

5

Sides

Fried okra featured

House-made sides — the honest backbone of a Texas plate. Fried okra is one of the best items on the menu: perfectly crunchy, crisp, and flavorful.

  • Fried okra — perfectly crunchy, crisp, and flavorful
  • Mac & cheese
  • Smoked baked beans
  • Tater salad
  • Coleslaw

Order the fried okra. Then mac & cheese plus smoked baked beans for the classic pairing.

6

Desserts

Simple sweets to finish a BBQ lunch.

  • Banana pudding
  • Homemade pies
  • Cobblers

Banana pudding — the canonical closer on a Central Texas plate.

Decision aid

Good for · skip if

What this kitchen does well, and when to aim somewhere else on the James Canyon strip instead.

Good for

  • A dedicated BBQ lunch on a JCH roadtrip day
  • A lean-vs-moist brisket decision with house-made sides
  • Active-duty, veteran, or military families (discount on request)
  • A counter-service BBQ room rather than table-service dinner
  • Anyone who came for the side that steals the plate — the fried okra

Skip if

  • It's Thursday — the shop is closed
  • You want post-oak credentials and a BBQ flight — Mad Jack's is two minutes down at 105
  • You want a diner-range menu with breakfast — Big Daddy's runs 7 AM–9 PM daily
  • You want a walkable Burro-Ave stroll with dinner — this is on the highway
  • You want a special-occasion dinner room — go to Eighteen99
  • You're arriving late in the afternoon without calling first — risk of sold out

Practical notes

Hours, sell-out & calling ahead

Posted hours are 11 AM – 6 PM, Monday / Tuesday / Wednesday / Friday / Saturday / Sunday, closed Thursday. Like most working BBQ kitchens, the shop can close early if inventory runs out. Regional dining-guide coverage flagged Brother-N-Law's online detail as thin — meaning the posted schedule isn't always the same-day reality.

If you're coming specifically for BBQ and driving up JCH, call (215) 858-0400 before leaving. Mid-week lunch is usually the lowest-risk window; summer weekends are when sell-out risk is highest.

Parking, payment & the 215 area code

Parking is handled next door in the Family Dollar lot — there's no dedicated BBQ-shop lot. It's a highway-side counter, so watch for mountain-road speeds coming in and out.

The shop's phone number carries a 215 (Philadelphia) area code. It's consistent across Chamber and in-shop signage, so it's not a typo — likely an owner mobile brought from out of state when the business opened.

Dietary & discounts

In-shop signage advertises a gluten-free-friendly orientation, and the owner is reported to be knowledgeable about ingredients — ask about specific menu items rather than assuming. Obvious carb-forward items (sandwich buns) aren't GF; most of the plate format and sides are workable.

Brother-N-Law offers a military discount. Ask for it at the counter when you order.

Takeout & family tables

Dine-in and takeout are both confirmed. The shop reads as family-friendly — counter-service speed, sides for sharing, and a mission statement that foregrounds community. Server Steven is a long-running presence on the floor per in-shop references; expect first-name-basis if you become a regular.
