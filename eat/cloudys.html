Four categories · everyday essentials

What they sell

Cloudy's describes itself as "Cloudcroft's Neighborhood Grocery," rooted in "quality, community, and everyday essentials for mountain life." The shelves cover the basics most visitors and residents need without driving down to the basin. Specific stock rotates; the official site at cloudysmarket.com and the Facebook page carry current details.

1

Fresh produce

Core category

Fresh fruit and vegetables — the category Cloudcroft Reader survey respondents most asked for, and the one the previous in-village options didn't really cover.

  • Fresh fruit and vegetables
  • Rotating seasonal selection
  • Local and regional growers where available
  • Saves the Alamogordo round trip for the basics

For a kitchenette stay or cabin rental, this is the section that changes the math on cooking in.

2

Meat & deli

Why locals asked for it

Meat and deli items — alongside produce, the second category Reader survey responses identified as the village's biggest gap. Cloudy's stated stock includes both.

  • Fresh meat
  • Deli items
  • Quality, thoughtfully sourced
  • Sized for cabin, condo, and home cooking

Pair a deli pickup with a stop at the brewery or a cookout at one of the Lincoln NF campgrounds.

3

Pantry staples

Forgotten ingredient

The dry goods, basics, and "I forgot the X" items that otherwise mean another trip down US-82 — pantry staples and quality everyday foods.

  • Pantry staples
  • Quality everyday foods
  • Mountain-life essentials
  • Walking distance from much of the village

If your rental's kitchen is mid-trip and you're missing one ingredient, this is the closest answer.

4

Local & regional goods

Vendor-driven

Cloudy's is actively inviting local and regional vendors, producers, growers, and makers. Expect the assortment of New Mexico–made goods to grow as the market settles in.

  • Local and regional vendors
  • New Mexico producers and makers
  • "Quality, community, and everyday essentials"
  • Selection expanding through the first season

Worth a return visit later in the year — the local-vendor shelf is the part most likely to change.

Good for · skip if

What Cloudy's actually solves for, and when a different stop in the village is the right call.

Good for

  • Cabin, condo, or short-term-rental cooks who want to skip the Alamogordo grocery run
  • Visitors who forgot one or two pantry items and don't want to drive 30 miles round trip
  • Picnic and trail lunches built from deli + produce + pantry snacks

Practical notes

A garage-to-grocery rebuild

The space at 102 Mexican Avenue is a former garage that owners Bre and Dylan Kincaid converted into a neighborhood grocery. The Cloudcroft Reader covered the project as it came together; the result is a small, rebuilt market in what had been an unconventional retail footprint.

That history shows up in the room itself — expect a compact store sized for everyday essentials rather than a full supermarket aisle plan.

A long-noted village grocery gap

Before Cloudy's opened, the nearest full grocery store required a roughly 30-mile round trip to Alamogordo and back. The Cloudcroft Reader's spring-2026 survey collected 229 responses from locals and seasonal residents on what they wanted in a village grocery — fresh produce and meat were the two categories that came up most.

Cloudy's launched stocked with both. For visitors, that changes the math on cabin and condo cooking; for residents, it removes a recurring drive.

Hours, opening status & what to confirm

Cloudy's posted starting hours of 10 AM to 6 PM, seven days a week, around its May 2026 soft opening. Earlier Reader reporting noted the hours were subject to change as the store stabilized. The official site at cloudysmarket.com carries the current schedule; for anything time-sensitive, that page or the Facebook page are the surest places to check.

Local vendors & what's still expanding

The market is actively inviting local and regional vendors, producers, growers, and makers. The local-goods shelf is the part of the store most likely to grow over the first season — worth a return visit to see how the New Mexico–made section evolves.

If you make or grow something locally, vendor info is collected via Cloudy's official site.

What we couldn't verify

A few details weren't available from the public sources we checked: a public phone number for the store, an exact full product list and deli menu, and whether posted hours will hold steady through the rest of 2026. We'll update this page as the market shares more.

Editor's take

"Cloudy's Market is the village rebuilding a piece of itself. A former garage, two local owners, fresh produce and meat that locals had been telling the Reader they wanted — and a 30-mile round trip to Alamogordo that no longer has to be the default. Selection will keep filling in through the first season; the local-vendor shelf is the one we're most curious to come back to."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
