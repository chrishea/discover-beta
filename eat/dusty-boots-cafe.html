The menu

Scratch-made café cooking from a 90%+ in-house kitchen (per the official site). Four distinct programs — breakfast, lunch & dinner plates, a real taco program, and a Saturday-only German dinner. Specific items within a category rotate.

1

Breakfast

Until noon Owner's pick

Breakfast is the strongest daily service. The room is regulars-driven in the mornings — local law enforcement and U.S. Forest Service crews show up, not just motel guests.

  • Biscuits & gravy
  • French toast
  • Pancakes
  • Chicken fried steak, two eggs over easy, hash browns, biscuits

Order the chicken fried steak with two eggs over easy, hash browns, and biscuits. That's the working breakfast the regulars are here for.

2

Lunch & dinner

Casual comfort-food plates rotating through daily specials. The menu is built around scratch-made core items, not a chef-driven concept.

  • BLT
  • Patty melt
  • Chicken strips
  • Steak and shrimp
  • Daily specials

Patty melt and a daily special is the working lunch. Ask what the special is before ordering off the menu.

3

Tacos & cantina

Ongoing program

The 2024 "Cafe & Tacos & Cantina" sign isn't signage — it's a real program. Carne asada tacos and beer are part of the kitchen's ongoing direction, confirmed April 2026.

  • Taco platter
  • Carne asada tacos
  • Beer available

Taco platter and a beer is the easiest casual-lunch move on the James Canyon corridor.

4

German Saturday

Saturday only

A weekly fixture (per the official site). The menu list below is what the site highlights — availability may rotate within the program.

  • Schnitzel
  • Sauerbraten
  • Bratwurst
  • German meatballs
  • Red cabbage with apples
  • Spatzel

Order schnitzel or sauerbraten with red cabbage and spatzel. Call ahead — German Saturday draws a crowd.

Practical notes

Hours, seasonality & busy windows

The official site publishes summer hours: daily 7 AM – 9 PM, with breakfast served 7 AM – 12 PM. Off-season hours are not published anywhere authoritative — call ahead for any shoulder-season or winter visit.

Breakfast is the strongest daily window and the most crowded one — the room fills with motel guests, tourists, and working crews between roughly 7:30 and 10. Saturday German dinner is a weekly draw and books the room for the evening.

Service, parking & payment

Table service (confirmed April 2026) in a casual dining room that's tied physically to the motel. On-site parking — it's a highway property, not a walkable Burro Avenue shop. Cards accepted; tipping is standard for table service.

Motel guests receive complimentary room service (per the official site). Public takeout, delivery, and patio seating are not confirmed — ask directly if any of those matter to your visit.

Gluten-free, allergies & alcohol

The Cloudcroft Reader's January 2025 coverage described the kitchen as nut-free with gluten-free options — but confirm directly for serious allergies. Beer is confirmed (official site and Cloudcroft Reader). A broader bar / cantina program is not confirmed by a primary source.

Owner-confirmed details (April 2026)

Direct input from Chris Hea, Cloudcroft local, confirms: the Wiley family owns and operates the property; service is table service; the taco program is real and ongoing (not a signage experiment); breakfast is the strongest daily draw, popular with local law enforcement and U.S. Forest Service crews; the specific recommended order is chicken fried steak with two eggs over easy, hash browns, and biscuits.

Editor's take

"Dusty Boots is easy to miss from the corridor — it's a motel café, after all. But the room has three distinct things none of the other James Canyon Hwy kitchens do: a working-crew breakfast the sheriff and the Forest Service actually eat, a taco program that stuck around, and a German Saturday that gives Cloudcroft an unlikely but genuinely different weekly ritual. The Wiley family has quietly built something more interesting than the signage suggests."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
