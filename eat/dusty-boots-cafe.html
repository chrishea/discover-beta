Scratch-made café cooking from a 90%+ in-house kitchen (per the official site). Four distinct programs — breakfast (served until noon), lunch & dinner plates, a recently added taco cantina and beer menu, and select Saturday German dinners. Specific items within a category rotate.

Breakfast

Breakfast is served until noon. The hiker boot omelette — loaded with veggies — is a fave. Or order the chicken fried steak with two eggs over easy, hash browns, and biscuits — the working breakfast the regulars are here for.

Practical notes

Hours, seasonality & busy windows

The official site publishes summer hours: daily 7 AM – 9 PM, with breakfast served 7 AM – 12 PM. Off-season hours are not published anywhere authoritative — call ahead for any shoulder-season or winter visit.

Breakfast is the strongest daily window and the most crowded one — served until noon, the room fills with motel guests, tourists, and working crews between roughly 7:30 and 10. Select Saturday German dinners are a regular draw and book the room for the evening.

Service, parking & payment

Table service (confirmed April 2026) in a casual dining room that's tied physically to the motel. On-site parking — it's a highway property, not a walkable Burro Avenue shop. Cards accepted; tipping is standard for table service.

Motel guests receive complimentary room service (per the official site). Public takeout, delivery, and patio seating are not confirmed — ask directly if any of those matter to your visit.

Gluten-free, allergies & alcohol

The Cloudcroft Reader's January 2025 coverage described the kitchen as nut-free with gluten-free options — but confirm directly for serious allergies. Beer is confirmed (official site and Cloudcroft Reader). A broader bar / cantina program is not confirmed by a primary source.

Owner-confirmed details (April 2026)

Direct input from Chris Hea, Cloudcroft local, confirms: the Wiley family owns and operates the property; service is table service; the recently added taco cantina and beer menu is part of the kitchen's ongoing direction; breakfast is the strongest daily draw, served until noon, popular with local law enforcement and U.S. Forest Service crews; the specific recommended order is chicken fried steak with two eggs over easy, hash browns, and biscuits, with the hiker boot omelette (loaded with veggies) as the other fave.

Editor's take

"Dusty Boots is easy to miss from the corridor — it's a motel café, after all. But the room has three distinct things none of the other James Canyon Hwy kitchens do: a working-crew breakfast served until noon (the hiker boot omelette, loaded with veggies, is a fave; the sheriff and the Forest Service show up too), a recently added taco cantina and beer menu, and select Saturday German dinners that give Cloudcroft an unlikely but genuinely different ritual. The Wiley family has quietly built something more interesting than the signage suggests."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
