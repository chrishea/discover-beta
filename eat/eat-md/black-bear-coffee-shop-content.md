# Black Bear Coffee Shop

## 1. Executive summary

Black Bear Coffee Shop is the west-end-of-Burro coffee anchor that reads less like a morning-rush espresso counter and more like a linger-and-socialize room. Current sources place it at 200 Burro Ave — the first meaningful stop as you walk onto the strip from the west end — operating with a specialty-coffee program, roasted beans sold for home use, seasonal drinks, and a menu that includes gluten-free, dairy-free, and sugar-free options. Owners Crystal and Nathan Tompkins also run a petite gallery space inside the shop with monthly featured artists; Crystal, a working photographer, displays her own work there as well. April 2026 local confirmation: cookies are on offer, there's an upstairs seating area, and locals treat the shop as a regular gathering place to catch up on town gossip.

## 4. What the coffee shop is

Black Bear reads as more than a basic espresso stop. The website, Instagram feed, and Cloudcroft Reader coverage all point to a shop with a stronger local identity than a generic coffee counter — a specialty-coffee program with seasonal drinks, beans roasted and sold for home brewing, and a steady rotation of featured local artists on the walls. April 2026 local input fills in the rest of the picture: this is a place locals use as a gathering room. The upstairs seating area is specifically where you'd settle in with a friend or a book, and the regulars-on-Burro social layer is real rather than implied.

In practical Burro Avenue terms: Black Bear is the first coffee stop as you walk onto the strip from the west end, and it's the one that rewards sitting down rather than taking the cup with you.

## 6. What I'd order

Three defensible picks using only confirmed items and programs:

1. **A seasonal drink — honey chai or strawberry rose matcha.** This is where the shop shows its character; both are currently on the spring menu per Instagram.
2. **A coffee and a cookie, upstairs.** The owner-confirmed cookies plus the upstairs seating area are the shop's own best argument for staying rather than going.

## 9. Background and history

Crystal and Nathan Tompkins own and operate Black Bear Coffee Shop. Cloudcroft Reader coverage places the shop inside the current downtown business ecosystem as both a coffee stop and a small cultural venue, with monthly featured artists in a petite gallery space. Crystal is a working photographer — Cloudcroft Reader specifically identified her as the shop's October featured artist — and her photography is displayed in the shop alongside the guest rotations.

The roasting and bean-retail program extends the shop beyond Cloudcroft's foot traffic: Cloudcroft Reader has covered ship-home bean availability as part of the town's grocery-and-pantry picture.
