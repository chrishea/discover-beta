# Brother-N-Law BBQ

## 1. Executive summary

Brother-N-Law BBQ is a small Central-Texas-style barbecue stop on James Canyon Highway — a 2021-established pit-and-counter operation at 209 JCH, next door to the Dollar General lot where most guests park. The Cloudcroft Reader / dining-guide picture is that online detail is thin, which matches what you find on the ground: a simple, family-run BBQ counter rather than a polished fine-dining concept. The menu runs on brisket (moist or lean), ribs, house-smoked sausages, sandwiches, and sides, with a separate stated commitment to gluten-free-friendly sides and a military discount. The kitchen is open six days a week (closed Thursday) and, like most BBQ rooms in the region, can sell out before stated closing. Specific current operating details — particularly hours and policies — should be confirmed by phone ahead of a trip.

## 4. What the restaurant is

Brother-N-Law BBQ is the more traditional-BBQ-counter stop on the James Canyon strip — a Central Texas pit kitchen in a town with more diner and café than smokehouse. The carved wood sign out front reads Est. 2021, which frames it as a relatively recent addition to the village's restaurant roster. The dining-guide read is candid: online detail is thin, and the best way to confirm the day's offering is to call.

On the ground it reads as a working pit-and-counter operation. Brisket is the headliner — moist or lean — with ribs, sausage (plain, jalapeño, jalapeño-cheddar), sandwiches, a small specialty list (brisket fries, loaded baked potato, Texas-style chili-no-beans), house-made sides, and banana pudding plus pie or cobbler to close. It's BBQ in the shape locals recognize — not a destination restaurant, but a trip worth making if the category matters.

## 6. What I'd order

Three defensible picks from the confirmed menu:

1. **Sliced moist brisket with mac &amp; cheese and smoked baked beans.** The safest read of a Central Texas BBQ kitchen's hand — brisket done right, two house-made sides that reveal the rest of the program.
2. **Jalapeño-cheddar sausage link sandwich.** The sausage program is the shop's own-make layer, and the jalapeño-cheddar is where the Texas accent shows up loudest.
3. **Brisket fries + banana pudding.** The loaded specialty plate as a shared starter, with the shop's simplest dessert to close. A lunch that doesn't commit you to a full dinner plate.

## 7. Atmosphere and guest experience

The room reads as casual and family-run. The shop's stated mission — "Treat others like family. Serve our community. Make great BBQ." — matches the counter-service, first-name-basis vibe. Regulars-and-server relationships are real: in-shop references identify a long-running server named Steven who remembers guests by name. Parking is usually handled next door in the Dollar General lot.

**Best fit**

- Barbecue travelers who've specifically come for smoked-meat lunch.
- Road-trip parties on James Canyon Highway who want one BBQ stop.
- Diners who want a counter-service plate, not a long sit-down dinner.
- GF drinkers and eaters — the shop is knowledgeable about which items are safe.
- Active-duty or veteran guests — there's a military discount on offer.

**Likely drawbacks**

- Online detail is thin; the posted hours may not reflect same-day reality.
- Like many BBQ shops, the day's inventory can sell out before 6 PM.
- Closed Thursday — a weekly catch if you're only in town mid-week.
- This is a BBQ counter, not a white-tablecloth room — wrong fit for a special-occasion dinner.
