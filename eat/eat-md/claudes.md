## Ownership / people

Cloudy’s Market is being opened by Cloudcroft villagers Bre and Dylan Kincaid. The Cloudcroft Reader reported in January 2026 that the couple planned to open the market next door to Osha Trail Depot on Mexican Avenue.

## Location notes

Cloudy’s Market is at **102 Mexican Ave.**, near Osha Trail Depot and close to Burro Avenue. The Reader described the space as a former garage next door to Osha Trail Depot and near Osha Trail Apartments. A Facebook result from the business also described the location as “just behind Burro.”

## What the store plans to offer

Public descriptions from the business and the Cloudcroft Reader describe Cloudy’s Market as a neighborhood grocery focused on:

- Fresh produce
- Meat
- Deli items
- Pantry staples
- Quality foods
- Thoughtfully sourced goods
- Local and regional vendors, producers, growers, and makers

The business describes itself as “Cloudcroft’s Neighborhood Grocery” and says it is rooted in “quality, community, and everyday essentials for mountain life.”

## Opening timeline

Cloudy’s Market was first announced publicly as “coming Spring 2026.” The Cloudcroft Reader reported that Cloudy’s planned a soft opening for **Friday, May 1, 2026**, with starting hours of **10 a.m. to 6 p.m., seven days a week**, subject to change. The business website now says the market is open daily from 10 a.m. to 6 p.m.

## Community context

Cloudy’s Market fills a long-noted grocery gap in Cloudcroft. In April 2026, the Cloudcroft Reader reported that the nearest full grocery store required a 30-mile round trip to Alamogordo and back, and that Reader survey results showed strong local interest in fresh produce and meat. In another Reader update, 229 locals and seasonal residents responded to a survey about what they wanted in a village grocery store.

## Vendor / hiring notes

The Cloudy’s website says its founding crew has been hired, but the business is still keeping applications on file for future openings. It also invites local and regional producers, growers, and makers to submit vendor information.

## Possible story angles

- **A grocery store returns to the village:** Cloudy’s fills a practical gap for residents, workers, second-home owners, and visitors who otherwise rely on Alamogordo grocery trips.
- **Garage-to-grocery transformation:** Bre and Dylan Kincaid are turning a former garage space on Mexican Avenue into a food market.
- **Local sourcing:** Cloudy’s is actively seeking local and regional vendors, giving New Mexico producers another route to Cloudcroft shoppers.
- **Mountain convenience:** The store may matter most for small, immediate needs: produce, meat, deli items, pantry staples, and forgotten ingredients.
- **Community demand:** The Reader survey response suggests broad interest in a local grocery option.
