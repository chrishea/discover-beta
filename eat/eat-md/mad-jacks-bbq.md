Mad Jack’s Mountaintop Barbecue

The Short Version

Mad Jack’s is a Central Texas-style barbecue joint in the mountains, known for long lines, brisket, ribs, turkey, sausage, cornbread, and a serious “come early or miss out” rhythm. It is best for lunch, not dinner. The official site lists hours as Thursday-Sunday, 11 a.m. until sold out.

What It Is

Mad Jack’s is not trying to be a polished restaurant. It is a destination barbecue joint with a cult following. The official Facebook page calls it a “Central Texas style Barbecue Joint in Cloudcroft NM.”  

That explains the whole experience: smoke, meat by availability, a line that forms early, and food that can run out before posted closing time.

What to Order

Confirmed menu and reporting point to:

* Brisket
* Beef ribs / “Dino rib”
* Pork ribs
* Smoked turkey
* Sausage
* Pulled pork
* Cornbread
* Mac and cheese
* Cobbler
* “Chile the Kid,” described by New Mexico Magazine via NM Gastronome as a green-chile-laced beef sandwich  

MySA reported ordering “a Dinosaur rib, pork ribs, smoked turkey, mac n’ cheese, two pieces of cornbread … and brisket sliced by Jackson himself.”  

The Experience

This is the kind of place where timing matters. MySA reported arriving at 11 a.m. and finding the line “wrapped around the porch and into the neighborhood,” with a possible two-hour wait and a risk of running out of food. The reporter returned another day at 9:47 a.m. for the 11 a.m. opening.  

NM Gastronome described a similar pattern: arriving five minutes before opening and finding “a queue at least 50 deep.” The site warned that on weekends, meats often sell out before 3 p.m.  

That is the useful point for visitors: do not treat Mad Jack’s like a normal restaurant where you show up when hungry. Treat it like a timed event.

What the Press Says

MySA framed the place bluntly: “Texas’ best barbecue joint might be in this tiny New Mexico village.” The story called Mad Jack’s “THE destination” in Cloudcroft and described people lining up “around the block hours before it opens.”  

NM Gastronome quoted New Mexico Magazine writer Cheryl Jamison saying that “anyone truly serious about barbecue should hightail it down to Cloudcroft,” where James Jackson and staff serve “world-class brisket, beef ribs, sausage” and the Chile the Kid sandwich.  

The Cloudcroft Reader reported in March 2026 that Mad Jack’s is “not closing,” even as owner James Jackson works on a Texas Hill Country expansion and the Cloudcroft location remains for sale.  

Ownership and Backstory

Owner James Jackson is central to the Mad Jack’s story. Cloudcroft Reader reported that Jackson put the business and building up for sale in 2024, with a $2.5 million listing, while also describing the restaurant as one of New Mexico’s most recognizable small-town food businesses.  

The Reader later reported that manager Chris Shaw has been with Mad Jack’s for eight years and has been running the operation when Jackson is away. Jackson has been building a second location in Texas near Johnson City, but both Jackson and Shaw told the Reader the Cloudcroft restaurant is not closing.  

Practical Notes

Go early. A safe plan is to arrive before opening, especially on weekends or during tourist season. Ordering late in the day risks a shorter menu or no food. The official hours say “until sold out,” and multiple published accounts confirm that selling out is part of the business model.  

This is best planned as lunch. Do not build a dinner plan around it unless Mad Jack’s posts special hours.
