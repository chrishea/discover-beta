Noisy Water Winery — Cloudcroft

The Short Version

Noisy Water Winery’s Cloudcroft tasting room is the better choice when you want wine, browsing, a low-pressure indoor stop, or an after-lunch drink on Burro Avenue. It is not primarily a restaurant. Think tastings, bottles, events, wine club, and New Mexico-made wine rather than a full meal.

What It Is

Noisy Water is a New Mexico winery that started in Ruidoso and expanded into multiple tasting rooms, including Cloudcroft. Its own site says it produces “New Mexico True” wines using grapes from growing regions across the state, and that the family business includes Mary Jo Piedmont and Jasper Riddle.  

The company presents itself as serious about winemaking but playful in tone. Its site says, “We make wine, grow our grapes & take our craft seriously. But we don’t take much else seriously.”

Specific wines surfaced on the official site include Carignan, Absolution, White Zinfandel, Besito Caliente Green Chile Wine, and Ruidoso Bubbly references elsewhere.  

The Experience

The Cloudcroft location functions as a tasting-room stop on Burro Avenue. The official event page shows Cloudcroft hosting events such as belly dance class, trivia night, and yoga, which suggests the space is used for more than quick tastings.  

The most useful visitor frame: this is a place to sample, sit, shop, and linger. It is a better fit for a couple or group looking for a relaxed stop than for someone looking for a full dinner.

What the Press Says

The U.S. Small Business Administration profiled Noisy Water as a rural business success story, saying Jasper Riddle bought controlling interest in the family company in 2010 and turned “a new, struggling business into a thriving employer in rural New Mexico.”  

The SBA also reported that the company grew from a winery and gift shop with four employees into multiple tasting rooms, including Cloudcroft, plus a production facility in Alto.  

The official Noisy Water site says the company makes “award-winning ‘New Mexico True’ wines from grapes grown across the state.”  

Awards and Background

Noisy Water was founded in Ruidoso, with broader public sources tying the brand to the Riddle family and New Mexico wine. The SBA profile says Jasper Riddle returned to Ruidoso in 2010 and purchased controlling share in the family company.  

A public summary notes that Noisy Water wines won 15 awards at the 2014 Finger Lakes International Wine Competition, including a Double Gold for Ruidoso Bubbly Moscato. Because this came from a secondary summary, verify from the competition or winery before using in formal promotional copy.  

Practical Notes

Use Noisy Water as a flexible stop. It pairs well with walking Burro Avenue, shopping, or killing time before dinner. It is also a safer bad-weather option than an outdoor activity.

The Cloudcroft event calendar matters. Trivia, yoga, or class nights may change the feel of the room. The official page lists trivia every other Tuesday from 6:30-8 p.m. and other Cloudcroft events.
