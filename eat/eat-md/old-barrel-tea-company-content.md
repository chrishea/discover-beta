# Old Barrel Tea Company

## 1. Executive summary

Old Barrel Tea Company is a female-run, family-owned loose-leaf tea business with a Cloudcroft retail location at 505 Burro Ave, Suite 101. It is not a restaurant in the usual sense, but it functions as a meaningful food-and-drink stop in downtown Cloudcroft because it sells tea, honey, spices, wellness products, and related goods in a walkable tourist corridor. In practice, it reads as part specialty tea shop, part gift retail, part Cloudcroft pause point.

## 3. What the restaurant appears to be

Old Barrel Tea Company is better understood as a specialty tea shop than as a restaurant. In Cloudcroft, though, that distinction matters less than it would in a larger town. Burro Avenue visitors often build an afternoon around short stops, and OBTC is one of the stronger stops for people who want a non-coffee drink option and a shop with a distinct product point of view.

Its identity is clearer than many small-town tea stores because the company operates multiple Southwest locations, blends its own teas and spices, and frames itself around wellness as well as flavor.

## 5. Atmosphere and guest experience

The strongest evidence suggests OBTC is a polished specialty-retail stop rather than a place to settle in for a full café experience. It likely appeals most to:
- tea drinkers
- gift buyers
- shoppers looking for local or regional specialty goods
- visitors who want a slower Burro Avenue stop that is not another T-shirt or souvenir store

Because the store sits in the Exchange/Burro Avenue cluster, it also works as part of a broader shopping-and-snacking afternoon.

## 6. Background and history

The company’s own founder-story page says Old Barrel Tea Company was founded in 2015 in Ruidoso, New Mexico. It has since expanded to multiple locations in New Mexico, Colorado, and Arizona. The Cloudcroft store is one of those current locations.

Local coverage adds a small but useful layer:
- During the 2024 Ruidoso fire emergency, Cloudcroft Reader reported that the Cloudcroft location was accepting donations, which suggests a visible community role beyond retail alone.
- The company’s own site and Chamber pages consistently stress family ownership and New Mexico roots.
