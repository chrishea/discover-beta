\f2\fs37\fsmilli18667 \cf0 Get to know Cloudcroft\'92s microbrewery behind the scenes.\'a0
\f3\b\fs28 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f1\b0\fs24 \cf0 \

\f4\fs32 \cf0 BY HANNAH DEAN
\f1\fs24 \
\
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 \cf0 Tucked into a renovated Forest Service tanker bay station that once housed wildland firefighting engines,
\f0\b  Cloudcroft Brewing Company 
\f2\b0 has become a cornerstone of the village's food and drink scene.\'a0
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 With indoor and outdoor seating in a family-friendly atmosphere, CBC offers wood-fired pizza, house-made chips and salsa, soup, and salads alongside their craft beers\'97brews like Railspike Red Ale and Trainwreck IPA. The adjoining Distillery side serves pizza by the slice and craft cocktails in a 21+ setting.
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 But what makes a Cloudcroft beer? To find out, we spent a morning with head brewer 
\f0\b Elliot Bell
\f2\b0  and 
\f0\b Denver Weise
\f2\b0  as they fired up the brew kettle for a batch of brown ale.
\f1\fs24 \
\
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f0\b\fs42\fsmilli21333 \cf0 Challenge of Altitude, Perks of Rainwater
\f3\fs36 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f1\b0\fs24 \cf0 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 \cf0 "The higher you go, the harder it is," Denver explains. At 8,676 feet, brewing in Cloudcroft requires constant vigilance and adaptation. Water boils at a lower temperature here, which means the brewers have to modify nearly every step of the process.
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 "We actually have to dial the fuel down when it starts to get about 190 degrees," Elliot adds, stirring the mash with a long paddle. "Any excuse it has, it'll boil over. And we have to use more hops to get the same effect at altitude because it boils at a lower temperature."
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 For Elliot, who spent 12 years brewing at
\f0\b  Colorado Boy Pub & Brewery
\f2\b0  at 7,000 feet, high-altitude brewing is familiar territory. But Cloudcroft presented new challenges\'97and opportunities. "This is my 15th year brewing commercially," he says. "I've tweaked the recipes here based on my experience. You're always trying to make the beer better."
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 One of Cloudcroft Brewing's most distinctive features is its water source. "We use rainwater to make the beer," Elliot explains, gesturing to a large storage tank. "That's very unique. I've never experienced it before, and it ends up making a better beer because of the water quality."
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 But rainwater presents its own challenge: it contains no minerals. "The rainwater has no minerals in it\'97none," Elliot says. "So we add them back\'97calcium chloride, gypsum, magnesium sulfate, sodium bicarbonate\'97both for the health of the grain to let it convert starch to sugar, and for flavor."
\f1\fs24 \
\
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f0\b\fs42\fsmilli21333 \cf0 From Barley to Beer
\f3\fs36 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f1\b0\fs24 \cf0 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 \cf0 The grain itself tells a story. "Ninety-nine percent of the grain we use is barley," Denver notes, running his hands through bags of malt ranging from pale gold to chocolate brown. "We have chocolate barleys, pale chocolate\'97all presenting different colors and flavors."
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 The roasted grains are particularly aromatic. "They go through a process called kilning," Elliot explains. "They put them in a giant metal drum and heat it. I went to a maltster in Bamberg, Germany\'97the Weyermann company. They've been there hundreds of years, started in someone's garage. Now it takes up three city blocks and provides malt for breweries around the world."
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 When asked about customer favorites, Elliot doesn't hesitate. "The light beers\'97the Cowboy Beer and the American Lager\'97are the most popular. Tourists usually want something light." His own preference? "The American Lager." Denver's pick is their seasonal German Pilsner, available only in summer.
\f1\fs24 \
\
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f0\b\fs42\fsmilli21333 \cf0 The Process: Hurry Up and Wait
\f3\fs36 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f1\b0\fs24 \cf0 \
\pard\pardeftab720\partightenfactor0

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 \cf0 As grain cascades down from the mill upstairs, mixing with hot water in the mash tun, the brewers explain their daily rhythm.
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 The process requires precision and patience. "It's kind of like oatmeal," Elliot observes, checking the consistency. "You don't want it too thick and you don't want it too loose." Temperature is critical\'97too cold and the starches won't convert to sugar; too hot and the beer will lack body and flavor.
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 "A lot of the day is hurry up and wait," Elliot admits.
\f1\fs24 \
\

\f2\fs29\fsmilli14667 As the mash rests and the brewers set their timers, it's clear that making beer at this altitude is equal parts chemistry, art, and mountain adaptation.
