# St. Andrews Bar

## 1. Executive summary

St. Andrews Bar looks worth a stop if you want a classic hotel-bar experience in Cloudcroft rather than a brewery, dive, or quick drink counter. The strongest current evidence shows it operating as **St. Andrew’s Lounge** inside **1899 at The Lodge**, with cocktails, bar food, mountain views, and a direct connection to one of the village’s most historic properties.

This appears to be a better fit for pre-dinner drinks, post-golf drinks, or a slower evening stop than for loud nightlife. It looks more polished than most drink options in town, but still relaxed enough for hotel guests, visitors, and locals who want the Lodge atmosphere without committing to a full dinner in 1899.

## 3. What this place appears to be

St. Andrews appears to be a **historic hotel lounge bar** rather than a standalone nightlife venue. The strongest official description positions it as the lounge inside 1899, where “legends (and golfers) unwind,” with cocktails, bar food, and mountain views.

In practical terms, it looks like:
- a hotel bar with real identity
- a pre-dinner or post-dinner stop
- a historic bar experience tied to the Lodge brand
- a place for cocktails, lighter food, and a slower pace
- more lounge than party scene

It appears to stand out in Cloudcroft because most other drinking options lean more brewery, tasting room, or casual-bar. St. Andrews seems to offer the strongest **classic Lodge atmosphere** in the market.

## 6. How to get there

St. Andrews Bar is inside **The Lodge at Cloudcroft**, at **601 Corona Place**.

From central Cloudcroft:
- head toward the Lodge property on Corona Place
- follow signs for The Lodge at Cloudcroft
- park at the Lodge
- go inside and ask for **St. Andrew’s Lounge** or the lounge inside **1899**

This is easier to find if you already know the Lodge. For first-time visitors, the main goal is simply to reach the hotel; the bar itself is an interior outlet, not a separate roadside business.

What to expect on arrival:
- hotel property parking
- a more destination-style arrival than a main-street storefront
- a Lodge-centered atmosphere rather than a separate street bar
