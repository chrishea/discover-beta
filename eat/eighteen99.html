The menu

Eighteen99 isn't a one-card dinner — the kitchen programs different menus for different nights. Four distinct programs: the chef's dinner, Prime Rib Nights, St. Andrew's Lounge, and reservation logistics. Specific dishes rotate; examples below are from recent coverage, not a fixed menu.

1

Chef's dinner

Menu rotates

Executive Chef Richard Lepree leads a changing dinner menu. The examples below surfaced in recent Cloudcroft Reader coverage — treat them as illustrative of the current direction, not a standing menu. Confirm tonight's card when you book.

  • Ahi ceviche (example · per Cloudcroft Reader, March 2026)
  • Prime New York strip (example · same source)
  • Tuna tartare · Caesar salad · seared short rib · pastry desserts

Book, then ask what's on that night. The menu changes often enough that picking a specific dish ahead of time isn't reliable.

Prime Rib Nights

Owner-confirmed hit

A recurring featured program flagged by locals (April 2026) as a specific draw. Not every Wed–Sat is a Prime Rib Night — call or check with the reservation desk for the current calendar.

  • Featured prime rib program

If you're driving up for one dinner, ask when the next Prime Rib Night is and book that date.

3

St. Andrew's Lounge

Inside the dining room

The Lodge's historic cocktail lounge operates inside the Eighteen99 space. Treat it as part of the reservation experience — a pre-dinner pour in a room with its own identity.

  • Full bar and cocktails
  • Lounge seating inside the restaurant
  • Historic bar identity
  • Part of the evening, not a separate trip

Arrive 30 minutes early for your reservation. Order a cocktail at St. Andrew's; walk to your table when it's ready.

4

Reservations & rhythm

Eighteen99 is reservation-based and has a narrow service window. Walk-ins aren't the expected mode — the kitchen is programming around reservations, not around a broad all-day menu.

  • Wednesday – Saturday only
  • 5:00 – 8:00 PM dinner window
  • Closed Sunday – Tuesday
  • Reserve via the Lodge dining page

Book ahead. The window is short and the room fills, especially on Prime Rib Nights.

Decision aid

Good for · skip if

Eighteen99 is the only special-occasion room in Cloudcroft. The decision isn't between fine-dining options — it's between "one occasion dinner" and something more casual.

Good for

  • Anniversaries, date nights, birthdays — any meal where the occasion matters more than the speed
  • Lodge guests who want one destination dinner on-property
  • Travelers who want one white-tablecloth night in a trip otherwise built around cafés and BBQ
  • Planning around a Prime Rib Night
  • People who enjoy the pre-dinner cocktail ritual at St. Andrew's Lounge

Skip if

  • You want a walkable Burro Avenue dinner — go to Dave's, Western Bar, or Cloudcroft Brewery
  • You want dedicated BBQ — Mad Jack's (Thu–Sun) or Brother-N-Law
  • You want a casual weekday or a Sunday dinner — the room is closed Sun–Tue
  • You want a broad all-day menu — this is a 3-hour evening window only
  • You want late-night dining — service ends at 8 PM

Practical notes

Reservations, timing & Prime Rib Night planning

Reservations are expected — Eighteen99 isn't a walk-in room. Book through the official Lodge dining page or by phone. The 3-hour service window fills fast, especially on weekends.

If a specific Prime Rib Night is the reason you're going, call the restaurant directly at (575) 682-2098 and ask for the next scheduled date. Build the trip around that reservation.

Dress, atmosphere & service

This is the only room in Cloudcroft where "dress up" is a reasonable instinct. It's not strictly enforced — a mountain town is a mountain town — but the atmosphere expects some intent. Service and kitchen output have meaningfully improved under the current operation (confirmed April 2026, local).

St. Andrew's Lounge inside the restaurant is the pre-dinner spot. Arrive 30 minutes early and order a cocktail.

Menu rotation & dietary notes

The kitchen programs different menus for different nights — the Wednesday card may not match the Saturday one. Specific dishes from recent coverage (ahi ceviche, prime New York strip, tuna tartare, Caesar, seared short rib) are illustrative of the chef's direction, not a fixed list.

Dietary accommodations aren't documented publicly — call ahead for allergies or specific needs.

Lodging, parking & The Lodge experience

The restaurant is inside The Lodge at Cloudcroft. On-site parking is easy. Lodge guests have the shortest walk (and the most reliable follow-on experience) — the stay-and-dine combination is the full Eighteen99 case.

Non-guests are welcome. Follow Corona Place signs to the main hotel building.
