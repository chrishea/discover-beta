Practical notes

Hours, seasons & calling ahead

Posted hours are Monday through Saturday, 7 AM to 5 PM. Closed Sunday. Older Cloudcroft Reader coverage flagged the early weekday open as part of the bakery's role in local routine, and the current village dining listings still hold the 7 AM to 5 PM, Monday–Saturday line.

Hours can shift in shoulder season. Before any off-season weekday drive up the hill, a quick call to (575) 682-2712 or a glance at the bakery's Facebook page or Instagram confirms the day.

Walk-in case vs. custom-cake side

KennaBelles runs as a walk-in bakery. You can step in off Burro Avenue, look at the case, and leave with pastries, cookies, cupcakes, or a slice. That's the daily mode, and it's how most locals use it.

The custom-cake side is separate. The village listings call out custom cakes and orders specifically, which means birthdays, weddings, and event cakes are part of what the bakery does. Plan a few days out and call to set timing — same-day cake work isn't guaranteed.

Owner & the pink storefront

Owner-baker Kenna Leonard runs the bakery, confirmed as still operating it as of April 2026 by local input. The shop has been a daily fixture in Cloudcroft for years — Cloudcroft Reader's 2023 feature called it part of the local morning routine, and the 2025 grocery-and-pantry roundup still names it as one of the village's core bakery options.

The pink front is the navigation cue. The village dining listings tell visitors to follow their nose to the pink patisserie at 308 Burro Ave, and that holds — once you're on the boardwalk, the storefront does the work for you.

Gluten-free, the menu, and what's confirmed

Gluten-free options are on the village dining listings. The full daily list rotates, so the answer at the counter on a given day is the answer that matters — ask when you order.

Menu items confirmed for 2026: pastries, cookies, cupcakes, croissants, kolaches, and fresh-baked pies. Cinnamon rolls and pistachio bread show up consistently in editorial and traveler coverage. Daily availability for any specific item should be treated as not guaranteed — bakery cases shift by what's coming out of the oven that morning.

Coffee & what kind of stop this is

KennaBelles serves coffee alongside the bakery items, but it's a bakery first — not a specialty-coffee program. If you want a full barista bar with seasonal drinks, Black Bear Coffee is three doors west at 200 Burro. If you want pastry and a workable cup, KennaBelles handles both inside one stop.

This is a quick-stop bakery. There's not a sit-down dining room built around long stays. Most visitors box up their order or eat on the boardwalk.
