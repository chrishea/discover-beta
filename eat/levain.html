Four categories · rotates

The menu

Sourdough is the center of the business. Around it, the bakery rotates pastries, danishes, and sweets, with the exact lineup shifting week to week. Follow Levain It Up on Facebook for the current drop.

1

Sourdough loaves

House specialty

The reason the bakery exists. Naturally leavened bread, baked in small batches and sold while it lasts.

  • Sourdough loaves
  • Small-batch, naturally leavened
  • Bake schedule tied to the Fri–Sun window
  • Buy a loaf, take it home

A loaf is the move. That's what the bakery is built around, and it's what locals stop for.

2

Danishes & pastries

Rotates weekly

Danishes are confirmed on the bakery's own Facebook posts. Specific flavors rotate, so you'll see whatever Garrett and Steven baked that morning.

  • Danishes (current Facebook posts)
  • Rotating pastry case
  • Whatever's fresh that day
  • First-come, first-served

If a danish looks good in the case, take one. You won't see the same flavor next weekend.

3

Sweets & bagels

Varies · ask in shop

Bagels and sweets show up in community references and gift-card mentions, but the day-to-day lineup isn't fixed. Treat anything beyond sourdough and danishes as a bonus.

  • Bagels (community references)
  • Sweets & baked goods
  • Subject to what's in the case
  • Not a confirmed standing menu

Ask what's out today. The honest answer is whatever Garrett and Steven baked.

4

The grab-and-go service

Walk-up only

Pull over, park, step inside, order, walk out. No table service, no long stay. The shop is built for the in-and-out transaction, not a café visit.

  • Walk-up counter
  • No sit-down café
  • Quick pickup, then back on the road
  • Pair with a coffee from up the hill

Treat it like a roadside bread stop. That's what it is, and that's what works.

Where you'll find it

Levain isn't on Burro Avenue. It's on US Highway 82, the road between Alamogordo and Cloudcroft, in the small mountain community of High Rolls. From the village, you drop down US 82 toward the basin for about ten minutes; the bakery sits right on the highway, near the High Rolls General Store at 845 US 82.

Practical notes

Hours, sell-outs & calling ahead

Posted hours are Friday, Saturday, and Sunday from 2 PM to 6 PM. Closed Monday through Thursday. Both Cloudcroft Reader and the bakery's Facebook page report the same window, so if you see those four hours, that's the bake.

Small-batch bakeries can sell out before posted close. If you're driving from out of town for a specific loaf or pastry, a quick call to (575) 520-8096 or a glance at the most recent Levain It Up Facebook post is the safest play.

Walk-up service, not a café

The owner-confirmed service model is walk-up only. You park, step inside, order, get your food, and head out. No table service, no extended café session, no espresso bar.

Plan it like a roadside bread pickup, then bring the goods back to the cabin or pair them with a coffee from a Cloudcroft shop on the way up or down the mountain.

The owners & the building

Levain It Up is run by Garrett Barnett and Steven Jackson. Cloudcroft Reader's January 2026 profile, "Rising to the Occasion: Levain It Up in High Rolls," frames them as transforming both the old building and their own lives through the bakery. As of April 2026, both are still running it.

That makes Levain one of the newer food businesses in the greater Cloudcroft market — closer in vintage to the recent wave of village openings than to the long-running Burro Avenue institutions.

Why it's a High Rolls stop, not a Cloudcroft one

High Rolls is the small mountain community on US 82 between Alamogordo and Cloudcroft, about 10 minutes down the mountain from the village. Levain belongs to that broader US 82 corridor rather than the in-village Burro Avenue or James Canyon Highway dining strips.

If you're already on US 82 — heading up from White Sands (35 miles away), or driving down toward the basin — Levain is on your route. If you're staying in Cloudcroft proper at 8,676 feet and don't plan to leave the village, you'll need to make a dedicated trip.

Online sales, website & ordering ahead

No standalone website is confirmed. Facebook is the bakery's primary online presence; Instagram (@levain.it.up) is also live. Online sales aren't confirmed, so plan to buy in person during the Friday–Sunday afternoon window.

If you want a specific item held, a phone call to (575) 520-8096 during open hours is your best bet. The bakery hasn't published a formal pre-order system.

Editor's take

"Levain is a roadside bakery in the best sense of the phrase. Small, specific, sourdough is the whole point, and the limited Friday-through-Sunday window keeps the operation honest. If you're driving US 82 on a weekend afternoon, it's worth pulling over for a loaf. If you're in the village all week, plan the trip — the bread is good enough that the ten-minute drive down the mountain isn't really a debate."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
