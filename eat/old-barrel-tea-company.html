Four categories · brewed + retail

What they sell

The drink program is small and rotating: house teas brewed to order, hot or iced. The retail side is the real depth — loose-leaf tins, raw honey jarred at the company's Albuquerque facility, gourmet herbs and spices, wellness goods, and brewing tools. Follow @oldbarrelteaco for current blends.

House-blend teas, brewed

House blend

A small, rotating menu of house teas made to order — hot or iced. The "sit and sip" part of the visit.

  • Loose-leaf teas brewed to order
  • Hot or iced
  • Rotating selection
  • Sit-and-sip at the coffee-table nook

Pick a house blend, take it to the reading nook, and grab a Mountain Monthly off the table.

Loose-leaf retail

Take home

The same teas the company blends in-house, sold by the tin. The point of the trip if you actually drink tea at home.

  • House-blended loose-leaf teas
  • Brewing tools and tea sets
  • Mexican vanilla bean products
  • Sold in-shop and online

Ask the staff for a recommendation based on what you ordered to drink — the easiest way in.

Honey, spices & provisions

NM-jarred

The provisions side is what separates this shop from the rest of Burro. Raw honey is jarred at the company's Albuquerque facility.

  • Raw and unfiltered honey
  • Gourmet herbs and spices
  • Mexican vanilla bean products
  • Pantry-grade goods

A jar of NM honey is the gift to bring back home — small, packs flat, distinctly local.

If wellness retail isn't your thing, skip this section — but the elixirs are why some regulars come back.

Practical notes

Tea shop, not a restaurant

The honest framing: this is a specialty tea and provisions shop with a small brewed-drink program, not a café you build a meal around. The drink menu is rotating teas, hot or iced, made to order. There is no kitchen and no pastry case.

If you want a meal on Burro, the building also holds Cloudcroft Sandwich Shop. You've got Dusty Boots, Dave's, and Cloudcroft Brewing within a short walk.

Hours, seasons & calling ahead

Posted hours per the local dining listings are Monday through Friday 11 AM to 5 PM, Saturday 10 AM to 6 PM, and Sunday 10 AM to 5 PM. Open seven days.

Hours can shift around holidays and shoulder seasons. A quick call to (575) 682-7474 or a check of the Facebook page is the surest way to confirm before driving up.

The reading nook & sitting awhile

The shop has a small coffee-table seating spot inside meant for sitting with a tea and a magazine. The Mountain Monthly is on the table. It's a quieter break than the boardwalk benches, and it works as a 10-to-15-minute reset between Burro Ave stops.

This isn't a long-haul work café. There's no full menu, no laptop culture, and the space is small. Treat it as a pause point, not a base camp.

Family-owned, founded 2015

Old Barrel Tea Company was founded in 2015 in Ruidoso, New Mexico, and has since opened locations across New Mexico, Colorado, and Arizona. The company blends its own teas and spices, and jars its raw honey at its Albuquerque facility.

During the 2024 Ruidoso fires, Cloudcroft Reader noted that the Cloudcroft store was accepting donations for affected residents. A small but real signal that the shop participates in the village beyond retail.

What's in the building & parking

The Burro Street Exchange holds Old Barrel Tea Co. (Ste 101), Cloudcroft Sandwich Shop, and Noisy Water Winery, plus a few retail tenants. It's a one-stop for a tea, a sandwich, and a glass of wine without moving the car.

Parking is along Burro Avenue. Weekends in summer and during festival days fill up early, so park where you can and walk the strip. Cloudcroft sits at 8,676 ft, so take it easy on the first walk if you've come up from the desert.

Editor's take

"Old Barrel reads as a specialty retail stop with a small drink program attached, not a tea café. That's a feature, not a bug. The brewed tea gets you in the door; the loose-leaf wall, raw NM honey, and spice shelves are why you actually leave with a bag. On a Burro Avenue afternoon, it's the cleanest 15-minute stop on the boardwalk, and the gift you bring home from it is small, local, and shelf-stable."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
