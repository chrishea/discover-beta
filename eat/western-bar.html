Decision aid

Good for · skip if

What this room does well, and when a different Burro Avenue or JCH stop is the better move.

Good for

  • A green chile cheeseburger and a beer in the dark
  • Ribeye or New York strip on a sit-down night
  • A late lunch or post-hike dinner — six days a week, until 10 PM
  • Pool, dominoes, jukebox music, and a casual local-bar feel
  • Mexican plates from a kitchen that takes them seriously
  • Take-home from the package-store side

Skip if

  • It’s Wednesday — the bar is closed
  • You want breakfast — try Big Daddy’s on JCH or Dusty Boots Café
  • You came specifically for craft beer with food — Cloudcroft Brewery is up Burro
  • You want fine dining — Eighteen99 at The Lodge is the dinner-reservation room
  • You prefer a polished restaurant feel — this is a dive bar by choice

Practical notes

Hours and the Wednesday closure

The Western runs Thursday through Tuesday, 11:00 a.m. to 10:00 p.m. Closed Wednesdays. That schedule shows up on the official directory listing and is the version to plan around — six days a week, with the bar going through close.

Off-season hours can shift on short notice. If a specific night matters, call (575) 682-2445 before driving down.

Cash, parking, and payment

Cash-only, with street parking. Bring cash to be safe; ask at the bar if you’d like to use a card.

Pets and family-friendly notes

Pet-friendly. Kid-friendly, casual attire, no reservations. Standard dive-bar etiquette applies.

Dine-in & takeout

Both dine-in and takeout. Walk in, sit at the bar or grab a booth, or call (575) 682-2445 to place a to-go order on the way down.

Pool, dominoes, and the room itself

Pool tables, a digital jukebox, dollar bills on the walls, and a classic local-bar feel. Play pool or dominoes while you wait. The Western reads exactly like a long-running mountain-town dive bar that happens to have a kitchen and a package store next door.
