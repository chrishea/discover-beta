## Cloudcroft 2026 events, visitor version

### May

**Mayfair Juried Artshow**  
**May 23–24, 2026**  
Best for: art buyers, casual browsers, couples, weekend visitors  
This is the first strong spring event on the 2026 Chamber calendar. It is a good reason to come up before the heavier summer crowds. Pair it with Burro Avenue shopping and a slow lunch in town.

### June

**BeerFest**  
**June 13–14, 2026**  
Best for: adults, groups, summer-weekend visitors  
This looks like one of the bigger early-summer social weekends. It is a good fit if you want Cloudcroft at its more festive and crowded. Expect a stronger weekend-town atmosphere than on a quiet weekday.

**Trails & Rails**  
**June 13, 2026**  
Best for: runners, active visitors, race-weekend travelers  
This is one of the better reasons to visit if you want an outdoor weekend built around the trestle area and Cloudcroft trail culture. It adds real event energy without requiring a full ultra-distance commitment.

### July

**Christmas in July**  
**July 11–12, 2026**  
Best for: families, first-time visitors, people who want classic Cloudcroft summer atmosphere  
This is one of the more recognizable Cloudcroft weekends. It leans into the town’s cool-weather identity and tends to work well for visitors who want a broad “mountain town in summer” experience rather than one niche activity.

### August

**Art and Wine in the Cool Pines**  
**August 7–9, 2026**  
Best for: adults, art shoppers, couples, repeat visitors  
This is one of the strongest event weekends if you want Cloudcroft at its more polished and browse-friendly. It pairs naturally with galleries, shops, and slower in-town time.

**Cloudcroft Ultra**  
**August 15, 2026**  
Best for: trail runners, race crews, strong outdoor travelers  
This is one of the most serious athletic events on the calendar. Even if you are not racing, it is a good weekend for people who like trail-running culture and an active town feel.

### September

**Labor Day Hoopla**  
**September 5–6, 2026**  
Best for: holiday-weekend visitors, families, people who want an easy festival weekend  
This is one of the most practical event weekends for visitors because Labor Day already gives many people time to travel. Expect more town activity and less quiet than on a normal September weekend.

### October

**Cactus to Cloud Sky Race**  
**October 17, 2026**  
Best for: serious runners, ambitious outdoor visitors, shoulder-season travelers  
This is the strongest October event I could verify. It is more specialized than the broad Chamber festival weekends, but it is a real draw if you like high-effort race events and cooler fall conditions.

### November

**Christmas Market**  
**November 27–28, 2026**  
Best for: holiday shoppers, families, visitors who like early Christmas atmosphere  
This is the cleanest late-November event on the Chamber calendar. It is a good fit if you want Cloudcroft leaning into holiday mode without waiting for mid-December.

### December

**Mountain Christmas Village**  
**December 11–19, 2026**  
Best for: families, holiday travelers, people who want Cloudcroft at its most seasonal  
This is the marquee Christmas-period event block. If you want the strongest holiday feel in Cloudcroft, this is the one to plan around. It is also one of the better reasons to book lodging early.

## Best event weekends by visitor type

**Best for first-time visitors**  
Christmas in July, Labor Day Hoopla, Mountain Christmas Village. These give the broadest Cloudcroft feel and the least specialized barrier to entry.

**Best for couples**  
Art and Wine in the Cool Pines, Mayfair Juried Artshow, Christmas Market. These work better for slower shopping, strolling, and dinner-centered weekends.

**Best for families**  
Christmas in July, Labor Day Hoopla, Mountain Christmas Village. These are the safest “there will be enough going on” answers.

**Best for runners**  
Trails & Rails, Cloudcroft Ultra, Cactus to Cloud Sky Race. These are the clearest race-centered reasons to plan a trip.

**Best if you want the town lively but not necessarily packed with one niche crowd**  
Christmas in July and Labor Day Hoopla.

## Practical planning notes

The Chamber calendar is the strongest current source for the main 2026 festival lineup. Race weekends are better confirmed through their live registration pages. Event details can still shift, so it is smart to recheck dates, times, and whether anything requires tickets or advance registration before you book a room.

One caution: Chamber social posts also point to an **Ugly Sweater** run/walk in 2026, but I did not find a stronger dedicated event page for it, so I would treat that as **appears current** rather than locked.
