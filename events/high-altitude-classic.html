About the Race

Ride at Altitude

The High Altitude Classic is recognized as one of the most scenic and demanding cycling events in the Southwest. Riders tackle challenging mountain roads through the Sacramento Mountains, climbing to nearly 9,000 feet through pine forests and past dramatic switchbacks.

With Classic, Half, and Junior categories, there is a course for every level of cyclist. The race starts at 7:00 AM, and packet pickup is available the day before. Awards are presented at Zenith Park following the race.

Race Categories

Choose the course that matches your skill level. Every category offers a challenging and scenic mountain ride.

🥇

Classic

The full course for experienced riders. Tackle the complete mountain route with significant elevation gain through the Sacramento Mountains. This is the flagship event and the ultimate test of endurance at altitude.

🥈

Half Classic

A shorter but still demanding route covering roughly half the Classic distance. Perfect for intermediate riders who want the mountain cycling experience without the full course commitment.

🥉

Junior

A course designed for younger riders to experience competitive mountain cycling. A great introduction to altitude riding in a supportive, family-friendly environment.

Route Highlights

What makes this one of the most talked-about cycling events in the region.

Ride at nearly 9,000 feet through the Sacramento Mountains. The thin air adds an extra challenge that sets this race apart from others.

Plan Your Race

Race Details

Everything you need to know to prepare for race day.

🕐

Race Start

7:00 AM start time for all categories. Arrive early for check-in and warm-up. Staggered starts may apply by category.

📦

Packet Pickup

Packet pickup is available the day before race day. Pick up your race number, timing chip, and event materials in advance.

🏆

Awards

Awards ceremony held at Zenith Park following the race. Category winners and overall champions recognized with prizes.

🚗

Getting There

Cloudcroft is 30 minutes from Alamogordo on US-82. Book lodging early as race weekend fills up quickly. El Paso airport is 2 hours away.
