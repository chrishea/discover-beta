About the Event

A Mountain Tradition

Cloudcroft Lumberjack Day celebrates the village's rich timber heritage with a full day of lumberjack competitions, family fun, and mountain hospitality. Held each September at Zenith Park, this free event draws competitors and spectators from across the region.

The day kicks off with a hearty pancake breakfast from 7:30 to 9:30 AM, followed by competitions starting at 9:00 AM. Watch skilled lumberjacks compete in axe throwing, chainsaw events, single buck sawing, and more — with both men's and women's divisions.

Day of Event

Event Schedule

From breakfast to the final competition, here is how the day unfolds at Zenith Park.

7:30 AM – 9:30 AM

Pancake Breakfast

Start the day with a hearty all-you-can-eat pancake breakfast. Fuel up before the competitions begin.

9:00 AM

Competitions Begin

Lumberjack competitions kick off with events running throughout the day. Men's and women's divisions compete for top honors.

All Day

Vendors & Activities

Browse food vendors, craft booths, and family activities. There is something for everyone throughout the day at Zenith Park.

Compete

Competition Events

Watch skilled competitors battle it out in these classic lumberjack disciplines. Men's and women's divisions.

🪣

Axe Throwing

Test your aim in this crowd favorite. Competitors throw axes at targets for points in both men's and women's categories.

🪚

Chainsaw Events

Speed and precision meet in the chainsaw competition. Watch competitors race through logs with impressive skill.

🪵

Single Buck Sawing

A classic test of endurance and technique. Competitors use a one-person crosscut saw to slice through timber.

🏋️

Men's Division

Men compete across all events for individual and overall honors. Experience levels range from novice to professional.

💪

Women's Division

Women compete in all events with their own division and awards. Fierce competition and incredible displays of skill.

🏆

Awards Ceremony

Top finishers in each event and overall champions are recognized at the awards ceremony at the end of the day.
