If I publish mountainmonthly.com and also discovercloudcroft.com how do I maximize their search engine optimization 

You’re running two domains. Treat them as distinct assets with clear roles — not competitors.

1. Define the lanes

MountainMonthly.com
Primary: journalism, features, long-form stories, authority.
SEO focus: branded search, local news, civic issues, profiles, opinion, history.

DiscoverCloudcroft.com
Primary: tourism, itineraries, lodging, dining, events, guides.
SEO focus: visitor intent, trip planning, “things to do,” seasonal searches.

Do not duplicate content. Do not mirror pages. Google will split authority or suppress one.



2. Build a keyword map before you publish

Create a spreadsheet with:
	•	Primary keyword
	•	Search intent (news / informational / commercial / navigational)
	•	Target URL
	•	Supporting internal links

Example:
	•	“Things to do in Cloudcroft” → DiscoverCloudcroft homepage
	•	“Cloudcroft Village Council meeting recap” → MountainMonthly article
	•	“Cloudcroft hiking trails” → DiscoverCloudcroft guide
	•	“Cloudcroft water issues” → MountainMonthly reporting

Each high-value phrase gets one clear owner.



3. Structure both sites for topical authority

Mountain Monthly

Organize by topic silos:
	•	Government
	•	Business
	•	Tourism economy
	•	Public safety
	•	History

Each silo should:
	•	Have a pillar page
	•	Link to related articles
	•	Link back up to the pillar

Discover Cloudcroft

Organize by visitor intent:
	•	Stay
	•	Eat
	•	Shop
	•	Events
	•	Outdoor
	•	Seasonal Guides

Each should have:
	•	A strong landing page (800–1,500 words)
	•	Structured subpages
	•	Internal links across categories



4. Cross-link strategically (not randomly)

From Mountain Monthly:
	•	Link to DiscoverCloudcroft guides when relevant
(“See our complete hiking guide here.”)

From DiscoverCloudcroft:
	•	Link to MountainMonthly reporting for authority
(“Read our in-depth coverage of the wildfire response.”)

Use natural anchor text. Avoid keyword stuffing.



5. Technical fundamentals (non-negotiable)

For both domains:
	•	Fast hosting (Core Web Vitals matter)
	•	Mobile-first design
	•	Clean URL structure
	•	XML sitemap
	•	Google Search Console for each domain
	•	Schema markup:
	•	NewsArticle (Mountain Monthly)
	•	LocalBusiness, Event, FAQ (Discover Cloudcroft)
	•	Proper canonical tags



6. Own seasonal search

Cloudcroft is seasonal. Build content 60–90 days ahead of demand.

Examples:
	•	“Cloudcroft fall colors 2026”
	•	“Best snow weekends in Cloudcroft”
	•	“Fourth of July in Cloudcroft”
	•	“Summer festivals in the Sacramento Mountains”

Update annually. Keep URLs constant. Refresh content, don’t replace.



7. Build backlinks differently for each

Mountain Monthly
	•	Journalism earns links.
	•	Pitch stories to regional media.
	•	Be cited in state coverage.
	•	Publish data-driven pieces.

Discover Cloudcroft
	•	Get links from:
	•	Lodging websites
	•	Real estate agents
	•	Regional tourism blogs
	•	Chamber pages
	•	Event organizers

Backlinks should match the domain’s purpose.



8. Capture search intent at every stage

DiscoverCloudcroft.com should rank for:
	•	“Where to stay in Cloudcroft”
	•	“Best cabins in Cloudcroft”
	•	“Cloudcroft NM weekend trip”

MountainMonthly.com should rank for:
	•	“Cloudcroft news”
	•	“Cloudcroft Village budget”
	•	“Otero County water issues”

Different intent. Different structure.



9. Build email capture into SEO

Every high-ranking page should:
	•	Offer a newsletter signup
	•	Offer a free guide download
	•	Capture first-party data

Search traffic is rented. Email is owned.

You already understand this model from the Cloudcroft Reader. Apply it to both.



10. Avoid cannibalization

Before publishing any page, ask:
	•	Does this topic already exist on the other domain?
	•	Which domain has the stronger authority for this subject?
	•	Will this page compete internally?

If yes, consolidate.



11. Consider a strategic hierarchy

If you want maximum long-term authority:

Option A (separate brands, equal weight)
Keep both independent.

Option B (authority stack)
Make Mountain Monthly the journalistic authority.
Make Discover Cloudcroft the commercial tourism arm.
Use Mountain Monthly to generate backlinks and authority that pass to Discover via internal linking.

Option B is stronger long term.



Direct Answer

To maximize SEO across both:
	1.	Assign clear topic ownership.
	2.	Build structured silos.
	3.	Avoid duplicate content.
	4.	Cross-link intentionally.
	5.	Publish seasonal evergreen content.
	6.	Earn backlinks that match site purpose.
	7.	Capture email traffic from search visitors.
	8.	Track performance separately in Search Console.



High-Level Reasoning

Google rewards clarity, authority, and topical depth. Two domains can work — but only if each has a defined purpose and does not compete with the other. Structure and discipline matter more than volume.
