FAQ · 2026

The questions visitors actually ask.

Getting up the mountain, when to come, where to stay, and what to know about altitude, bears, and the drive.

Getting there

The drive up the mountain

Cloudcroft sits at 8,676 feet on US 82 in the Sacramento Mountains of southern New Mexico. Here's what to know before the drive.

How do I get to Cloudcroft?

From Alamogordo, take US 82 east — about 22 miles up the mountain, gaining roughly 4,300 feet. From El Paso it's about 100 miles and 2 hours. From Albuquerque, plan on around 210 miles and 3.5 hours.

The nearest commercial airports are El Paso International (ELP) and Albuquerque Sunport (ABQ). There's no public transit up the mountain, so you'll need a vehicle.

Is the drive up hard?

US 82 is a paved two-lane highway with switchbacks and the well-known S-Curve near the top. It's not technical — just steady climbing with a few tight bends and a long descending grade if you're heading back down.

Drive it in daylight if you can, slow down for fog and ice in winter, and expect mountain weather to change fast. Check nmroads.com in winter before you leave.

Do I need a 4WD vehicle?

Not for the village itself. US 82 is paved year-round and most lodging is reachable in any car. 4WD or AWD helps for forest-road exploring, big snow days, and back-mountain cabins on dirt access roads.

If you're booking a remote cabin in winter, ask the host about the last quarter-mile of road before you commit to a sedan.

When to visit

Seasons, weather, and timing

What's the weather like?

High alpine, four real seasons. Summer highs are in the 70s°F while the Tularosa Basin below sits over 100°F — that 20-degree drop is most of the reason people come up.

Winters bring snow, ice, and below-freezing nights. Spring and fall are the most variable: a blue-sky 65°F afternoon can drop into the 20s after sundown. Pack layers regardless of season.

When is the best time to come?

June–August for the cool-summer escape — peak season, book early. October for fall color in the aspens. December for the Mountain Christmas Village weekends and snow.

Spring is the quiet season. Fewer crowds, but the weather is less reliable — expect a mix of warm afternoons, late snow, and windy days.

What's open in the off-season?

Most lodgings stay open year-round. Some restaurants and shops run reduced hours from November through April, especially mid-week, and a few close Mondays and Tuesdays altogether.

Always call ahead for specific places before driving up — schedules in a small mountain town change with the weather.

Stay, eat & do

Trip planning basics

How many days should I stay?

Two nights gets you settled and out on a trail. Three or four is the sweet spot for a real mountain reset — long enough to acclimate to the altitude, eat through the village, and add a side trip like White Sands (35 miles down the mountain).

Where should I eat?

Popular anchors: Mad Jack's BBQ, Black Bear Coffee, Dave's Cafe, Big Daddy's Diner, Cloudcroft Brewery, and the Lodge dining room (Eighteen99).

Where should I stay?

The Lodge for a historic resort experience. The Crofting Inn for B&B. Spruce Cabins for cabin life. Dusty Boots or Summit Inn for budget and practical.

Mountain logistics

Altitude, bears, and the small stuff

Do I need to worry about altitude?

At 8,676 feet, sea-level visitors usually notice it on day one — slower runs on the trail, easier breath fatigue walking the village, more thirst, sometimes a mild headache the first night.

Drink water, take it easy the first 24 hours, and limit alcohol the first night. Most people adjust within a day or two.

Are there bears?

Yes. The Sacramento Mountains have a healthy black bear population. They mostly avoid people, but they will absolutely investigate an unattended cooler.

Don't leave food, trash, or coolers outside — especially at cabins. Store everything indoors or in a hard-sided vehicle overnight, and use bear-resistant trash bins where they're provided.

What's cell service like?

Spotty in the village and weaker on forest roads. Verizon and AT&T tend to be the most usable carriers in the village core; service drops off quickly past the village limits.

Most lodgings have Wi-Fi — assume you'll be relying on it for anything important, and download maps before you head out on a trail.

Is there a hospital in Cloudcroft?

No hospital in the village. Cloudcroft has volunteer fire and EMS for emergencies. The nearest hospital is Gerald Champion Regional Medical Center in Alamogordo, about 30 minutes down the mountain. For life-threatening emergencies, call 911.
