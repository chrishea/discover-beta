Season Overview

Your Mountain Escape

Summer is Cloudcroft's peak season, and for good reason. When El Paso and Las Cruces swelter in 100°+ heat, Cloudcroft's temperatures hover in the comfortable 70s and 80s — perfect for hiking, biking, golfing, and simply enjoying the outdoors.

The village comes alive with festivals, outdoor concerts, and visitors seeking refuge from the lowland heat. With over 50 miles of trails, dark sky stargazing, and trout fishing nearby, every day offers a new adventure. Book early — this is our most popular time of year!

Summer Activities

Things to Do This Season

From dawn hikes to stargazing at midnight, summer in Cloudcroft is packed with adventure.

🥾

Hiking & Trail Running

Over 50 miles of trails wind through Lincoln National Forest. The Rim Trail, Osha Trail, and Willie White Loop offer stunning views and cool forest shade.

🌌

Dark Sky Stargazing

Summer's clear skies and certified dark sky status make Cloudcroft a premier destination for stargazing. The Milky Way is spectacular here.

🎣

Fishing

Cast a line at Silver Lake or La Junta Creek for rainbow trout. Mountain streams and stocked lakes offer excellent summer fishing for all skill levels.

⛳

High-Altitude Golf

Play one of America's highest courses at The Lodge. At 9,000 feet, your drives fly farther and the views are unforgettable.

🚴

Mountain Biking

From beginner-friendly forest roads to challenging single track, Cloudcroft offers riding for every skill level in cool mountain temperatures.

🎶

Live Music & Events

Summer brings outdoor concerts, art walks, and community events nearly every weekend. Check the events calendar for what's happening.

What to Expect

Summer Weather

Sunny mornings, monsoon afternoons, and cool mountain nights.

☀️

Sunny Mornings

Most summer days start sunny and clear. Get your outdoor activities in during the morning hours for the best weather and comfortable temperatures.

⛈️

Monsoon Afternoons

July and August bring the monsoon season. Expect brief but intense afternoon thunderstorms — they usually clear by evening for beautiful sunsets.

🌙

Cool Nights

Even in summer, nights are cool in the mountains at 45–55°F. Bring a light jacket for evening activities and enjoy sleeping with windows open.

Summer Events

Celebrate the Season

Festivals, markets, and community celebrations all summer long.

July 4th

Independence Day Celebration

Small-town Americana at its finest. Parade down Burro Avenue, community picnic, live music, and fireworks over the mountains.

July

Moonlight Forest Run

Experience the magic of running through the forest under a full moon. This unique summer event draws runners from across the region.

August

Sacramento Mountain Wine Festival

Sample wines from New Mexico's best vineyards alongside local food, live music, and mountain views at this beloved annual event.

All Summer

Saturday Farmers Market

Fresh produce, local honey, handmade goods, and live music every Saturday morning through the summer season.

Practical Tips

Plan Your Summer Visit

Everything you need for the perfect summer mountain escape.

📅

Book Early

Summer is peak season. Reserve lodging well in advance, especially for holiday weekends like July 4th. Weekday visits offer better availability.

⛈️

Monsoon Prep

Plan outdoor activities for mornings. Afternoon storms in July and August are brief but can be intense. Always carry a rain layer on hikes.

🧴

Sun Protection

The sun is intense at 9,000 feet. Wear sunscreen, a hat, and sunglasses even when temperatures feel comfortable. Stay hydrated at altitude.

🌙

Evening Layers

Temperatures drop into the 40s and 50s after sunset. Bring a jacket or fleece for evening dining, stargazing, and outdoor events.
