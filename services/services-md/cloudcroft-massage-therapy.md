# Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage

## Quick Summary

Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage is an appointment-only massage therapy practice at **404 Burro Ave., Suite 201**, upstairs in the Otero County Electric Cooperative building in the heart of Cloudcroft. The practice focuses less on spa-style indulgence and more on pain relief, injury recovery, mobility, and individualized bodywork.

Owner and therapist **Robin Faux, LMT, CLT**, describes her work as problem-solving. In a Cloudcroft Reader profile, Faux said her training is muscle-specific and focused on problem-solving and pain relief, not just relaxation. That matches the business’s own positioning: **Specializing in Pain Relief and Injury Recovery.**

This is a strong local option for visitors, seasonal residents, hikers, skiers, cyclists, athletes, and people dealing with chronic pain, swelling, post-surgical recovery, or specific movement issues. It is not a walk-in massage shop. Call, text, email, or book online before going.

## What You’ll Find

Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage offers customized massage sessions built around the client’s goals. Services include:

- Therapeutic massage
- Pain-relief-focused massage
- Injury recovery support
- Neuromuscular therapy
- Manual lymphatic drainage
- Lymphedema therapy
- Kinesio taping
- Thai massage / Table-Thai massage
- Gift certificates
- Membership and package discount options

## Techniques

### Neuromuscular Therapy

Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage describes neuromuscular therapy as muscle-specific work used to reduce tension, relieve pain, and improve movement. The business says both Robin Faux and Jim Swaagman are trained in neuromuscular therapy.

### Manual Lymphatic Drainage

Robin Faux is a certified Manual Lymphatic Drainage therapist trained in the Vodder method. The business describes this as a lighter-touch technique that encourages fluid movement and may help with swelling after injury or surgery.

### Kinesio Taping

Kinesio taping can be added to a massage session or booked as a 30-minute stand-alone service. The business says tape is applied with specific attention to tissue manipulation and desired results.

### Thai Massage

Robin has training in Traditional Thai Massage and Table-Thai Massage. The business describes Thai massage as using deep massage, stretching, and acupressure.

## Owner Profile: Robin Faux

Robin Faux is a licensed massage therapist and certified Manual Lymphatic Drainage therapist. Her New Mexico massage license is listed on the business website as **MT-5600**. The site says she graduated from Sinclair Community College in Dayton, Ohio, with an Associate of Applied Science degree in Integrative Medical Massage Therapy. She later received IANMT certification in Neuromuscular Therapy from the Colorado Institute of Massage Therapy and trained in Manual Lymphatic Drainage and Lymphedema Therapy through Klose Training.

The Cloudcroft Reader reported that Faux moved to Cloudcroft permanently in 2011 and has run Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage with her partner, Jim Swaagman, since 2016. Jim recently retired from providing massage but remains a founding partner and consults on complex cases.

The Reader profile emphasizes Faux’s clinical, investigative style. She gathers information through conversation, observation, and touch, watching for imbalances in tissue density, texture, and temperature. Faux told the Reader she sees many clients with chronic pain and has worked with everyone from athletes to people recovering from joint replacement surgery.

## Jim Swaagman

James Swaagman, LMT, MMP, NMT, Reiki Master, is listed on the business website as a founding partner of Cloudcroft Therapeutic Massage. His New Mexico massage license is listed as **MT-5294**. The site says he recently retired from providing massages but still consults on complex cases.

The website says Jim graduated from the Humanities School of Massage in Pinellas Park, Florida, in 2003 as a Certified Neuromuscular Therapist, became a certified Reiki Master in 2005, and became a certified Medical Massage Practitioner. It also says he became certified as a Myoskeletal Alignment Practitioner for the lower body in 2015.

## Visitor Tips

- Book ahead. The business says no walk-ins.
- Use online booking if you are visiting part-time or coming from out of town.
- Tell the therapist your goal: relaxation, pain relief, injury recovery, swelling, mobility, or a specific issue.
- Wear comfortable clothes if you are booking taping or a session that includes movement assessment.
- Ask about stairs or accessibility before booking, since the practice is described as a second-floor office.
- Give yourself time to park and find the upstairs suite.
- Gift certificates may work well for locals, seasonal residents, hikers, skiers, or people recovering from injury.
