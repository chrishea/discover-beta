When what you want on a Cloudcroft shopping day is actual clothing — not a curated gift, not a crystal, not a t-shirt — Coco Blu is the stop on the map. "Head to Tootsies" is the whole pitch.

What's in the shop

Clothing drives the visit; shoes, jewelry, handbags, beauty, accessories, and home accents round out a full-look browse. Specific brands aren't published on the site — expect rotating fashion inventory with a boutique-speed staff.

Practical notes

Hours & seasonal rhythm

Sun–Thu 10:30 AM – 5 PM; Fri–Sat 10:30 AM – 6 PM. Source is the official website, which is unusual in its consistency for Cloudcroft retail. Seasonal-town hours can still shift, so a quick check before a timed visit is reasonable — but this is the strongest schedule we have on the retail map.

Busiest windows are weekends and early afternoons during summer. Mid-week mornings are quieter.

Location & pairing with Burro Ave

94 Little Glorietta Ave is one block off Burro. The side-street address gives Coco Blu a slightly quieter browse than a Burro storefront, but the shop is still an easy walk from any Burro parking. The natural pairing is a Burro stroll (High Altitude, Poke The Bear, Aspen & Ivy) with a Coco Blu detour in the middle.

Brands, sizes & online

Specific brand roster is not published on the official site — the inventory rotates and the boutique's editorial approach is category-led ("Head to Tootsies") rather than brand-led. Size ranges aren't documented; call (575) 682-3039 if it matters for a specific purchase.

Online-ordering and shipping policies aren't confirmed from crawlable sources. Treat Coco Blu as an in-person boutique unless confirmed otherwise.

Parking, accessibility & payment

Parking is on-street along Little Glorietta and Burro. The side-street location usually means slightly easier parking than the busiest Burro Avenue storefronts.

Specific accessibility details for the 94 Little Glorietta unit aren't documented; confirm before relying on it. Card and cash payment are standard.

Editor's take

On a shopping day you'd build around this one and let the rest of Burro fill in."
