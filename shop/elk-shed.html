What's in the shop

Wider than a souvenir rack, tighter than a general gift store. Southwestern goods anchor the identity, gourmet pantry jars add a category most Cloudcroft shops skip, and Cloudcroft-branded throw pillows, mountain home décor, mugs, glassware, and clothing fill out the rest.

1

Southwestern goods

Anchor category

The shop's identity lane — Southwestern textiles, blankets, home décor, and regional accent pieces in the warm-wood-and-rug presentation.

  • Southwestern-pattern textiles and blankets
  • Southwestern home décor
  • Regionally themed accent pieces

A Southwestern blanket or runner is the cleanest take-home from this category — design-forward, lodge-friendly, and unmistakably regional.

2

Gourmet pantry

Skipped elsewhere

Jarred pantry staples and gift-quality food — a category most Burro Avenue shops don't carry. Specific brand lines aren't yet documented.

  • Jarred pantry staples (jams, sauces)
  • Gift-quality food items
  • Specialty pantry rotation

A jar of something pantry-shelf-worthy doubles as a gift you'd actually keep — and it's the category that makes The Elk Shed different from the souvenir lane.

3

Cloudcroft-branded home goods

House signature

Throw pillows with Cloudcroft, NM motifs and mountain-themed décor pieces — designed for here, not a generic mountain town.

  • "Welcome to the Mountains" pillow
  • "Real Housewives of Cloudcroft" pillow
  • Elk graphic and "It's Better in the Mountains" pillows
  • Mountain-themed décor pieces

The pillows are the most specifically Cloudcroft pickup on the floor — the merchandise that says where it came from without saying souvenir.

4

Mugs, glassware, tabletop & clothing

Mugs on the rustic wooden hutch, glassware, tabletop serving pieces, plus the clothing line listed at the shop level.

  • Mugs displayed on a rustic wooden hutch
  • Glassware and tabletop pieces
  • Clothing (specific brand lines not yet documented)

Browse the hutch — mugs and glassware are gift-bag-friendly and round out a Southwestern-textile or pantry-jar pickup nicely.

Decision aid

Good for · skip if

Good for

  • A Cloudcroft-branded gift you'd actually keep on a couch at home
  • Gourmet pantry pickups — a category most Burro shops skip
  • Southwestern textiles and mountain home décor
  • Pairing with Old Barrel Tea, Noisy Water Winery, and the Cloudcroft Sandwich Shop in the same building
  • Visitors who like dense, warm, browse-friendly retail interiors

Skip if

  • You want handmade, taste-driven boutique gifts — go to Aspen & Ivy
  • You want rummage-and-find vintage and used books — try Poke The Bear
  • You want broad outdoor gear, boots, and a Nambé-plus-pantry spread — The Highland on James Canyon Hwy is the wider-spread alternative
  • You need turquoise and Native American jewelry as the focus — Burro Ave Trading Post and the Turquoise Shop are nearby
  • You're walking the boardwalk before 11 a.m. — the door's closed

Practical notes

Hours, suite finding & calling ahead

Open daily 11 AM to 5 PM, Mountain Time. The 11 a.m. open is later than several Burro shops — not the right first stop on a 9 a.m. coffee walk, but a reliable mid-day pickup. Suite 109 sits inside the Burro Street Exchange; on a busy weekend it can take a minute to find. Call (575) 415-9365 if you need to confirm before you walk over.

An online storefront runs at shop.theelkshed.com alongside the Burro Avenue retail floor. Specific shipping terms, return policy, and which inventory is online versus in-store only aren't documented here — ask in-store or email info@theelkshed.com for specifics.

Editor's take

"The Elk Shed is the gift-and-pantry stop on the 505 boardwalk. Wider than a souvenir rack, tighter than a general gift store. Southwestern goods anchor it, gourmet pantry jars give it a category nobody else on Burro is bothering with, and the Cloudcroft-branded throw pillows do the work a souvenir tee usually does — except you'd actually put them on the couch. The interior is a deliberate mountain-lodge build: wood paneling, oversized metal letter signage, antler accents, a Persian-style rug under a wooden display bench full of pillows. Warm, dense, browse-friendly. Pair it with tea, wine, or a sandwich next door and the visit pays for itself."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
