The 20-year family-run gift-and-souvenir anchor on Burro Avenue. Cloudcroft shirts, woodland toys, baby gifts, candles, rugs, and country home goods packed into one densely stocked storefront — with a 1969 Ford pickup parked out front for the photograph.

Cressie & Dwayne Brown · 508 Burro Ave · open daily 10–5 · gift-shop pricing
Six categories · rotating

What's in the shop

Mountain Magic is a working, daily-stocked Americana gift shop — not boutique-curated, not vintage rummage. The merchandise mix below comes from the local listings file, Cloudcroft Reader and Chamber coverage, and an April 2026 photo-evidence pass. Specific brands and individual items rotate; the categories stay steady.

1

Cloudcroft & mountain-town apparel

House specialty

The village-branded apparel layer most visitors come looking for.

  • Cloudcroft-branded t-shirts and tops
  • Patchwork and country-style dresses
  • Aprons and wearable home goods

If you came for one thing, the Cloudcroft t-shirt is the obvious pickup — and the shop carries the village-branded line at gift-shop prices.

2

Toys, plush & baby gifts

Strong stock

The shop's deepest specialty after apparel — woodland-themed and gift-ready.

  • Woodland-themed toys
  • Plush bears, sun characters, and stuffed animals
  • Baby gifts and giftable kid items
  • Decorative wood and folk-art animals (rooster, horse, etc.)

Plush and baby gifts are stocked deep enough to actually pick from — not a token end-cap.

3

Candles, soaps & natural goods

The candle wall is one of the photographed signature displays.

  • Jarred candles arranged by scent on dedicated shelving
  • Soap, lotion, and small natural-goods pickups
  • Country-pantry-style consumables

A jarred candle is the low-friction take-home that says "mountain town" without resorting to a magnet.

4

Rugs, blankets & soft goods

Center-floor textiles — practical pieces in country-cabin patterns.

  • Braided and hooked rugs, runners, and rounds
  • Throw blankets and quilted goods
  • Pillows and small textiles

If you've been hunting a braided rug or runner without a trip down the hill, this is the closest stocked source.

5

Home, country & seasonal décor

Country-cabin accents with seasonal rotation visible from the windows.

  • Country-cabin home accents
  • Rooster, horse, and farm-animal décor
  • Seasonal items (Easter pastels visible in spring photos)
  • Painted dressers and antique-style cabinets
  • Religious and inspirational wall art and stitched textile pieces

Style runs Americana and country, not contemporary or designer — go in expecting the cabin, not the gallery.

6

Souvenirs & small gifts

The pickup-and-go layer: small, gift-quality, Americana-leaning.

  • Magnets, ornaments, and pickup-and-go souvenirs
  • Greeting cards and small paper goods
  • Gift-quality tchotchkes that lean Americana over Southwestern

For Southwestern jewelry, turquoise, or boots, head to The Turquoise Shop or Burro Ave Trading Post instead.

Decision aid

Good for · skip if

What Mountain Magic actually does well, and where to point yourself instead when it isn't the right fit on the Burro Avenue strip.

Good for

  • A Cloudcroft t-shirt or branded souvenir
  • Baby and kid gifts with a woodland theme
  • Candles, soaps, and small natural-goods pickups
  • Country-and-cabin home décor and seasonal accents
  • Braided rugs, runners, and quilted soft goods
  • A long, slow browse through a multi-category small-town shop
  • A photo with the 1969 Ford pickup parked out front

Skip if

  • You want curated handmade-gift editing — go to Aspen & Ivy
  • You want vintage and humor-driven gifts — go to Poke The Bear next door
  • You want New Mexico-made pantry, boots, and contemporary general retail — go to The Highland on James Canyon Hwy

  • You need fine turquoise or Southwestern jewelry — try Burro Ave Trading Post or The Turquoise Shop

Practical notes

Address — 504, 506, or 508?

The painted storefront sign at the shop reads "Mountain Magic, 508 Burro Ave, The Biggest Little Store in Town" — confirmed in April 2026 photo evidence and Cloudcroft Chamber coverage. Yelp and TripAdvisor list the address as 504 Burro Ave; Loc8NearMe and the local listings file show 506. Treat 508 as the storefront-signage address and the others as legacy directory entries.

If you're navigating by GPS, anything in the 500-block of Burro will put you within a 30-second walk of the door — the storefront sign and the antique pickup are easy to spot from the boardwalk.

Hours, seasons & calling ahead

Listings-file hours are 10:00 a.m. to 5:00 p.m. seven days a week. Yelp's variant (10:00 a.m.–4:30 p.m., Monday through Saturday) is close enough to assume both reflect summer-pattern hours; winter and shoulder-season closures aren't documented in a primary source.

Before making the drive up — especially in winter — call (575) 682-2348 to confirm.

Family ownership & the Poke The Bear connection

Mountain Magic has been owned and operated by Cressie Brown for more than 20 years, per Cloudcroft Chamber and Cloudcroft Reader coverage. Cressie's family homesteaded in nearby Mayhill in 1884; Dwayne Brown is a co-owner of record.

The Browns also previously operated the next-door Rendezvous storefront, which transitioned to Poke The Bear under new ownership. Some of the same vendor goods and Cloudcroft-branded merchandise the Browns supply continue to appear at Poke The Bear — useful context if you're shopping the strip and noticing overlap.

Name vs. folder slug

The published business name is Mountain Magic — confirmed by storefront signage, Cloudcroft Chamber coverage, and Cloudcroft Reader. The internal photo folder slug magic-mountain and filenames such as magic-mountain-XXXX reverse the name; that's a filename-only convenience and not the published brand. Some third-party directories also list it as "Mountain Magic Market" — same shop.

Payment, returns & online sales

Online sales and shipping aren't confirmed from a primary source — treat this as an in-person shop. Payment, return, and shipping policies aren't documented in current coverage; ask at the counter for anything that matters (gift returns, large textiles, fragile items).

The 1969 Ford pickup out front

The vintage Ford parked in front of the shop is a chamber-encouraged photo prop — the Cloudcroft Chamber's 2Cool Highlight feature explicitly invites visitors to take a picture with it. It's also the easiest landmark for finding the storefront on a busy boardwalk day.

Above the entry, a placard reads "There's no place like this place, so this must be the place" — if you can see that and the OPEN sign, you're at the right door.

Accessibility & pets

Specific accessibility details for the Mountain Magic storefront aren't documented; the boardwalk is generally step-up, and the densely stocked interior layout has narrow aisles between full-height shelving. Confirm at the door if mobility access matters.

Small well-behaved dogs on leash are generally tolerated at walkable Burro Avenue shops, but check at the door rather than assuming.

Editor's take

"Mountain Magic is the Burro Avenue shop that does what a small mountain-town gift store is supposed to do, and has been doing it under the same family for more than two decades. Cloudcroft shirts, woodland toys, baby gifts, candles, braided rugs, and country décor packed wall-to-wall — not curated, not edited, just stocked. If you want one shop on the boardwalk that hands you both the souvenir and the gift for the kid back home, this is it. Park near the antique pickup, take the photo, then go inside."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
