Thirty years of curation on a pink-and-turquoise side street. Off the Beaten Path is the Cloudcroft art-gift gallery where you walk out with something framed, not something printed.

Since 1996 · 100 Glorietta · original artwork · sculpture · ceramics · metalwork · one-off gifts
Two layers · curated

What's in the shop

Original artwork is the headline; eclectic handmade gifts are the second layer. Artist roster spans local, New Mexico, and across the United States. Inventory rotates often — the site itself cautions that photos don't necessarily represent current stock.

1

Original artwork

House specialty

The reason to drive up the side street — framed, sculptural, and one-off pieces.

  • Paintings
  • Sculpture
  • Ceramics
  • Metalwork
  • Prints and framed work

Walk slowly. Thirty years of curation rewards a second lap around the room.

2

Eclectic handmade gifts

The gift-shop layer — still maker-forward rather than distributor-stocked.

  • Handmade gift items
  • One-of-a-kind objects
  • Regional + national maker mix

A small ceramic, a metalwork piece, or a gift-scale item — the portable version of the gallery experience.

3

Artist mix

Not local-only. The shop explicitly brings in work from NM and across the US.

  • Local Cloudcroft/Sacramento Mountain artists
  • Broader New Mexico makers
  • National artists
  • Roster varies by season

Ask at the counter which pieces are currently from Cloudcroft or NM — the staff will tell you.

4

What the shop isn't

Context — what not to expect so you plan the rest of your Cloudcroft shopping elsewhere.

  • Not a souvenir shop (no volume mugs, t-shirts)
  • Not a clothing boutique
  • Not a hardware or utility stop
  • Not a Southwest-specialty turquoise/stone shop

If you want Cloudcroft retail outside this category, the shopping guide map points elsewhere.

Good for · skip if

What the gallery does well, and when to aim elsewhere on the Cloudcroft retail map.

Good for

  • A handmade gift with real maker provenance

Practical notes

April 2026 closure — read first

The shop's official site carries a notice that it's closed for the month of April 2026 while interior work is done. Check the site or call before planning a trip built around this shop.

Inventory rotation

The site itself says "photos do not necessarily represent current stock." That's accurate — this is a handmade-art shop, and specific pieces rotate. Don't drive up expecting a specific item you saw in a photo; drive up expecting a good selection of current work.

Artist roster scope

The shop mixes local Cloudcroft/Sacramento artists, broader NM makers, and national artists. Don't read "local gallery" and assume every piece is from the mountain — the mix is intentionally wider.

Parking, payment & accessibility

On-street parking along Glorietta and Burro. Card and cash standard. Specific accessibility details for the Glorietta Ave building aren't documented; confirm before relying on it.
