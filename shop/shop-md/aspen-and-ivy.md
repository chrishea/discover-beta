# Aspen &amp; Ivy Boutique

## 1. Executive summary

Aspen &amp; Ivy Boutique is one of Cloudcroft's more curated gift-and-boutique stops — a Burro Avenue shop owned by Nicole Alcorn carrying bespoke handmade gifts, original art, home goods, and beauty products. The shop sits in the Burro Avenue courtyard retail cluster next to the Pine Stump Mall and near The Fat Rabbit. Cloudcroft Reader reported the August 2024 relocation from Village Plaza to Burro Street, and multiple 2025 retail blurbs continue to place the shop there with the same contact details. This is less a clothing-heavy boutique (that's Coco Blu) and less a general souvenir stop — it sits in the curated, taste-driven middle of the Burro retail mix.

## 4. What the shop is

Aspen &amp; Ivy reads as a Burro Avenue boutique that's specifically *not* trying to be a gift-shop-with-everything. The strongest current local coverage — Cloudcroft Reader across 2024 and 2025 — consistently frames it as a curated, taste-driven source of bespoke handmade gifts, original art, home goods, and beauty products. That category mix separates it from Coco Blu (clothing-forward), from Poke The Bear (rummage-and-discover vintage), and from general souvenir shops.

In practical terms: this is the stop on Burro Avenue for a gift that wouldn't embarrass a discerning recipient — a handmade piece, a locally made beauty item, or a piece of original art — rather than a magnet or a t-shirt. The 2024 Village-Plaza-to-Burro-Avenue relocation moved the shop into the walkable courtyard cluster, which is the most browse-friendly real estate in town.

## 6. What I'd buy

Three defensible picks using only confirmed merchandise categories:

1. **A handmade gift for a specific person.** The bespoke/handmade layer is the shop's signature — the item you'd rather carry home than order online.
2. **A piece of original art by a local artist.** Local-art coverage is explicit in Cloudcroft Reader reporting; this is the stop for it on Burro if your budget isn't ready for a dedicated gallery purchase.

## 7. Atmosphere and shopping experience

Aspen &amp; Ivy sells the calm side of Burro Avenue. The courtyard retail cluster around the Pine Stump Mall and The Fat Rabbit encourages slow browsing rather than get-in-get-out shopping, and Aspen &amp; Ivy's curated inventory rewards that pace. The boutique fits next to The Little Gallery in vibe — a small, taste-driven retail run by an owner-operator who personally chose what's on the shelves.

**Best fit**

- Gift shoppers who want character over quantity.
- Visitors already doing a Burro Avenue stroll.
- Anyone who prefers handmade and local over mass-market.
- Art browsers who'd like a lower-commitment stop than a dedicated gallery.
- Home-and-beauty fans who appreciate a curated edit.

**Likely drawbacks**

- Hours aren't posted reliably enough to plan a specific trip around — text before coming.
- Inventory changes; specific items from coverage aren't guaranteed on a given day.
- Not a full souvenir stop — wrong fit if you need magnets, shot glasses, or t-shirts at volume.

## 9. Background and history

Cloudcroft Reader reported in March 2024 that Aspen &amp; Ivy was in Village Plaza and carried home goods, beauty products, bespoke gifts, and more. The same outlet reported on August 19, 2024 that Nicole Alcorn relocated Aspen &amp; Ivy from Village Plaza to Burro Street next to the Pine Stump Mall. Later 2024 and 2025 coverage continues to place Aspen &amp; Ivy on Burro Avenue and identifies Nicole Alcorn as the owner; her name also comes up in Cloudcroft Reader's piece on Trey McCurley and The Little Gallery in the same Burro Avenue courtyard cluster.

That makes Aspen &amp; Ivy a useful example of a newer, evolving Burro Avenue retail business rather than a long-established legacy shop — it moved into a better-trafficked address and has kept the same owner, curation angle, and contact details through the move.
