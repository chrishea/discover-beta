# Burro Street Trading Post

## 1. Executive summary

Burro Street Trading Post (also published as Burro Street Trading Co.) is the Southwestern anchor inside the Burro Street Exchange at 505 Burro Ave, Suite 103 — a long-running specialty shop built around Native American jewelry, turquoise, sterling silver, rocks, crystals, geodes, pottery, and Southwest home décor. The current Chamber listing and the store's own website align closely on identity and contact details. Chamber materials frame it as a nearly-20-year Burro Avenue fixture; official and third-party sources agree on the 505 Burro address but split on exact hours, so a same-day check by phone is the honest way to confirm before making the drive.

## 4. What the shop is

Burro Street Trading Post is the themed Southwest retail stop on the avenue — not a general gift shop, not a souvenir stand. The store's own website opens with Native American handmade jewelry, handmade sterling silver, Southwestern art, rocks, crystals, and "unique treasures," and the Chamber member page describes essentially the same inventory. Chamber materials also position the shop as one of the long-running anchors inside the Burro Street Exchange — the indoor retail mall at 505 Burro — with a roughly twenty-year run, though that age is worth reconfirming with the shop directly.

Practically: if you're after turquoise, a gemstone, a crystal, a kachina, or a piece of Southwestern pottery, this is a much more direct fit than a mixed souvenir shop. If you want a fair-trade or boutique identity, Instant Karma sits up the same avenue; if you want curated handmade gifts and local art, Aspen &amp; Ivy and Off the Beaten Path are the other stops.

## 6. What I'd buy

Three defensible picks using only confirmed merchandise:

1. **A turquoise piece — ring or cuff.** The shop's headline category. The depth here is greater than a general gift shop can offer.

## 7. Atmosphere and shopping experience

The shop sits inside the Burro Street Exchange, which is the indoor retail mall at 505 Burro — a different browsing pattern from the walk-in storefronts elsewhere on the avenue.
