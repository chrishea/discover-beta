# High Altitude

## 1. Executive summary

High Altitude is Cloudcroft's practical outdoor outfitter — the shop that overlaps outdoor gear, apparel, bikes, and a small women's boutique section under one roof at 310 Burro Ave. It's one of the most useful retail stops in town because it sits at the junction of "forgot something" and "bought something" — footwear, outerwear, camping gear, mountain bikes, maps, souvenirs, and gifts on a single floor. Official hours post consistently (Mon–Thu 10–5:30, Fri–Sat 10–6, Sun 10–5), so this is one of the easier shops to plan a trip around. The one live caveat: Chamber materials have described High Altitude as doing bike rentals, but the shop's own current bicycles page has said no rentals for the season — confirm before relying on it.

## 4. What the shop is

High Altitude is the most practical retail stop on Burro Avenue. The official site frames it as an outdoor-gear store, but the actual inventory crosses multiple categories — footwear (Patagonia, Merrell, Hoka), outerwear, camping and winter gear, mountain bikes, sunglasses, plus a women's boutique section and a small souvenir/gift layer. Chamber directory coverage matches that mix.

The shop's utility is simple: it's the Cloudcroft answer to "I forgot my rain jacket," "I need hiking socks," "I want a trail map," "I'd like to look at a mountain bike before buying one," and "I need a last-minute gift that isn't a magnet." The boutique section on top of the gear means travelers who don't want a mountain-mountain shop still have reason to walk in.

## 6. What I'd buy

Three defensible picks using only confirmed merchandise categories:

1. **A pair of hiking shoes.** Patagonia, Merrell, or Hoka on the floor — the real reason this shop earns a trip versus a trailhead-only outing.

## 7. Atmosphere and shopping experience

A proper outfitter — gear on racks, footwear walls, bike floor space, and a smaller boutique section worked in. Service tends to be informed; staff answer trail questions and map out routes at the counter. The store sits at 310 Burro, directly on the walkable strip, with parking on-street.

**Best fit**

- Visitors who forgot gear (rain jacket, warm layer, shoes).
- Hikers preparing for a specific trail — map + footwear + fleece on one stop.
- Mountain-bike shoppers browsing pre-purchase.
- Families who need weather-appropriate mountain gear.
- Anyone who wants to leave with Patagonia/Merrell/Hoka options rather than chain-brand substitutes.

**Likely drawbacks**

- Bike rentals are not currently offered despite third-party listings — don't plan around them.
- Service-shop scope isn't cleanly documented — call before bringing in a specific repair.
- Not a pure-souvenir shop.
