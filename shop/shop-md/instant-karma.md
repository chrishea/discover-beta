# Instant Karma

## 1. Executive summary

Instant Karma is the most unusual retail stop in Cloudcroft — a yoga / Ayurveda / fair-trade-boutique / chai-bar / organic-produce hybrid run by Alex at 302 Burro Ave. It's not a shop; it's a wellness studio with a storefront. The mix — yoga classes, Ayurvedic counseling, Club Veg organic produce, a boutique with fair-trade and globally sourced goods, and a chai bar serving Ayurvedic teas — makes it the least conventional and arguably most distinctive retail stop on the avenue. Public site posts daily hours (12 PM – 5 PM), names Alex as owner/teacher/Ayurvedic counselor/buyer, and maintains both a wellness site and a Square shop site.

## 4. What the shop is

Instant Karma doesn't fit the Cloudcroft retail mold — and that's the point. The official site leads with yoga and Ayurveda, not products. Alex runs the teaching, counseling, and buying. The storefront layer is a fair-trade and globally sourced boutique plus a chai bar pouring Ayurvedic teas and drinks. Club Veg, an organic-produce pickup club, operates out of the same space. Chamber coverage matches the hybrid frame.

For a visitor: this is a yoga class followed by a chai, plus a browse through a boutique whose inventory is rooted in fair-trade sourcing — not a quick souvenir stop. For locals: it's the town's wellness anchor.

## 6. What I'd buy

Three defensible picks from confirmed offerings:

1. **A chai at the chai bar.** The lowest-commitment way into the shop's whole identity — Ayurvedic drink, ten minutes inside the wellness layer.
2. **A fair-trade piece from the boutique.** Anything that signals the shop's sourcing — a gift or small personal pickup you won't find at a mountain-gear or souvenir stop.
3. **A yoga class.** Not a product, but arguably the best pickup. Check the class schedule on the official site before planning a trip around it.

## 7. Atmosphere and shopping experience

Unlike any other Burro Avenue shop. Slow-paced, incense-tinged, more studio-like than retail. Expect a small boutique footprint alongside yoga-studio and chai-bar use, with produce pickups slotting into the weekly rhythm. The audience is split between visitors curious about the hybrid and locals using it for weekly classes or Club Veg.
