# Mountain Magic

## 1. Executive summary

Mountain Magic is one of Cloudcroft's longest-running Burro Avenue retail anchors — a densely stocked, family-run gift, souvenir, and home-goods shop owned and operated by Cressie Brown for more than 20 years. The painted sign above the storefront reads "Mountain Magic, 508 Burro Ave, The Biggest Little Store in Town" and that line is the cleanest summary of the store: a small footprint stuffed with Cloudcroft shirts, woodland toys, baby gifts, candle and pantry-style natural goods, braided rugs, blankets, plush, seasonal décor, and rotating antiques. The folder slug `magic-mountain` and filenames `magic-mountain-XXXX` reverse the canonical name — confirmed signage and Cloudcroft Chamber coverage both publish the business as **Mountain Magic**, not Magic Mountain. The Brown family also previously operated the neighboring Rendezvous storefront, which transitioned to Poke The Bear; Mountain Magic remains their flagship.

## 4. What the shop is

Mountain Magic is the family-owned gift-and-souvenir anchor on the Burro Avenue boardwalk — a long-running Cloudcroft retail fixture that does what a small mountain-town shop is supposed to do. The merchandise list from the local listings file lines up with what's visible inside the store: Cloudcroft-branded shirts, woodland and forest-themed toys, baby gifts, natural goods, candles, and a long bench of supporting categories. Photos show a single shopfront packed wall-to-wall with braided rugs and runners, plush figures, ceramic and decorative pieces, jarred candles arranged on shelving, country-style pantry décor, hand-stitched and printed wall art, patchwork dresses and clothing on rounders, and country-cabin home accents.

The character of the store is not boutique-curated and not vintage rummage. It's a working, daily-stocked Americana gift shop that has been on the same block under the same family for two decades. For visitors, it's the obvious stop for a Cloudcroft shirt, a baby gift, a candle, or a bag of country-store-style goods. For locals, it's a steady source for seasonal décor, gifts, and home accessories without a trip down the hill.

## 9. Background and history

Cressie Brown has owned and operated Mountain Magic on Burro Avenue for more than 20 years, per Cloudcroft Chamber coverage from 2024. Her family's ties to the area go back further: her ancestors homesteaded in nearby Mayhill in 1884, and the family has remained in the Sacramento Mountains across generations. Cressie and Dwayne Brown were also the previous owners of the next-door Rendezvous storefront; Cloudcroft Reader's reporting on the transition of Rendezvous into Poke The Bear identifies Mountain Magic as the Browns' flagship store and notes that goods and t-shirts the Browns supplied have continued to appear at Poke The Bear under new ownership.

Third-party listing sources put the founding year around 2003, which aligns with the chamber's "20 years on Burro" framing. Mountain Magic has not transitioned ownership in the public record, which makes it one of the more stable long-tenured shops on the street.
