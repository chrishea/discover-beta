# Instant Karma

## 1. Executive summary

Instant Karma appears current as one of Cloudcroft’s more distinctive hybrid businesses: part boutique, part yoga and Ayurveda studio, part wellness-oriented gathering space. It is not a standard gift shop, and it is not just a yoga studio. The strongest current sources show it operating at 302 Burro Ave with retail, yoga, Ayurvedic counseling, and a current chai-bar identity in local coverage.

## 3. What the shop appears to be

Instant Karma appears to be one of the least conventional retail stops in Cloudcroft. Its identity is rooted in wellness and Eastern traditions more than in standard mountain-town gift merchandise. That makes it stand out. It likely appeals to shoppers and visitors looking for yoga, chai, wellness events, boutique goods, and a slower, more intentional browsing experience.

## 5. Atmosphere and shopping experience

Local reporting and the official site suggest a place where the experience matters as much as the products. Cloudcroft Reader says the Burro Avenue space added an Afternoon Chai Bar and describes the yoga studio as having heated wood floors and props assembled over years of teaching. That points to a shop where people may browse, pause, and participate rather than simply buy and leave.

## 6. Background and local context

Cloudcroft Reader reported in 2024 that owner Alex Carilli opened the Burro Avenue yoga studio in 2005 and that it gradually became the boutique with Eastern flair seen today. The official site’s current about page identifies Alex as the owner, yoga teacher, Ayurvedic health counselor, Club Veg coordinator, and buyer. Taken together, those sources show a business with real continuity and a distinctive evolution from studio to broader lifestyle space.
