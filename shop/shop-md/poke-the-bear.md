## 4. What the shop is

Poke The Bear doesn't shop with a fixed list, and it isn't a boutique. The Chamber listing names more categories than most shops would try to hold under one roof — used books, upcycled furniture, lamps, antiques, vintage, collectibles, blankets, outdoor gear, hats, shirts, Bigfoot items, souvenirs, "and more." The name itself signals the personality. This is the Burro Avenue stop for visitors who want quirk over polish.

The mix is appeal to both visitors and locals at once: outdoor gear, blankets, and hats pull in the road-trip side; antiques and used books pull in the local-curiosity side. If you like small-town retail that rewards a slow browse, this is the shop. If you need a specific item fast, this isn't it.

## 6. What I'd buy

Three defensible picks from confirmed categories:

1. **A used book.** The most portable item from a rummage shop — pick one on the way through.
2. **A Bigfoot item.** The Chamber calls this out by name; it's the personality pickup.
3. **A vintage or upcycled object.** A lamp, a small piece of furniture, an antique with character — the shop rewards asking "what's the story on this?"

## 7. Atmosphere and shopping experience

Informal, densely stocked, browse-friendly. Not a boutique — aisles are closer to antique-mall spacing than clothing-shop spacing. Expect conversation at the counter and a slower pace than the average Burro Avenue storefront.

**Best fit**

- Visitors who like quirky, mixed-inventory shopping.
- Used-book browsers looking for small finds.
- Shoppers who prefer oddball, vintage, or upcycled over polished.
- Souvenir shoppers who want something less generic than a standard gift rack.
- Travellers amused by a Bigfoot-items section.

**Likely drawbacks**

- No posted hours — call before timing a visit.
- Website not confirmed; online presence thin.
- Specific items seen in a photo won't necessarily be there next visit.
- Not a fast in-and-out shop; rewards slowing down.
