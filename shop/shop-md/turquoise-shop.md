# The Turquoise Shop

## 1. Executive summary

The Turquoise Shop is Cloudcroft's longest-running fine-jewelry stop and the deepest turquoise specialty on Burro Avenue. Founded in 1975 by Jeannie Read Elkins — a Cloudcroft native whose family lines run back to the village's railroad and auto-parts era — the shop sits at 507 Burro Ave., directly next door to the Burro Street Exchange jewelry cluster (RedWall at Suite 107, Trading Post at Suite 103). The shop's identity is built on direct-from-the-reservation buying in New Mexico and Arizona, traditional and contemporary Native American jewelry, and a broader Southwest art layer that includes Pueblo pottery, Zuni fetish carvings, Hopi kachinas, Navajo sand paintings, rugs, and baskets. Hours are confirmed: 10 a.m. to 5 p.m., closed Sunday. The shopping-guide pitch line — "fine jewelry at every price point" — is also the shop's clearest self-description.

## 4. What the shop is

The Turquoise Shop is the fine-jewelry anchor of Burro Avenue. The shopping guide frames it as "a trusted destination for authentic turquoise jewelry and Native American art" with more than 40 years on the avenue; the shop's own story page pushes that history back to 1975, founded by Jeannie Read Elkins of Cloudcroft. The buying model is the load-bearing detail: pieces come direct from reservations in New Mexico and Arizona, which is how the shop sustains depth across both traditional and contemporary work.

Within the Burro Avenue jewelry cluster, the three stops play distinct roles. Trading Post at 505 Suite 103 is the broad Southwestern anchor — turquoise plus rocks, crystals, and home décor. RedWall at 505 Suite 107 is the handmade-forward boutique, in Cloudcroft since the 1970s. The Turquoise Shop at 507 is the dedicated turquoise institution: deeper turquoise depth, more Native American art breadth (pottery, kachinas, sand paintings, rugs, baskets), and the longest single-identity tenure of the three.

Three defensible picks from confirmed merchandise:

1. **A turquoise piece — ring, cuff, or pendant.** The shop's headline category and the reason to walk past the two neighboring jewelry stops and into 507. Direct-from-reservation buying gives the depth the broader gift shops can't match.

## 9. Background and history

The Turquoise Shop was founded in 1975 by Jeannie Read Elkins, a Cloudcroft native. Per the shop's own story page, her grandfather conducted the train depot in the village and her father owned and operated an auto-parts warehouse; she met Sam Elkins, her husband and a Grants, NM cowboy, at New Mexico State University. The shop has been on Burro Avenue ever since — making it one of the longest-tenured retail businesses in Cloudcroft, alongside RedWall (also 1970s) and King's Treasure Thrift Store (38+ years).

The shop has stayed in the family across generations; the story page is published by a grandchild of the founder. A current-day owner / operator named Shawna shows up in recent third-party reviews as the on-the-floor face of the business, but the family-line continuity from Jeannie Read Elkins is the thread the shop publishes itself.
