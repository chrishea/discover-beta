What's in the shop

Fine jewelry first, broader Native American art second. Pieces come direct from reservations in New Mexico and Arizona, which is what gives the shop the depth across both traditional and contemporary work.

1

Turquoise & Native American jewelry

House specialty

The headline category and the reason to walk past the two neighboring jewelry stops and into 507. Traditional and contemporary work in sterling silver, copper, and gold settings.

  • Traditional Native American jewelry
  • Contemporary Southwest jewelry
  • Sterling silver, copper, and gold settings
  • Rings, cuffs, pendants, earrings, necklaces
  • Direct-from-reservation buying in New Mexico and Arizona

"Fine jewelry at every price point" is the shop's own framing — entry-level pieces sit in the cases alongside higher-end work.

2

Pueblo pottery & storytellers

Pueblo pottery and storyteller figures — the Native American art layer that separates 507 from a jewelry-only store.

  • Pueblo pottery
  • Storyteller figures
  • Pieces small enough to travel home

A small Pueblo piece is gift-portable, regionally rooted, and not the kind of object you'll replicate online.

3

Zuni, Hopi & Navajo art

Distinguishing layer

Zuni fetish carvings, Hopi kachinas, and Navajo sand paintings — three named tribal art categories, all under one roof.

  • Zuni fetish carvings
  • Hopi kachinas
  • Navajo sand paintings

A Zuni fetish or a small kachina is the higher-commitment souvenir that makes the visit count.

4

Textiles & baskets

Rugs and baskets round out the Southwest art layer. The textile-and-basket inventory rotates with what comes back from the reservations.

  • Rugs
  • Baskets
  • Inventory rotates with sourcing trips

Ask the counter about the current rugs and baskets on hand — the floor changes with what's been brought in.

Decision aid

Good for · skip if

Good for

  • Turquoise buyers who want depth, not a single case
  • Native American jewelry shoppers — traditional or contemporary
  • Pueblo pottery, kachina, Zuni fetish, and sand-painting browsers
  • Visitors looking for a higher-quality Southwest souvenir than a gift-shop magnet
  • Anyone shopping the Burro Avenue jewelry cluster who wants the longest-tenured stop in the trio

Skip if

  • You want general Southwest retail with crystals, geodes, and rocks — go to Burro Street Trading Post
  • You want handmade nature-inspired jewelry rather than turquoise specialty — go to RedWall Jewelry
  • You want broad gift-shop variety — this is jewelry and Native American art, not magnets and t-shirts
  • You're shopping on a Sunday — closed

Practical notes

Hours, Sunday close & calling ahead

Open Monday through Saturday, 10 AM to 5 PM. Closed Sunday — the only major Burro Avenue shop with a hard Sunday close. The "Open now" pill at the top of the page reflects today's status.

If your trip falls on a Sunday and turquoise is on the list, plan to come back Monday or pick a different jewelry stop on the avenue. Trading Post and RedWall are the two next-door alternatives.

Direct-from-reservation sourcing & provenance

The shop sources pieces direct from reservations in New Mexico and Arizona — that's the load-bearing detail behind the depth on the floor. Specific signature mines (Sleeping Beauty, Kingman, Royston) and named tribal artists rotate with inventory and aren't published as fixed lines.

If a specific mine, tribe, or artist matters for your purchase, ask at the counter. Independent provenance documentation beyond the shop's own statements isn't published in crawlable form.

Custom design, sizing, repair & appraisal

Custom design, ring sizing, restringing, and repair services are plausible for a fine-jewelry store of this age and specialty, but aren't published in crawlable sources. Appraisal services aren't confirmed either.

Call (575) 682-3327 before planning a visit around a specific service — ask at the counter what the shop currently handles in-house and what it sends out.

Online ordering & shipping

The shop's website is cloudcroftturquoise.com. Online-ordering and shipping policies aren't cleanly crawlable from current sources — if you want a specific piece shipped, ask at the counter or on the call.

Parking, payment & accessibility

On-street parking along Burro Avenue. Card and cash standard. The shop is a stand-alone Burro storefront — entry directly off the boardwalk rather than through an indoor mall. Specific accessibility details for 507 aren't documented; confirm before relying on it.

Editor's take

"The Turquoise Shop is the fine-jewelry institution of the Burro Avenue jewelry cluster. Trading Post is the broad Southwestern anchor with crystals and geodes; RedWall is the handmade-forward boutique. The Turquoise Shop, at 507, is the dedicated turquoise stop — fifty years in, direct-from-reservation depth, and a wider Native American art layer (pottery, Zuni fetishes, kachinas, sand paintings, rugs, baskets) that the other two don't try to cover. If turquoise is the reason you came to Cloudcroft, walk in here."

— DiscoverCloudcroft editors
