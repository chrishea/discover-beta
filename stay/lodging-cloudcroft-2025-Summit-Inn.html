About

A Practical Base for the Mountains

The Summit Inn sits at 9,000 feet on Chipmunk Avenue, a short walk from Cloudcroft's main street, restaurants, and shops. It's a no-fuss, well-run base for exploring the Sacramento Mountains — close to hiking trails in Lincoln National Forest, the Trestle Recreation Area, and Ski Cloudcroft.

The inn has 10 rooms, each named after a New Mexico city. Rooms are simply furnished but consistently described by guests as clean and well-maintained. Owners Timothy and Gail King run the property themselves and are known for pointing guests toward trails, restaurants, and current road conditions. The property has gone through recent renovations under their ownership.

This is not a resort. There's no hot tub, no concierge, no pool. What you get is a clean room with a kitchenette, Wi-Fi, a fire pit out back, and a location that puts you within walking distance of nearly everything in the village.

Why Stay Here

What Sets the Summit Inn Apart

Honest strengths from a well-run, owner-operated inn at 9,000 feet.

🚶

Walk Everywhere

At 0.1 miles from downtown Cloudcroft, you can walk to Mad Jack's BBQ, Brother-N-Law BBQ, Burro Avenue shops, and the Sacramento Mountains Museum without moving your car.

🍳

Kitchenette in Every Room

Full kitchenettes let you eat on your own schedule. Stock up at the grocery in Alamogordo on the way up and save on meals — or just make coffee and head out early to the trails.

🤝

Owner-Operated

Timothy and Gail King run the property themselves. Guests consistently mention their helpfulness — particularly for trail recommendations, road conditions, and local knowledge.

🐶

Pets Welcome

Unlike most Cloudcroft lodging, the Summit Inn allows pets on request. Charges may apply — confirm at booking. A welcome option for guests traveling with dogs.

🔥

Outdoor Fire Pit

Summer evenings at 9,000 feet cool down quickly. The outdoor fire pit is a good place to wind down, swap stories, and actually see the Milky Way without driving anywhere.

💰

Good Value

Starting at $134/night with kitchenettes and breakfast included, the Summit Inn is one of the more affordable options in a village where lodging can run scarce on busy weekends.

What's Nearby

From the Inn's Front Door

Cloudcroft's top attractions, all within reach.

🛤️

Trestle Recreation Area

The Mexican Canyon Railroad Trestle — one of southern New Mexico's most photographed structures. 0.9 miles from the inn.

⛷️

Ski Cloudcroft

The southernmost ski area in the U.S. is minutes away. A short drive up the mountain opens up winter terrain for all skill levels.

🔭

Sunspot Solar Observatory

The Sacramento Peak Solar Observatory is 20 miles south — a fascinating detour with tours and one of the best dark-sky viewing spots in the region.

🏖️

White Sands National Park

Approximately 60 miles west — a day trip that pairs perfectly with a mountain stay. Early morning or sunset are the best times to visit the gypsum dunes.
