# The Summit Inn

**A no-frills, well-kept inn in the heart of Cloudcroft — comfortable rooms, kitchenettes, and friendly owners.**

-----

## Overview

The Summit Inn sits at 9,000 feet on Chipmunk Avenue, a short walk from Cloudcroft’s main street, restaurants, and shops. It’s a practical, affordable base for exploring the Sacramento Mountains — close to hiking trails in Lincoln National Forest, the Trestle Recreation Area, and Ski Cloudcroft.

The inn has 10 rooms, each named after a New Mexico city. Rooms are simply furnished but consistently described by guests as clean and well-maintained. The owners, Timothy and Gail King, run the property themselves and are known for being helpful — pointing guests toward trails, restaurants, and road conditions. The property has gone through recent renovations, and the current ownership takes the upkeep seriously.

This is not a resort. There’s no hot tub, no concierge, no pool. What you get is a clean room with a kitchenette, Wi-Fi, a fire pit out back, and a location that puts you within walking distance of nearly everything in the village.

## Location

**103 Chipmunk Ave, Cloudcroft, NM 88317**

The inn is 0.1 miles from the center of Cloudcroft — walking distance to Burro Avenue shops, Mad Jack’s Mountaintop BBQ, and Brother-in-Law’s BBQ. Lincoln National Forest trailheads are nearby.

**Nearby attractions:**

- Trestle Recreation Area (Mexican Canyon Railroad Trestle) — 0.9 mi
- Sacramento Mountains Museum & Pioneer Village — walking distance
- Ski Cloudcroft — minutes away (southernmost ski area in the U.S.)
- Sunspot Solar Observatory — 20 miles
- White Sands National Park — approximately 60 miles west
- Valley of Fires Recreation Area — day trip
- Three Rivers Petroglyph Site — day trip
- Apache Mescalero Indian Reservation & Inn of the Mountain Gods Casino — 40 miles

**Driving distances:**

- El Paso, TX — 87.5 miles
- Alamogordo, NM — approximately 20 miles (via US-82)
- Roswell, NM — 74.1 miles

The elevation is approximately 9,000 feet. No air conditioning in rooms — not needed at altitude. Winter guests should note that mountain road conditions can change quickly; the owners are a reliable source for road condition updates.

## What Guests Say

Reviews across TripAdvisor, Booking.com, and KAYAK trend positive, with consistent praise for cleanliness, the owners’ helpfulness, and the kitchenette setup. Guests frequently mention the walkability to restaurants and the cozy, cabin-like atmosphere.

Common positives: clean rooms, friendly and knowledgeable owners, great location, full kitchenettes, good value.

Common negatives (older reviews): some rooms showed wear; no bathtubs (showers only); décor varies by room. The property has undergone renovations under current ownership, so older critical reviews may not reflect current conditions.

Booking.com score: **8.3** (recent guests). Couples rate the location 9.3.
