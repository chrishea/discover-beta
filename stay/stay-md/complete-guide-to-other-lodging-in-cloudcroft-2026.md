# Complete Guide to Other Lodging in Cloudcroft (2026)

_Last updated: April 3, 2026_

This guide covers the **non-hotel side of the Cloudcroft lodging market**: RV parks, campgrounds, cabin compounds, and short-term rentals in and around Cloudcroft. It is written for travelers deciding **what kind of stay fits them best**, not just where to book.

## Bottom line

Cloudcroft has a deeper “other lodging” market than many small mountain towns its size. The choices fall into four useful buckets:

1. **In-village RV parks** for people who want to park once and walk into town.
2. **Forest and edge-of-town campgrounds** for lower-cost, more outdoorsy stays.
3. **Private RV parks east or west of town** for travelers who want hookups, quieter settings, or longer stays.
4. **Cabins and short-term rentals** for families, couples, and groups who want kitchens, decks, fireplaces, or more privacy.

The biggest practical divide is not price. It is **style of stay**. Some places are close to Burro Avenue and the village core. Others are really **basecamps in the wider Cloudcroft/Mayhill/High Rolls corridor** and work best if you are driving everywhere.

## What the market looks like in 2026

### 1) RV parks

The RV market is more varied than it first appears.

**Closest to the village**
- **Sugar Pines RV Park** is inside the village and markets itself as the only RV park within walking distance of Cloudcroft. It has 16 full-hookup 30-amp sites, welcomes guests 18 and older, and is open for the 2026 season.  
- **Scenic Canyon RV Park** sits about 6.5 miles east of Cloudcroft and is adults-only, with 14 sites, Wi-Fi, and full hookups, but no bathhouse or shower facilities.  
- **Silver Springs RV Park & Trout Pond** is about 5 miles from Cloudcroft and positions itself as family-friendly.
- **16 Springs** is farther east and leans into seclusion, pets, and self-contained rigs.
- **Cloud Climbing Railroad RV Park** in High Rolls is a year-round full-hookup option tied to a restored historic site. https://www.cloudclimbingrailroad.com/about#:~:text=We%20are%20a%20family%20operated,in%20the%20Land%20of%20Enchantment.
- **Camp Rio** in Mayhill is broader than a typical RV park: full-hookup RV sites plus tents, glamping, and cabins.
- **Lazy Day Cabins & RV Hideaway** is also in the Mayhill corridor and mixes cabins, motel rooms, and RV spaces.

### 2) Public campgrounds

For classic camping, Lincoln National Forest is the backbone of the market.

The most relevant public campground options near Cloudcroft are:
- **Pines Campground**
- **Deerhead Campground**
- **Sleepy Grass Campground**
- **James Canyon Campground**

These are better for travelers who want forest access, lower nightly costs, and a more traditional campground feel than a private RV park. They also come with the usual tradeoffs: stronger seasonality, length limits for some RVs, and a bigger need to plan around forest conditions and fire restrictions.

### 3) Cabins and short-term rentals

This is where the market gets big.

- **Vrbo** currently shows **more than 148 vacation rentals** in the Cloudcroft market.
- **AirDNA** reports **296 short-term rentals** across Airbnb and Vrbo in Cloudcroft, with an average daily rate of **$192** and average occupancy of **48%**.
- **Airbnb** surfaces a strong guest signal, showing Cloudcroft stays averaging about **4.9 out of 5**.

That does not mean every option is equal. It means Cloudcroft has a **real short-term rental ecosystem**, not just a handful of cabins.

Three useful ways to shop this category:

**Small branded cabin operations**
- **The Cabins at Cloudcroft** offers a cabin compound close to the village.
- **Cabin Rentals Cloudcroft** runs a small curated set of cabins rather than a large marketplace.

**Broker-managed vacation rentals**
- **Cloudcroft Properties** markets vacation rental homes alongside real estate and property management.

**Marketplace inventory**
- Airbnb and Vrbo offer the widest choice, but the inventory moves constantly. These are best when you need a specific combination such as pet-friendly, fenced yard, hot tub, mountain view, workspace, or a larger group layout.

## Expert read: how to choose

### Best for walking to town
- **Sugar Pines RV Park**
- **The Cabins at Cloudcroft**
- Many village-based short-term rentals on Airbnb/Vrbo

If your trip is built around Burro Avenue, shops, dining, the Lodge area, or avoiding repeated drives, staying in or near the village matters more than almost anything else.

### Best for a quiet RV stay
- **Scenic Canyon RV Park**
- **16 Springs**
- **Silver Springs RV Park & Trout Pond**

These work best for travelers who value elbow room, scenery, and a lower-key environment more than immediate village access.

### Best for tent camping or a more classic campground trip
- **Pines**
- **Deerhead**
- **Sleepy Grass**
- **James Canyon**

These are stronger picks if your trip is really about the forest, trails, campfires when allowed, and a less commercial feel.

### Best for families or groups
- **The Cabins at Cloudcroft**
- **Cloudcroft Properties vacation rentals**
- Larger homes on **Vrbo**

Families usually do better in the short-term rental market than in RV parks because kitchens, multiple bedrooms, decks, laundry, and living space matter.

### Best for couples
- **Cabin Rentals Cloudcroft**
- Smaller branded cabins
- Village-area Airbnb/Vrbo cabins

For two people, the best options are often the smallest cabins with privacy and a porch rather than the biggest houses.

### Best for longer stays
- **Sugar Pines RV Park**
- **Lazy Day Cabins & RV Hideaway**
- Some monthly-discount properties on **Vrbo**

### Best for self-contained RV travelers
- **16 Springs**
- **Scenic Canyon RV Park**

Both make more sense if you are arriving with a rig that can operate comfortably without depending on a bathhouse.

## Profiles by property

## Sugar Pines RV Park
**Best for:** walkability, village access, simple stays close to restaurants and shops.

Sugar Pines is the clearest choice for RV travelers who want to be **in Cloudcroft**, not outside it. The official site says it is the only RV park within walking distance of the village and lists 16 full-hookup 30-amp sites. It is open for the 2026 season, welcomes guests **18 and older**, and notes holiday minimum stays plus nonrefundable deposits.

**Why choose it**
- Strongest walk-to-town position
- Full hookups
- Good fit for short or extended stays
- Cleaner choice for visitors who want Cloudcroft itself, not just the surrounding mountains

**Watch-outs**
- Adults only
- Back-in expectations on many sites
- Deposit policy is strict

## Scenic Canyon RV Park
**Best for:** a quiet adult-only RV stay east of town.

Scenic Canyon is about 6.5 miles east of Cloudcroft and explicitly markets itself as a peaceful **adults-only** park for guests **30 and over**. It lists 14 spacious sites with Wi-Fi, water, sewer, and electric. The big caveat is that it **does not provide bathroom or shower facilities**.

**Why choose it**
- Quiet setting
- Adults-only atmosphere
- Good if you dislike crowded family parks
- Full hookups and Wi-Fi

**Watch-outs**
- Not close enough to walk to town
- No bathhouse
- Better for fully equipped rigs

**Contact**
- Official site: https://www.cloudcroft-rv.com/

## 16 Springs
**Best for:** pet-friendly RV trips, a more remote feel, and self-contained travelers.

16 Springs positions itself as an “off the beaten path” RV park in the Lincoln National Forest corridor, about 20 minutes east of Cloudcroft. The site emphasizes pets, open space, and nature. It also makes an important operational point: **campers must be self-contained**, and there are **no public bathhouse/restrooms**.

**Why choose it**
- Pet-friendly
- More secluded feel
- Good for travelers who want to disconnect
- Water, electric, septic, and dump station access

**Watch-outs**
- Not in the village
- No public bathhouse/restrooms
- Some sites have tight occupancy limits

- https://www.16springs.com/

## Pines Campground
**Best for:** traditional forest camping close to Cloudcroft.

Pines Campground is in Lincoln National Forest at about **8,700 feet** and is one of the main official campground options in the immediate Cloudcroft area. Recreation.gov says it is heavily forested with large pine trees and wildlife viewing.

**Why choose it**
- Strong classic forest-camping feel
- Good proximity to Cloudcroft
- Official reservation system

**Watch-outs**
- Seasonal
- Public campground tradeoffs: fewer comforts, more competition, more rules tied to fire and forest conditions

**Book/verify**
- Recreation.gov Pines Campground listing

## Deerhead Campground
**Best for:** campers who want to be close to town and trails.

The Forest Service says Deerhead has **19 sites**, is very close to Cloudcroft, and sits across from the Rim Trail trailhead. Recreation.gov notes standard sites for tent and RV camping plus picnic tables, grills, fire rings, water spigots, vault toilets, and paved parking spurs.

**Why choose it**
- Close to the village
- Trail access
- Good balance between campground feel and Cloudcroft convenience

**Watch-outs**
- Seasonal conditions apply
- Less privacy than some private options

**Book/verify**
- Forest Service Deerhead Campground page
- Recreation.gov Deerhead Campground listing

## Sleepy Grass Campground
**Best for:** campers who want a public campground just south of the village.

Recreation.gov describes Sleepy Grass as just south of Cloudcroft, with sites for RVs up to **30 feet**, tents, and campers. The Forest Service page says it is typically first come, first served, with seasonal opening around mid-May.

**Why choose it**
- Near town
- Traditional campground setup
- Good option for smaller RVs and tents

**Watch-outs**
- 30-foot RV limit is important
- First-come, first-served conditions can complicate peak weekends
- Seasonality matters

**Book/verify**
- Recreation.gov Sleepy Grass listing
- Forest Service Sleepy Grass page

## James Canyon Campground
**Best for:** lower-elevation camping and shoulder-season flexibility.

The Forest Service says James Canyon has **5 sites** along US 82 west of Mayhill at a lower elevation of about **6,800 feet**, making it usable longer than many higher Sacramento Mountains campgrounds.

**Why choose it**
- Lower elevation
- Simpler, smaller campground
- Can work well when higher sites are colder or closed

**Watch-outs**
- Small
- Not in the village
- More basic setup than private campgrounds

**Book/verify**
- Forest Service James Canyon Campground page

## Editor’s take: best choices by traveler type

### If you want the simplest answer
Book **inside or next to the village** unless you have a reason not to. Many visitors underestimate how much easier the trip feels when you can get to shops, dining, and the village core quickly.

### If you are bringing an RV and want town access
Start with **Sugar Pines**.

### If you are bringing an RV and want peace and space
Start with **Scenic Canyon**, **16 Springs**, or **Silver Springs**, depending on whether you want adult-only, pet-friendly, or family-friendly.

### If you want a true camping trip
Start with **Deerhead**, **Pines**, or **Sleepy Grass**.

### If you want a cabin and do not want to sort through hundreds of listings
Start with **The Cabins at Cloudcroft** or **Cloudcroft Properties**.

### If you want the best exact fit and do not mind searching
Use **Airbnb** or **Vrbo** and filter aggressively.

## Practical advice before you book

- **Check exact location.** “Cloudcroft” listings can be in the village, outside town, east toward Mayhill, or west toward High Rolls.
- **Ask about winter access.** Grades, snow, and driveways matter.
- **Check fire restrictions.** Campfire and grill rules can change quickly in the Sacramento Mountains.
- **Verify bathhouse access.** Some RV parks are best only for self-contained rigs.
- **Check age rules and pet rules.** They vary widely.
- **Do not assume walkability.** Many mountain properties are “near Cloudcroft” but not close enough to walk anywhere.
- **Read cancellation terms closely.** Some private RV parks and rentals use strict deposit policies.
