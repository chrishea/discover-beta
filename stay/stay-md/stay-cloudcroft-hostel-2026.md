# Cloudcroft Hostel

**A real mountain hostel on Highway 82 — shared kitchens, dorm beds, group rentals, and trails right outside the front door.**

---

## The Short Version

Cloudcroft Hostel is a genuine hostel, not a hotel with a quirky name. It's a budget-friendly, community-oriented mountain base in High Rolls Mountain Park — not central Cloudcroft, but close enough to reach everything by car.

The strongest reasons to book here are price, shared kitchen and common spaces, group flexibility, and immediate access to outdoor recreation. The tradeoffs are equally clear: shared bathrooms, hostel norms instead of hotel norms, and a location that requires driving to reach Burro Avenue.

This is a deliberate choice for travelers who want value, shared space, and an outdoor base. If you want privacy, on-site dining, or standard hotel amenities, this isn't it.

---

## Accommodations

The hostel offers 8-bed dorm rooms that can also be reserved for private use. Group rentals allow exclusive use of the first floor for up to 25 guests.

| Type | Capacity | Notes |
|------|----------|-------|
| Dorm Bed | 1 guest per bed | 8-bed dorm rooms, shared bathrooms |
| Private Dorm | Up to 8 guests | Reserve an entire 8-bed dorm for your group |
| First Floor Group Rental | Up to 25 guests | Exclusive use of the first floor |
| Private / Family Rooms | Varies | Listed on third-party platforms — confirm availability directly |

All stays include linens, pillows, and lockers. Shared bathrooms and common spaces are part of the hostel model.

Private and family room options are referenced on Booking.com and TripAdvisor, but the current official site focuses on dorms and group bookings. Confirm the current room mix directly.

## Amenities

- Fully equipped shared kitchen (stovetop, oven, fridge, cookware, dishes)
- Common lounge areas with books and board games
- Large porch with mountain views
- Outdoor fire pit (weather permitting)
- Free Wi-Fi in all areas
- Free private parking on-site
- Lockers for personal items
- Linens and pillows included
- Star-gazing from the property

**Not available:**
- On-site restaurant, café, or bar
- Room service or daily housekeeping
- Private bathrooms (shared facilities)
- Pool, spa, or gym

**The food setup:** You cook, snack, or drive into town. The shared kitchen is a real kitchen — well-equipped and frequently praised by guests. Cloudcroft dining is a short drive away.

## Who Should Book Cloudcroft Hostel

**Best for:**

- Hikers, cyclists, and outdoor travelers
- Solo travelers comfortable with hostel culture
- Friend groups looking for a shared mountain base
- Family reunions, retreat groups, and wedding parties

## Location

**1049 Hwy 82, High Rolls Mountain Park, NM 88325**

The hostel is on Highway 82 between Alamogordo and Cloudcroft — in High Rolls Mountain Park, not central Cloudcroft. Trails start right outside the front door.

**Nearby:**

- Lincoln National Forest trailheads — immediate vicinity
- Cloudcroft village center — short drive up the mountain
- Cloudcroft Ski Area — nearby
- Trestle Recreation Area — nearby
- Sunspot Solar Observatory — ~20 miles
- White Sands National Park — ~50 miles west

You'll need a car to reach Cloudcroft's restaurants and shops. The tradeoff is a quieter, more outdoors-forward setting.

## What Guests Say

Reviews on Booking.com, TripAdvisor, Hostelworld, and Google trend positive.

**Common positives:** Cleanliness consistently praised, kitchen and common areas are well-valued, hosts and check-in experience described positively, group and family stays work well, mountain atmosphere is a recurring strength.

**Common concerns:** Shared bathrooms and hostel norms are part of the deal. Privacy is lower than an inn or motel. Location requires driving to central Cloudcroft. Comfort expectations need to match the hostel model.

The overall pattern: guests who understand what a hostel is like it a lot. Guests expecting a traditional hotel may be mismatched from the start.
