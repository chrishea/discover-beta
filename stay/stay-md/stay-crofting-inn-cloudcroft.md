# The Crofting Bed & Breakfast

**A century-old historic home turned B&B, two blocks from Burro Avenue, with homemade breakfast and individually decorated rooms.**

---

## The Short Version

The Crofting is a true bed and breakfast — not a hotel that happens to serve breakfast. The house dates to 1909, making it one of the oldest structures in Cloudcroft. It sits on Swallow Place, a quiet side street just a short walk from the village's main drag, surrounded by spruce trees.

The inn has eight individually furnished rooms with private balconies, vintage decor, and the kind of character you don't find in new construction. Mornings include a full home-cooked breakfast served at 8:30 a.m. The hosts draw consistent praise for their warmth and local knowledge.

This is a stay built around charm, breakfast, quiet, and host interaction — not luxury amenities, late-arrival convenience, or hotel anonymity. If you want a small B&B with a personal feel and a real breakfast, this is one of the strongest options in Cloudcroft. If you want a front desk, flexible check-in, or resort amenities, look elsewhere.

**Not available:**
- Kitchenettes
- Daily housekeeping
- Rollaway/extra beds or cribs
- Front desk
- After-hours check-in

**A note on breakfast:** This is one of the strongest reasons to book here. The official site and reviews both emphasize hearty, home-cooked breakfasts served by the hosts. There's no published menu — it changes daily. If you have dietary needs, ask when you book.

## What Guests Say

**Expedia score: 9.2 / 10** (678 reviews)

**Common positives:** Warm and knowledgeable hosts, good full breakfast, charming antique decor, mountain views from balcony rooms, quiet setting, cleanliness consistently praised.

**Common concerns:** Rooms are small. Wood floors can carry noise between rooms. Decor is heavy vintage — may not suit minimalist tastes. Breakfast is at a fixed time (8:30 a.m.), which doesn't work for early departures. The stay is more B&B than hotel, which means less flexibility.

The overall pattern is favorable if you want a hosted B&B experience. The same pattern may frustrate travelers who want hotel-style convenience.
