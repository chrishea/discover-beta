# The Grand Cloudcroft Hotel (2026)

_Last updated: April 3, 2026_

---

## Overview

The Grand Cloudcroft Hotel is a 50-room independent hotel on James Canyon Highway in Cloudcroft, New Mexico. It opened on September 15, 2023, making it the newest lodging property in the village and the first purpose-built hotel in Cloudcroft in recent memory. The property was designed, built, and managed locally, and it positions itself as an affordable alternative to The Lodge with a modern feel that still reflects the mountain character of Cloudcroft.

Co-owner Jim Maynard handled much of the custom woodwork throughout the property, using white fir — a nod to Cloudcroft's logging heritage. General Manager Julie Bragg, a native Cloudcrofter and former ICU nurse, joined the project in January 2023 and assembled the initial 15-person staff. Bragg described the hotel's positioning as: "We are affordable but with the feel of a luxury hotel. It feels modern while evoking a sense of Cloudcroft and its place in the mountains."

The hotel was built to compete directly with The Lodge as a lodging anchor in the village. Its appeal is more about convenience, consistency, and modern comfort than about history or fine dining. For travelers who want a recognizable hotel format without paying for historic ambiance, The Grand is the clearest option in Cloudcroft.

## Amenities

### Breakfast
Hot breakfast included five mornings per week. Muffins and cereal available on the other two mornings. Complimentary for all guests.

### Indoor Pool & Hot Tub
The hotel has an indoor year-round swimming pool and hot tub. The pool was under construction at opening in September 2023 and has since been completed.

**Public swim:** Every Sunday, the pool is open to the public in two sessions — 12:00–2:00 p.m. and 2:00–4:00 p.m. Fees are $10 per person and $5 for children under 5. Ages 14 and under require adult supervision.

### Parking
- Underground parking for 20 cars and 10 motorcycles
- Ample surface parking available
- Radiant in-laid heating in front lot concrete to prevent snow and ice accumulation
- Free for all guests

### Outdoor Space
- Second-floor Aspen deck overlooking James Canyon Highway
- Hot rock fire pit on the deck

### Winter Features
- Roof and eaves equipped with ice-melting systems
- Heated front lot concrete
- Underground parking for winter protection

### Other
- 24-hour front desk
- 24-hour snack area (cereals, fresh fruits, yogurt, juice)
- Elevator access
- Free Wi-Fi throughout the property
- Local art displayed throughout with QR codes linking to artist portfolios

## Guest Fit

**Best for:**
- Travelers who want a standard hotel setup with modern amenities
- Families needing king beds and connecting rooms
- Guests who want something more straightforward than a historic resort stay
- Business travelers needing a work desk and Wi-Fi
- Travelers with ADA accessibility needs
- Pet owners (limited pet-friendly rooms)

**Less ideal for:**
- Travelers seeking historic character or romantic atmosphere (that's The Lodge)
- Guests who want on-site fine dining (no restaurant)
- Visitors looking for cabin-style privacy

## What Stands Out

- Newest hotel in Cloudcroft — modern construction, not a retrofit
- Every room has a king bed — no queen or double rooms
- Custom white fir woodwork by co-owner Jim Maynard reflecting Cloudcroft logging heritage
- Indoor year-round pool and hot tub (with public swim Sundays)
- Underground heated parking — serious winter infrastructure
- Locally designed, built, and managed — not a chain
- Affordable pricing relative to The Lodge
- 24-hour front desk and snack area

---

## Drawbacks & Caveats

- No on-site restaurant or bar. Guests need to go out for meals beyond the included breakfast.
- Does not offer the historic character or destination atmosphere of The Lodge. The appeal is convenience, not romance.
- Only 2 pet-friendly rooms — book early if bringing a dog.
- Only 2 ADA-accessible rooms.
- Located on James Canyon Highway, not in the walkable Burro Avenue core. A short drive or longer walk to downtown shops and restaurants.

## Editor's Take

The Grand is the best choice in Cloudcroft for travelers who want a simple, comfortable hotel stay without paying for history or ambiance. It is the closest thing to a conventional hotel in a market dominated by cabins, B&Bs, and historic properties. The included breakfast, indoor pool, and underground heated parking are practical advantages that matter, especially in winter. It is not the most memorable stay in town — The Lodge and the cabin properties win on character — but it is the most frictionless.

From the lodging guide: "Best for a practical overnight," "best for convenience over charm," and "best for travelers bringing a dog" (some pet rooms clearly listed).
