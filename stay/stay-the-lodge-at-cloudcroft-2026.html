About

A Century of Mountain Hospitality

The Lodge at Cloudcroft has welcomed guests since 1899, when it was built as a retreat for Southern Pacific Railroad passengers escaping the summer heat of the desert. Perched at 9,000 feet in the Sacramento Mountains, it remains one of the most storied and beloved resorts in the American Southwest.

Today the property features elegantly appointed rooms and suites, the celebrated Rebecca's Restaurant, a full-service spa, and one of the highest-elevation golf courses in the United States. Whether you come for a romantic weekend or a family mountain escape, The Lodge delivers a genuinely timeless experience.

Why Stay

The Lodge Experience

What makes The Lodge at Cloudcroft the region's most celebrated mountain resort.

🍴

Rebecca's Restaurant

One of southern New Mexico's most romantic dining rooms. Rebecca's serves elevated New American cuisine in a candlelit Victorian setting — a destination in its own right.

🧨

Full-Service Spa

The Lodge Spa offers massages, facials, and body treatments using products suited to the high-altitude mountain climate. An ideal way to recover after a day on the trails.

⛳

High-Altitude Golf

The Lodge Golf Course sits at 9,000 feet — making it one of the highest-elevation courses in the US. The thin air adds distance to every drive, and the scenery is unforgettable.

🏛️

Historic Character

Built in 1899 for Southern Pacific Railroad passengers, The Lodge retains its original Victorian architecture and warm mountain character — over 125 years of history in every room.

🌌

Dark Sky Views

At 9,000 feet with minimal light pollution, the stargazing from The Lodge grounds is extraordinary. The Milky Way is often visible from the courtyard on clear nights.

🌿

Lincoln National Forest

Step directly from The Lodge into miles of hiking and mountain biking trails through Lincoln National Forest — aspen groves, canyon overlooks, and endless pine-scented singletrack.
