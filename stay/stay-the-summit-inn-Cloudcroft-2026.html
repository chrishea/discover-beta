About

A Practical Mountain Base

The Summit Inn sits at 9,000 feet on Chipmunk Avenue, a short walk from Cloudcroft’s main street, restaurants, and shops. It’s a practical, affordable base for exploring the Sacramento Mountains — close to hiking trails in Lincoln National Forest, the Trestle Recreation Area, and Ski Cloudcroft.

This is not a resort. There’s no hot tub, no concierge, no pool. What you get is a clean room with a kitchenette, Wi-Fi, a fire pit out back, and owners who know every trail and restaurant in town. Timothy and Gail King run the property themselves and have been actively renovating since 2021, with a modern Southwest aesthetic replacing dated interiors.

If your priorities are walkability, price, and a small-inn feel, this is a strong pick. If you want on-site dining, resort amenities, or the atmosphere of The Lodge, look elsewhere.

What Works

Clean rooms, friendly and knowledgeable owners, great walkable location, full kitchenettes, and good value for Cloudcroft. Staff go out of their way to help with trail recommendations and road conditions.

💬

Common Cautions

Breakfast is basic and can underwhelm. Some rooms still show wear or dated elements. No bathtubs (showers only). Comfort depends on which room you get — the renovation hasn’t reached every room equally.

💡

The Pattern

Typical of a small independent inn midway through an upgrade: the bones are good, the owners care, and the stay improves as renovations continue. Ask for a recently updated room.
