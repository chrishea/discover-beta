# where-to-visit.md — fact-check review and enrichment notes

## Highest-impact corrections

### 1. Sunspot claim is contradicted by current official source
The draft says:
- “Sunspot Solar Observatory was permanently closed and demolished following a mercury incident.”
- “Do NOT plan this outing around visiting Sunspot Solar Observatory.”

Current official Sunspot visitor information says the **Sunspot Astronomy & Visitors Center is open daily 9 AM to 5 PM during peak season**, and the **Dunn Solar Telescope observation room is open on select days**. That directly contradicts the draft’s closure/demolition claim. Treat the closure claim as **contradicted** unless you can document a newer official closure notice than the current public Sunspot site.  
Sources: sunspot.solar visit page; Lincoln National Forest scenic drives page.

### 2. White Sands holiday closure is wrong
The draft says White Sands is open year-round except **December 25**.
The current NPS hours page says White Sands is open daily year-round except **Thanksgiving and Christmas Day**.  
Source: NPS hours page.

### 3. Trestle Recreation Area details need correction
The draft says:
- seasonal closure “typically April”
- water provided
- stage 1 fire restrictions in effect through Sept. 30, 2026

Current Forest Service page says:
- **Site closed for the winter season** and **re-opening in spring**
- **Trails to the overlook remain open from Village Spur Trail parking**
- **$5 day-use fee**
- **restrooms available**
- **potable water is not available**
I did not confirm a current 2026 Stage 1 order in this pass, so that line should be removed unless you confirm it from a current order page.  
Source: Forest Service Trestle Recreation Area page.

### 4. Osha Trail length and facilities are off
The draft says Osha is:
- **2.5 miles**
- has possible stream water mid-loop

Current Forest Service trail guide says:
- **2.1 miles**
- **no water sources or restrooms**
- trailhead is at **US 82 and Muchachita Street**
- hikers, pets, and pack animals only
- elevation around **8,600 feet**
Source: Forest Service Osha Trail guide PDF.

### 5. Sunspot Scenic Byway framing needs revision
The draft presents the byway as still scenic but built around a closed observatory. Current Forest Service scenic-drives page still describes the **Sunspot Scenic Byway (NM 6563)** as a **15-mile paved route from Cloudcroft to the Sunspot Solar Observatory**. That does not prove all facilities are fully operating every day, but it does undermine the draft’s permanent-closure framing.  
Sources: Forest Service scenic drives page; Sunspot visit page.

### 6. New Mexico Museum of Space History details should be tightened
Current official museum pages support:
- **3198 State Route 2001, Alamogordo**
- **Monday and Wednesday–Saturday 10 AM–5 PM**
- **Sunday noon–5 PM**
- **closed Tuesday**
- closed on **Thanksgiving, Christmas Day, New Year’s Day**, and official tour page also lists **Easter**
- current prices vary by age and package; adult “museum only” pricing was not fully visible in the snippet I reviewed, so do not hard-code an adult price without a direct check on the live page
Sources: museum home, getting here, tours, and prices pages.

### 7. Ski Cloudcroft tubing and rates need revision
The draft says tubing reservations are recommended.
Current Ski Cloudcroft tubing page says:
- **no reservations**
- tubing sold **first come, first served**
- sold in **2-hour blocks**
- **$36 per session**
- riders must be at least **42 inches** tall
Current rates page supports:
- 2025–2026 lift tickets, rentals, lessons, tubing prices
- top elevation **9,100 feet**, base **8,400 feet**, vertical **700 feet**
- **25 trails**
- **3 lifts**
Sources: Ski Cloudcroft tubing and rates pages.

### 8. White Sands facts mostly hold, but a few should be sharpened
Supported by current NPS pages:
- largest gypsum dunefield in the world
- about **275 square miles**
- park preserves **more than half**
- park closes for missile tests at times, and **US 70 also closes**
- current fees: **$25 vehicle / $20 motorcycle / $15 per person**
- pets allowed on leash no longer than 6 feet; not allowed in the visitor center
Sources: NPS nature, hours, fees, pets, and regulations pages.
